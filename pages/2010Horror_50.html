The film is a sequel to the 2008 film ''[[Death Bell]]''.{{cite web|url=http://10.asiae.co.kr/Articles/new_view.htm?sec=ent0&a_id=2010071916291477927|work=10Asia|title=Kim Su-ro says "Yoon Si-yoon is truly remarkable"|editor=Kim, Lynn|date=July 19, 2010|accessdate=October 15, 2010}} The story is unrelated to the previous film. ==Synopsis== This film is about a group of high school students and teachers who get locked in the school after the swimming instructor is murdered. In South Korea, the high school student and swimmer Jeong Tae-yeon ([[Yoon Seung-ah]]) is found dead in the pool, apparently a suicide. Two years later, teacher Park Eun-su ([[Hwang Jung-eum]]) joins the high school, where Tae-yeon's stepsister Lee Se-Hee ([[Park Ji-yeon]]) is haunted by nightmarish visions and is bullied by the student Eom Ji-yun ([[Choi Ah-jin]]). Eun-su finds it difficult to get respect in the classroom and is backed up by an older teacher, Cha ([[Kim Su-ro]]). Se-Hee and her classmates are selected for an elite "study camp" held at the school during the summer break where 30 students study for their university entrance exams. The school's swimming trainer is murdered in the showers, and the words "When an innocent mother is killed, what son would not avenge her death?" found scrawled on a blackboard. A voice warns the students that they'll all be killed unless they can answer who is the murderer and why. The students and teachers find they're locked in the school when more deaths begin to happen. ==Release== ''Death Bell 2: Bloody Camp'' premiered at the [[Puchon International Fantastic Film Festival]] on July 23, 2010 where it was the festivals closing film.{{cite web|url=http://www.filmbiz.asia/reviews/death-bell-2-bloody-camp|work=Film Business Asia|title=Death Bell 2: Bloody Camp (고死 두번째 이야기 : 교생실습)|author=Elley, Derek|author-link=Derek Elley|accessdate=October 15, 2010|date=July 28, 2010|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714072309/http://www.filmbiz.asia/reviews/death-bell-2-bloody-camp|archivedate=July 14, 2011}}{{cite web|url=http://www.filmbiz.asia/news/pifan-set-to-torture-vip-guests|work=Film Business Asia|title=PiFan set to torture VIP guests|author=Frater, Patrick|accessdate=October 15, 2010|date=June 16, 2010|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100622050944/http://www.filmbiz.asia/news/pifan-set-to-torture-vip-guests|archivedate=June 22, 2010|deadurl=yes|df=}} The film received wide release in South Korea on July 28, 2010. On wide-release, the film was very successful with over 50,000 people seeing it in Korea on its opening day. An official for the film said it was "four times what we expected".{{cite web|url=http://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201007290205481003|script-title=ko:포토엔 티아라 지연-효민, ‘은정 언니 모르게~빙글빙글’|language=Korean|author=|date=July 29, 2010|accessdate=October 15, 2010|work=Newsen.com}} While writing the script, it was suggested that the characters should solve their problems in a quiz show format like they did in the first film. Director Yoo Sun-dong was against this ideas as he felt it was too much of an imitation of the first film. Yoo was influenced by his own high school experiences, stating that "Authoritative teachers like Teacher Kang (played by Kim Byung-ok) and Teacher Cha (played by Kim Su-ro) or the competition and violence between the students were things that I saw and felt when I was in school. I tried to put such horrifying elements into the film." ==Reception== ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'' wrote that the film is an "alarmingly brainless and sloppily directed follow-up to ''Death Bell''" noting that the only "scene worthy of attention is when student Jang-kook is stranded on a corridor and repeatedly attacked by a motorbike outfitted with revolving blades. It has the Gothic, apocalyptic taste of ''[[Mad Max]]''."{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/death-bell-2-bloody-camp-29877|work=Hollywood Reporter|title=Death Bell 2: Bloody Camp -- Film Review|author=Lee, Maggie|accessdate=October 19, 2010|date=August 14, 2010}} ''[[JoongAng Daily]]'' wrote a negative review of the film, saying that "it should have taken more chances and offered audiences more than blood...the film won't do much for viewers who are die-hard slasher film fans."{{cite web|url=http://joongangdaily.joins.com/article/view.asp?aid=2923896 |title=School horror sequel offers few reasons to scream|author=So-ya, Seo|work=[[JoongAng Daily]]|date=July 30, 2010|accessdate=October 15, 2010}} Despite negative reviews, both ''The Hollywood Reporter'' and ''JoongAng Daily'' praised the scene involving a metal-spiked motorcycle that attacks a student.

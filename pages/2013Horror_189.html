Fantastic Film Festival}} | runtime = 84 minutes | country = United Kingdom | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Stalled''''' is a 2013 British zombie comedy film directed by Christian James. It stars Dan Palmer, who also wrote the screenplay, as a man confined to a bathroom stall after zombies attack. Produced by Richard Kerrigan, the film's cast also includes Mark Holden and Antonia Bernath. It has been screened at several film festivals and was scheduled to be released on home video in 2014. ==Premise== On [[Christmas Eve]], office maintenance worker W. C. (Dan Palmer) avoids an office party to clean the toilets. He soon sees two women arrive and make out but one of them is revealed to be a zombie and bites the other woman, infecting her as well. More zombies arrive and W.C. becomes trapped in the stall. A coworker of W.C. arrives and attempts to save him but is bitten on the neck and dies. In order to save W.C. fellow survivor Heather (Antonia Bernath), who was in the stall next to W.C., sacrifices herself by opening the doors and letting the zombies eat her. W.C. then goes to a phone booth and calls his mother, apologizing for everything he has done and tells her to lock her windows and doors and to not go outside. He promises her he will be home for Christmas, but he soon discovers the phone booth is surrounded by zombies and the credits roll, leaving W. C.'s fate unknown. ==Production== The film was directed by Christian James.{{cite web|title=Flickering Myth's Stalled screening to be recorded for the DVD|url=http://www.flickeringmyth.com/2013/11/flickering-myths-stalled-screening-to.html|work=Flickering Myth|accessdate=November 7, 2013}} Dan Palmer, who wrote the film's screenplay, starred as W. C. in the film,{{cite web|last=Gray|first=Scott A.|title=Stalled - Directed by Christian James - Toronto After Dark Reviews|url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/TorontoAfterDark/stalled-directed_by_christian_james|work=[[Exclaim!]]|accessdate=November 7, 2013}} with Mark Holden and [[Antonia Bernath]] co-starring. The cast also includes Giles Anderson, Sarah Biggins, and Victoria Bloom.{{cite web|title=Stalled|url=http://www.screendaily.com/reviews/the-latest/stalled/5059594.article|work=[[Screendaily]]|publisher=[[Screen International]]|accessdate=November 7, 2013}} The film's production company was Cubicle Hero. Sashi Kissoon did cinematography and Mark Gilleece edited the film. ==Reception== ''Stalled'' has received positive reviews. Kim Newman of [[Screendaily]] wrote that ''Stalled'' "is a worthy successor to Shaun Of The Dead and Harold's Going Stiff in its mix of homage to past zombie film glories and very British social comedy." Scott A. Grey of [[Exclaim!]] praised ''Stalled'' and considered it "a welcome addition to the limited pantheon of successful zombie comedies." A writer for [[Ain't It Cool News]] opined that "the zom-com has been tried before, but very few of these films are as successful at being entertaining as STALLED is."{{cite web|title=AICN horror at TADFF|url=http://www.aintitcool.com/node/64689#4|work=[[Ain't It Cool News]]|accessdate=November 7, 2013}} Edward Boff, writing for ''Film Juice'', gave ''Stalled'' four stars out of five, writing that "Just the right mix of thrills, gore, laughs and genuinely touching character moments, using the very limited location to the fullest."{{cite web|title=Stalled – FrightFest Review|url=http://www.filmjuice.com/stalled-frightfest-review/|work=FilmJuice|accessdate=November 7, 2013}} Todd Brown, writing for [[Twitch Film]], considered the film "proof that sometimes all you need to sell a movie is a good concept" and that it was "just pretty damn funny". The film has a score of 28% on Rotten tomatoes. ==Release== ''Stalled'' premiered at the [[London FrightFest Film Festival]] on 24 August 2013. Stalled also screened at the [[Lund International Fantastic Film Festival]], where it won the "Feature Film Méliès d'Argent" award.{{cite web|title=Lund International Fantastic Film Festival|url=http://www.melies.org/the-festivals/lund-international-fantastic-film-festival|work=melies.org|accessdate=November 7, 2013}} The film screened on 14 November 2013 at the [[Prince Charles Cinema]], where the filmmakers will attend and give away props from the film.

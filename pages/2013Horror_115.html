Scion Pictures | distributor = | released = {{Film date|2013|10|26|Austin Film Festival|2014|09|05|United States}} | runtime = 96 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = $289,613{{cite web|title=Innocence (2014)|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=innocence2014.htm|publisher=Box Office Mojo|accessdate=13 September 2014}} }} '''''Innocence''''' is a 2013 American [[horror film|horror]] [[drama film]] directed by Hilary Brougher, who co-wrote the film with [[Tristine Skyler]]. The movie is based on the 2000 [[Innocence (Mendelsohn novel)|novel of the same name]] by [[Jane Mendelsohn]]. It had its world premiere on 26 October, 2013 at the [[Austin Film Festival]] and received a limited theatrical release in the United States on 5 September, 2014. The movie stars Sophie Curtis, [[Kelly Reilly]], [[Graham Phillips (actor)|Graham Phillips]], [[Linus Roache]], [[Sarah Sutherland]] and [[Stephanie March]].{{cite web|last1=Goodykoontz|first1=Bill|title=Sophie Curtis talks about 'Innocence' and fear|url=http://www.azcentral.com/story/billgoodykoontz/2014/09/06/curtis-movie-innocence/15193447/|publisher=AZ Central|accessdate=12 September 2014}} ==Plot== Beckett (Sophie Curtis) is a young teenager mourning the loss of her mother. She's moved to the [[Upper West Side]] of [[Manhattan]] with her father Miles ([[Linus Roache]]) and is set to begin school at Hamilton, an exclusive [[University-preparatory school|prep school]]. Beckett is so engrossed in her grief that she fails to notice that her school is a little stranger than most schools, as its students are prone to suicides and is full of extraordinarily beautiful female teachers. Things grow worse when the school nurse Pamela ([[Kelly Reilly]]) decides to move in with Beckett and Miles, especially since Pamela keeps instructing Beckett to remain a virgin.

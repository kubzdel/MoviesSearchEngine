{{Infobox film | name = The Dead and the Damned | image = | alt = | caption = | director = Rene Perez | producer = Mattia Borrani | writer = {{plainlist| * Rene Perez * Barry Massoni }} | starring = {{plainlist| * David Lockhart * Camille Montgomery * Rick Mora * Robert Amstler }} | music = {{plainlist| * Mattia Borrani * Rene Perez }} | cinematography = Paul Nordin | editing = Rene Perez | studio = Mattia Borrani Productions | distributor = | released = {{Film date|2010|07|17}} | runtime = 85 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''The Dead and the Damned''''' (also '''''Cowboys & Zombies''''') is a 2010 American [[Western film|Western]] [[horror film]] directed by Rene Perez, written by Perez and Barry Massoni, and starring David Lockhart, Camille Montgomery, Rick Mora, and Robert Amstler. A meteorite unleashes a zombie virus in the American Old West. == Plot == During the [[California Gold Rush]], Mortimer receives a bounty for Brother Wolf, a Native American accused of rape. Mortimer recruits a prostitute, Rhiannon, as bait to lure out Wolf. However, Mortimer becomes dubious of the charges once he meets Wolf. Meanwhile, a group of prospectors unleash a zombie virus when they attempt to mine a meteorite. Mortimer, Rhiannon, and Wolf must band together to stop the zombies. == Cast == * David Lockhart as Mortimer * Camille Montgomery as Rhiannon * [[Rick Mora]] as Brother Wolf * Robert Amstler as The German == Production == Filming took place near [[Yosemite National Park]].{{cite book|title=The Zombie Movie Encyclopedia, Volume 2: 2000–2010|last=Dendle|first=Peter|authorlink=Peter Dendle|publisher=[[McFarland & Company]]|year=2012|page=62|isbn=978-0-7864-6163-9}} == Release == ''The Dead and the Damned'' premiered at the Another Hole In the Head film festival on July 17, 2010.{{cite news|url=http://www.mercurynews.com/ci_15402241?source=rss_emailed|title=Another Hole In the Head film fest returns to San Francisco|last=Harttington|first=Jim|work=[[San Jose Mercury News]]|date=2010-07-02|accessdate=2015-04-01}}{{cite web|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/news/20605/|title=The Undead Head West in 'The Dead and the Damned'|last=Miska|first=Brad|work=[[Bloody Disgusting]]|date=2010-06-16|accessdate=2015-04-01}} It was released on DVD in the US on July 26, 2011,{{cite web|url=https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/24751/artwork-and-more-details-on-the-us-dvd-release-of-the-dead-and-the-damned/|title=Artwork and More Details on the US DVD Release of The Dead and the Damned|last=Barton|first=Steve|work=[[Dread Central]]|date=2011-06-10|accessdate=2015-04-01}} and in the UK on August 1, 2011.{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/24659/cowboys-and-zombies-saddle-up-for-uk-dvd-release/|title=Cowboys and Zombies Saddle Up for UK DVD Release|last=Jones|first=Gareth|work=[[Dread Central]]|date=2011-06-07|accessdate=2015-04-01}} == Reception == Jim Harrington of the ''[[San Jose Mercury News]]'' called the plot "as ludicrous as it is fun".

[[Focus Films]] | distributor = [[Anchor Bay Entertainment]] (U.S.) | country = United Kingdom | released = {{Film date|2008|05|04|df=y}} | runtime = | language = English | budget = }} '''''Chemical Wedding''''' (released in the [[United States|U.S.]] as '''''Crowley'''''[http://fangoriaonline.com/home/news/16-dvd-a-blu-ray/883-assorted-horror-dvd-news.html Fangoria Online – Assorted Horror DVD News]) is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[Supernatural horror film|supernatural]] [[Science fiction film|science fiction]] [[Horror film|horror]] film produced by Bill&Ben Productions in conjunction with the London-based [[Focus Films]]. It is directed by [[Julian Doyle (filmmaker)|Julian Doyle]]. The story is based on an original screenplay by [[Bruce Dickinson]], frontman of [[heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band [[Iron Maiden]]. Dickinson released a solo album entitled ''[[The Chemical Wedding (Bruce Dickinson album)|The Chemical Wedding]]'' in 1998, which, despite sharing the title and title track from the film's soundtrack, is otherwise unrelated. ==Plot== Upon entering a [[Virtual reality|VR]] machine, Professor Oliver Haddo, a modern Cambridge scholar, becomes possessed by the spirit of the infamous occultist [[Aleister Crowley]], as the machine's program has been corrupted by a former follower of Crowley. Resurrected 50 years after his death, Crowley begins his occult practices anew, seeking a new "scarlet bride" whom he can marry in an occult ceremony which will increase his power. ==Cast== *[[Simon Callow]] as Professor Oliver Haddo/Aleister Crowley *[[Kal Weber]] as Dr. Joshua Mathers *[[Lucy Cudden]] as Lia Robinson *[[Jud Charlton]] as Victor Nuberg *[[Paul McDowell (actor)|Paul McDowell]] as Symonds *[[John Shrapnel]] as Aleister Crowley (original) *[[Terence Bayler]] as Professor Brent *[[Mike Shannon (actor)|Mike Shannon]] as Alex *[[Bruce Dickinson]] as Crowley's landlord and as a blind man ==Production== The film was originally proposed in 2000 and was to have been produced by [[Terry Jones]]' "Messiah Films",[http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/948331.stm BBC News, Friday, 29 September 2000, 12:45 GMT 13:45 UK] but was later adopted by [[Focus Films]]. David Pupkewitz and [[Malcolm Kohll]] produced the film, with Ben Timlett and Justin Peyton of Bill and Ben Productions and Duellist Film Production in association with MotionFX and E-Motion. Executive producers are [[Andy Taylor (music entrepreneur)|Andy Taylor]], Paul Astrom-Andrews and Peter Dale. Warner Music released the film's soundtrack in the UK, while Edward Noeltner's Cinema Management Group handled international sales.[http://www.ironmaiden.com/index.php?categoryid=8&p2_articleid=659 Iron Maiden: Official Website][http://www.moviecentre.net/upcomingmovies/preview/movie_id_2655.htm Moviecentre] The film received its world première at the [[Sci-Fi-London]] film festival on 4 May 2008.[http://www.sci-fi-london.com/festival/2008/programme/feature/chemical-wedding.php Chemical Wedding – SCI-FI-LONDON Film Festival 7 :: 30 April – 4 May 2008] According to Rockerparis, [[Iron Maiden]] lead singer [[Bruce Dickinson]] was in [[Paris, France]], on 26 November to promote the film's DVD release. The screening and press conference were held in a private cinema in front of [[Europe 1]] radio near the Champs Elysées.[http://www.ultimate-guitar.com/news/general_music_news/iron_maiden_singer_to_promote_chemical_wedding_dvd_release.html Iron Maiden Singer To Promote 'Chemical Wedding' DVD Release] Dickinson, who has a small cameo role in the film, has stated that, "On several levels, I think it will be nice for them ([Iron Maiden fans]) to see somebody from Maiden doing something else that gets the band's name out there and also potentially gets a bit of respect for heavy metal and all the rest of it....But, in addition, I think they'll just enjoy it.

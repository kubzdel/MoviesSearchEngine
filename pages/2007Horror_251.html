English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Zibahkhana''''' (English: ''Slaughterhouse'' (literal meaning), '''''Hell's Ground''''' (English title)) is a 2007 [[Urdu]]-English [[slasher film]] directed by Omar Khan. It premiered at the [[NatFilm Festival]] in Denmark and has been screened at festivals including Toronto, New York City, London, Neuchatel, Stockholm, Cape Town, Austin, Philadelphia, Cambridge, Puerto Rico, Sitges, Valencia, Oslo and Helsinki. {{cite news|last=Sandhu|first=Sukhdev|title=Zibahkhana: Beware zombies wearing saris|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/starsandstories/3667146/Zibahkhana-Beware-zombies-wearing-saris.html|accessdate=4 July 2012|newspaper=The Telegraph|date=11 August 2007}} The film passed censors in Pakistan (with nine seconds cut) and became the first non 35mm, HDV feature film released in Pakistan cinema history. It was shot in 30 days.{{Cite web|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117951886.html?categoryid=19&cs=1 |publisher=Variety |title=Zombies invade Pakistan}} ==Plot== {{no plot|date=May 2018}} ==Cast== * [[Kunwar Ali Roshan]] as Vicky * [[Rooshanie Ejaz]] as Ayesha * [[Rubya Chaudhry]] as Roxy * Haider Raza as Simon * [[Osman Khalid Butt]] as O.J. * [[Rehana (actress)|Rehan]] as Deewana ==Release== {{expand section|date=May 2018}} ===Home media=== The film was released on [[DVD]] by TLA Releasing on June 24, 2008. It was later released by Danger After Dark on August 4th that same year.{{cite web|title=Hell's Ground (2006) - Omar Khan|url=https://www.allmovie.com/movie/hells-ground-v405011/releases|website=Allmovie.com|publisher=AllMovie|accessdate=11 May 2018}} ==Critical reception== {{expand section|date=May 2018}} ''Zibahkhana'' received mostly positive reviews upon its release. [[Ain't It Cool News]] praised the film, calling it "one of the most badass flicks of the year". The reviewer also noted that although the film had its faults, mainly that it was stupid and predictable, "it's got something so incredibly captivating about it that it has its own place in the line of truly great indie horror flicks".{{cite web|title=AICN Intern Muldoon checks out the Pakistani Zombie Horror Film, HELL'S GROUND, at Fantastic Fest!!!|url=http://www.aintitcool.com/node/34153|website=Ain't It Cool News.com|publisher=headgeek|accessdate=11 May 2018}} [[Dread Central]] awarded the film a score of 4/5, calling it "a genuinely weird and somber horror movie that transposes familiar narrative themes from the genre into a cultural aesthetic few in the West will have had any prior glimpse of."{{cite web|last1=McCanibal|first1=Paul|title=Hell's Ground (2007) - Dread Central|url=https://www.dreadcentral.com/reviews/4791/hell-s-ground-2007/|website=Dread Central.com|publisher=Paul McCanibal|accessdate=11 May 2018}} [[Film Threat]] gave the film a positive review, commending its effective visuals, and strong nighttime atmosphere. The reviewer concluded by writing, "Overall, a measured mix of clichéd horror bits and wit to joke about them keeps ''Hell’s Ground'' even for a horror audience."{{cite web|title=HELL'S GROUND|url=http://filmthreat.com/uncategorized/hells-ground/|website=Film Threat.com|publisher=Film Threat|accessdate=11 May 2018}} === Awards === {{unsourced section|date=May 2018}} * Won "Jury's Award for Best Film of 2008" at the Riofan Film Festival, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.{{citation needed|date=May 2018}} * Won "Jury's Special Award for Best Gore 2007" at the Fantastic Film Festival, Austin, Texas.{{citation needed|date=May 2018}} * Won "Best Film" award at the Fantaspoa film festival 2009.

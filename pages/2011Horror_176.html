The article claimed that beneath its streets "lies another city remembered only in obscure Indian legends, an underground world built by a strange race that vanished five thousand years ago."{{cite web|last1=staff|title=The Underground City Of The Lizard People|url=http://www.weirdus.com/states/california/fabled_people_and_places/lizard_people/index.php|publisher=Weird US|accessdate=11 May 2015}}{{cite news|last1=Bosquet|first1=Jean|title=Lizard People's catacomb city haunted |url=http://www.reptoids.com/Vault/Schufeltsearch.htm|accessdate=11 May 2014|format=2000 reptoids archive|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=9 January 1934}}{{cite news|last1=Ballinger|first1=Matt|title=Throwback Thursday: Did Lizard People live under L.A. 5,000 years ago?|url=http://www.latimes.com/la-sh-lizard-people-throwback-thursday-20140123-story.html|accessdate=11 May 2015|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=23 January 2014}} ==Synopsis== Based on (alleged) actual eye witness accounts, a group of five New York film students wish to make a documentary. Led by director Jason Watts (Clint Byrne), camera-man Jack (Colin Walker, sound-girl Lynda (Lauren Klemp), 'executive producer' Cheryl (Courtney Hammond), and Jason’s girlfriend Rachael (Caitlin Gold) explore the "Banning Tunnels", long-abandoned railway tunnels under New York, full of [[graffiti]] and [[homelessness|homeless people]], to discover the truth behind a series of mysterious disappearances claimed to be due to the [[urban legend]] of a race of cannibalistic lizard people living beneath the streets. After a series of run-ins with some of the tunnel's human inhabitants, including the childlike Roy and the mysterious Chip ([[Jarded Cohn]]), the group soon finds the truth to the stories by discovering a forgotten civilization of half human, half lizards. Officer * Courtney Hammond as Cheryl * Hugo Dillon as Lizard #1 * Mike Baez as Lizard #3 * Ashley Blades as Spy * Pierre Bonny as Lizard #2 * Brittany Falcone as Lizard Child #2 * Chloe Falcone as Lizard Child #3 * Matt Baxter Luceno as Matt * Myles MacVane as War secretary * Christian Musto as Lizard Child #1 * Danielle Richard as Lizard Queen #1 * Leajato Amara Robinson as Agent Hurley * Nicole Rosenberg as Emma * Craig Scribner as Corporal Donnelly * Ray Soltani as Tunnel Worker * Mike Struck as Agent M. * Ren Valentine as Lizard Queen #2 {{div col end}} ==Reception== [[MJ Simpson]] gave the film a "B+" and offered that even with the film giving an impression of being a student project, it was worth watching. He complemented the directing and acting, and praised the film as being "atmospheric, creepy and slickly professional". While the concept of delving into dark tunnels plot was not particularly original, the film offered "generally sympathetic, rounded, believable characters, even if what they’re up to is a bit dumb." He found the film protagonist's lack of preparation unbelievable, as none seemed ready for subterranean urban exploration. He pointed out the ridiculousness of character Rachael "wearing pumps and a short, strapless dress", and how the character of Linda was shocked that the city's abandoned underground tunnels might contain rats, and that titular leader Jason had not briefed his film team beforehand and waited until the group was underground that he spoke toward legends of lizard people. Even with such flaws, Simpson wrote the "dialogue is good and the character conflict well-handled." He wrote that while the film {{sic}} "not ''[[The Descent]]'', I’ll give you that, but it’s a lot, lot better than ''The Blair Shit Project'', for example."{{cite news|last1=Simpson|first1=MJ|title=(review) Underground Lizard People|url=http://mjsimpson-films.blogspot.com/2014/03/underground-lizard-people.html|accessdate=11 May 2015|publisher=[[MJ Simpson]]|date=21 March 2014}} ''[[Dread Central]]'' revealed that the director co-wrote the film's screenplay with Ed Erdelac, and that the film alleges to be a true story of a modern exploration of creatures living below the streets of Los Angeles, concluding "''Underground Lizard People'' looks like a feature-length episode of [[Animal Planet]]’s ''[[Lost Tapes]]''".{{cite news|last1=Barton|first1=Steve|title=Trailer Exposes Underground Lizard People to the World|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/21571/trailer-exposes-underground-lizard-people-to-the-world/|accessdate=11 May 2015|publisher=[[Dread Central]]|date=9 January 2011}} [[io9]] wrote of ''Underground Lizard People'', "Any movie with a title like that is OK by me.

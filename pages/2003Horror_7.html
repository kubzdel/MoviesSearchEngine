[[TheForce.Net]] | released = {{Film date|2003|7|19}} | runtime = 8 minutes | language = English | budget = }} '''''Batman: Dead End''''' is a [[fan film]] written and directed by [[Sandy Collora]] that premiered July 19, 2003 at the [[San Diego Comic-Con International|San Diego Comic-Con]], and on the [[internet]] shortly thereafter. The film crosses over the [[DC Comics]] superhero ''[[Batman]]'' with the [[Alien (film series)|''Alien'']] and [[Predator (film series)|''Predator'']] science fiction film franchises. ==Plot== [[Batman]] corners an escaped [[Joker (comics)|Joker]], only to have the villain suddenly dragged off by an [[Alien (creature in Alien franchise)|Alien]]. The Alien is, in turn, killed by a [[Predator (alien)|Predator]], which Batman fights and defeats. Other Predators then appear just as more Aliens emerge from the darkness behind Batman. The film ends abruptly with Batman caught in between the two alien species. ==Cast== * Clark Bartram as Bruce Wayne/[[Batman]] * [[Andrew Koenig]] as [[Joker (comics)|The Joker]] * Kurt Carley as the [[Predator (alien)|Predator]] * Jake McKinnon as the [[Alien (creature in Alien franchise)|Alien]] * Patrick Oronet, Patrick Magee and Kurt Carley as other Predators ==Production== The film was made for a reported $30,000 and filmed in parts of [[North Hollywood, Los Angeles|North Hollywood]], [[California]], as a stand-in for [[Gotham City]].{{cite news|first=James|last=Pethokoukis|url=https://www.usnews.com/usnews/tech/nextnews/archive/next030807.htm|title=Finally, a good Batman sequel|work=[[US News & World Report]]|publisher=US News & World Report, LP|location=Washington DC|date=August 8, 2003|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090522080609/http://www.usnews.com/usnews/tech/nextnews/archive/next030807.htm|archivedate=May 22, 2009}} Collora filmed a similar project, 2004's ''[[World's Finest (fan film)|World's Finest]]'', with much of the same cast and crew. ==Reception== Film director and comic book writer [[Kevin Smith]] called it "possibly the truest, best Batman movie ever made",{{cite web|author=JoBlo (pseudonym)|url=http://www.joblo.com/index.php?id=3252 |title=BATMAN: DEAD END |publisher=Joblo.com |date=2004-01-02 |accessdate=2011-01-01}} and comic book artist [[Alex Ross]] praised it as "Batman the way I've always wanted to see him".{{cite web |url=http://www.filmthreat.com/index.php?section=reviews&Id=4789 |title=BATMAN: DEAD END |publisher=Filmthreat.com |date= |accessdate=2011-01-01 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090113153932/http://www.filmthreat.com/index.php?section=reviews&Id=4789 |archivedate=2009-01-13 |df= }} Collora has stated in interviews that the film was made as a demonstration reel to attract attention to his directing skills, and as such, succeeded in its goal.

[[Christopher Fairbank]] | music = Russell Currie | cinematography = John Pardue | editing = [[Richard Milward]] | studio = [[Millennium Pictures]] | distributor = High Point Film and Television | released = {{Film date|2001|9|14|df=yes|TIFF|2002|09|27|United Kingdom}} | runtime = 95 minutes | country = [[United Kingdom]] | language = [[English language|English]] | budget = }} '''''The Bunker''''' is a 2001 horror film directed by [[Rob Green (film director)|Rob Green]], written by [[Clive Dawson]] and starring [[Jason Flemyng]]. ==Plot== {{plot|date=March 2015}} In late 1944, the remnants of a platoon of German [[Panzergrenadier]]s from the [[Gro√üdeutschland Division]] are caught in an ambush by American troops. In their retreat, during which Pvt. Hugo Engels is killed in action, they find a German bunker manned by elderly Pvt. Mirus ([[John Carlisle (actor)|John Carlisle]]) and young Pvt. Neumann ([[Andrew-Lee Potts]]) who had been left to defend the bunker under orders from Area Command to wait until relieved. Cpl. Schenke confronts Sgt. Heydrich about why the group didn't counter-attack the Americans, claiming that there couldn't have been that many of them. Pvt. Mirus expands on the history of the area, revealing that victims of the Black Plague were massacred on the orders of a stranger, who influenced the townspeople to turn against one another. Low on ammunition, the men are haunted by bad dreams, memories and the feeling that something about the area just isn't right. As the night proceeds, strange tunnels are found beneath the bunker and the situation in the bunker becomes increasingly tense. Cpl. Schenke ([[Andrew Tiernan]]) wants to explore the tunnels but Lt. Krupp ([[Simon Kunz]]) orders against it. Later, Pvt. Mirus sneaks down into the tunnels and a curious Pfc. Kreuzmann ([[Eddie Marsan]]) follows him. When they are discovered to be missing, Lt. Krupp believes they've deserted and orders a pursuit. Interrogation of Pvt. Neumann reveals that Pvt. Mirus had been wandering off into the tunnels for several weeks and treats the tunnels as his property, as if something is down there that Mirus doesn't want him to know about. Cpl. Baumann ([[Jason Flemyng]]) discovers a set of plans for the complex while Cpl. Ebert ([[Jack Davenport]]) finds a danger sign. Exploring further, Cpl. Ebert comes to a mine shaft where he hears movement and a pile of sand nearly falls on top of him. Terrified, he is suddenly stabbed from behind. As he lays dying, a silhouetted figure resembling Pfc. Kreuzmann walks up. Cpl. Baumann hears his scream and packs up the papers to investigate when his torch dies. He lights a match and, seeing figures walking towards him, aims his weapon, but they turn out to be his comrades. When Lt. Krupp tries the torch, it turns on easily. Cpl. Baumann thinks that the Americans are in the tunnels. The group then hears movement, which turns out to be Pvt. Mirus. The soldiers turn on a rusty old generator, causing lights to turn on down in the tunnels. Lt. Krupp asks Pvt. Mirus what's going on but Pvt. Mirus is evasive. Before the questioning can go any further, the men hear machine gun fire coming from the bunker. When the men arrive, it appears that Pvt. Neumann, Pfc. Franke and Cpl Schenke had been firing at nothing. Lt. Krupp attempts to call headquarters, but the phone lines are dead. Convinced that the Americans have entered the tunnels, a game of cat and mouse develops. Stalked by shadowy figures, it becomes increasingly unclear where the men's fantasies end and the danger begins. Lt. Krupp agrees to send an expedition to secure the back entrance to the tunnels but on condition that if too much resistance is encountered they return. Pvt. Mirus and Pvt. Neumann are left behind in the bunker, while the remaining men split up: Lt. Krupp and Cpl. Schenke in one group and Cpl. Baumann, Pfc. Franke and Pfc. Neumann in the other. Before leaving, Cpl. Schenke gives a Nazi salute, which none of the men in the other group return. Pvt. Neumann stands guard while Pvt. Mirus scorns the whole idea of trying to fight anymore. Pvt. Mirus then claims that his son Werner, who died in Normandy, still talks to him and told him something about the tunnels. Down in the tunnels, Cpl. Schenke and Lt. Krupp force their way into an ammunition storage room only to find nothing but shells. Arriving at the rear entrance, they find it is sealed but Cpl. Schenke hypothesises that there's another way in. They continue searching and discover Cpl. Ebert's body. The other group comes across the mine shaft and discover a mass grave. Following a voice, they find Pfc. Kreuzmann, catatonic and repeatedly screaming "no". Pfc. Kreuzmann begins babbling about someone being back. They hear a women's voice and try to get Pfc. Kreuzmann out but Pfc Kreuzmann resists them. He breaks away, knocking Cpl. Baumann out cold. He then hallucinates a soldier with his throat ripped out while two others take pictures and runs off. Lt. Krupp and Cpl. Schenke explore a corridor when the lights begin to flicker. They hear movement and look, only to spin around a second later when they hear movement behind them and see a lamp swinging from side to side. They slowly back down the corridor to the back door of the tunnel complex. They hear loud banging as something tries to break into the tunnels. Pfc. Kreutzmann comes rushing up the tunnel, screaming. Startled, Cpl. Schenke and Lt. Krupp open fire, killing him. The gunfire causes a collapse, burying Lt. Krupp under a pile of wooden beams and killing him. When Schenke realises it was Pfc. Kreutzmann that they killed, he begins laughing hysterically. Back in the bunker, Pvt. Neumann hears the gunfire and ventures down into the tunnels despite Mirus' protests. Alone, Pvt. Mirus sees a shadowy figure and takes off in panic. As he flees, he hears pounding on each consecutive door he closes behind him, as if he is being pursued. Pvt. Mirus leaves the bunker and falls outside into the barbed wire. He sees another dark figure approaching and in his panic to escape becomes more and more tangled in the barbed wire. Meanwhile, Pvt. Neumann, who is searching through the tunnels, is caught by an increasingly paranoid Cpl. Schenke, who puts a knife to his neck. Pvt. Neumann regains his trust and the two return to the artillery ammunition room and booby trap the room to prevent the shells from falling into the hands of the Americans, whom Cpl. Schenke is sure are stalking them through the tunnels. Sgt. Heydrich, Cpl. Baumann and Pfc. Franke decide to blow the bunker door with a stick grenade and fight their way out. Cpl. Schenke and Pvt. Neumann hear the explosion and think that the Americans have set off a booby trap. Cpl. Schenke lights a fuse to destroy the artillery ammo and the two of them move out. Sgt. Heydrich, Cpl. Baumann and Pfc. Franke's plan to blast the door open fails so they go looking for another entrance. They run into Cpl. Schenke and Pvt. Neumann, who thinking that the three of them are Americans, open fire. A short firefight ensues in which Sgt. Heydrich, Pfc. Franke and Cpl. Baumann use up their remaining STG-44 ammo they withdraw. Pfc. Franke finally cracks and offers to surrender but it turns out that instead of Americans, Schenke is the one in the corridor. He looks rather deranged at this point. Pvt. Neumann comes up and aims his Kar98K rifle at Sgt Heydrich, Cpl. Baumann and Pfc. Franke as Cpl. Schenke announces his plan to execute the three of them. When Pvt. Neumann refuses to execute the three of them Cpl. Schenke pulls out his Luger and executes Pfc. Franke. He then puts the gun to Pvt. Neumann's head and threatens to kill him. Pvt. Neumann is about to pull the trigger when the explosives planted in the ammunition room detonate, knocking everybody down. Sgt. Heydrich, Cpl. Baumann and Pvt. Neumann make a run for it while Cpl. Schenke wildly fires his pistol after them. The three men race through the tunnels but the main exit is blocked by wreckage. Sgt. Heydrich pulls Pvt. Neumann into the mine shaft to get away, but Cpl. Baumann who is holding the rifle, stays behind and shoots at the helmeted figure approaching. To his amazement and terror the bullets seem to do nothing. He then begins hallucinating and fires a third shot before Sgt. Heydrich pulls him away. Soon Cpl. Schenke arrives, but the others have already erected a barricade over the mine shaft entrance. The men stumble across the mass grave again and then Cpl. Schenke appears, destroying the barricade. Sgt. Heydrich shoots Cpl. Schenke in the chest with a flare gun and Cpl. Schenke suddenly bursts into flames, screaming and reeling away. The three men begin whacking at the mass grave wall with entrenching tools, which break ground. Pvt. Neumann breaks out and Sgt. Heydrich is about to follow him when he's stabbed in the back by Cpl. Schenke, who seems to have put his flames out, despite being badly burned. Cpl. Baumann launches himself at Cpl. Schenke and the two begin a fight, which seems even for a while, until Cpl. Schenke throws Cpl. Baumann back. Sgt. Heydrich intervenes, only to get stabbed to death by Cpl. Schenke. The distraction gives Cpl. Baumann time to get away. Cpl. Baumann breaks through a layer of soil made up of bones, which seems to try and suck him back into the earth, but is pulled out by Pvt. Neumann. Cpl. Baumann then rolls over and hurls a grenade down into the hole, which lands next to the gravely injured Cpl. Schenke. Several seconds before it explodes, Cpl. Schenke sees several silhouetted figures standing nearby. The movie then cuts to Pvt. Neumann and Cpl. Baumann running away as the grenade explodes behind them, presumably killing Cpl. Schenke. Pvt. Neumann then checks out the bunker from the rear, and comes back to tell the wounded Cpl. Baumann that it's safe. Cpl. Baumann gives Pvt. Neumann a handkerchief and tells him to go surrender. Pvt. Neumann wants to stay but Baumann refuses, telling Neumann to leave. Neumann agrees to leave and on his way down the road finds Pvt. Mirus' body tangled up in the barbed wire. He then sees American soldiers emerging from the woods and waves his handkerchief to get their attention. The film then cuts back to Cpl. Baumann's memories which show the squad advancing across a sunny field only to find a bunch of deserters about to be executed. An officer hustles over Cpl. Baumann to participate in the execution. One of the deserters is seen praying and Cpl. Baumann misses his first shot before shooting the man twice. Quickly the firing squad moves in, with one soldier taking pictures with his camera and an officer finishing off the wounded with a pistol. A member of the firing squad is seen striking a pose while the picture-taking soldier snaps another shot. Cpl.

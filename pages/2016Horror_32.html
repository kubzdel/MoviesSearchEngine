A [[remake]] of Roth's [[Cabin Fever (2002 film)|2002 film of the same name]], the film stars Samuel Davis, [[Gage Golightly]], [[Matthew Daddario]], Nadine Crocker, and Dustin Ingram. The film was released on February 12, 2016 by [[IFC Midnight]]. [[Eli Roth]], writer and director of the original film, acts as co-writer and executive producer. ==Premise== A group of college students on a weekend getaway in a remote cabin are exposed to a [[Necrotizing fasciitis|flesh eating bacteria]], killing them one by one. ==Cast== {{Col-begin|width=60%}} {{Col-break|width=30%}} * Samuel Davis as Paul * [[Gage Golightly]] as Karen * [[Matthew Daddario]] as Jeff * Nadine Crocker as Marcy * Dustin Ingram as Bert * Randy Schulman as Henry {{Col-break|width=30%}} * George Griffith as Cadwell * [[Louise Linton]] as Deputy Winston * Timothy G. Zajaros as Connor / Grim * Aaron Trainor as Tommy * Derrick R. Means as Dennis * Teresa Decher as Anna {{Col-end}} ==Production== ===Development=== Initially a fourth film, entitled ''Outbreak'', was planned with the story taking place on a [[cruise ship]].{{cite web|last1=Wilson|first1=Samantha|title=‘CABIN FEVER’ REMAKE IS HAPPENING; AND YES, WE’RE SURE IT’S NOT ANOTHER DVD SEQUEL|url=http://filmschoolrejects.com/news/cabin-fever-remake.php|website=Filmschoolrejects|accessdate=12 February 2016}} The film was supposed to be [[Back to back film production|filmed back-to-back]] with ''[[Cabin Fever: Patient Zero]]'', but these plans eventually fell through. and the idea of a remake was formed. ===Writing=== The film uses the same script as Roth's original, although director Travis Zariwny trimmed the page count from 134 to only 92.{{cite web|last1=COLLIS|first1=Clark|title=Cabin Fever remake director promises gore aplenty|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2016/02/09/cabin-fever-remake|website=Entertainment Weekly|accessdate=12 February 2016}} Zariwhy also discussed and incorporated aspects that Roth wanted to include in the original but wasn't able to. One change is that Deputy Winston, a male (portrayed by [[Giuseppe Andrews]]) in the first and [[Cabin Fever 2: Spring Fever|second]] films, is now played by a female (portrayed by [[Louise Linton]]). Zariwny claimed he made this choice because "There was no way for me to emulate Giuseppe's performance." ===Filming=== [[Principal photography]] took place in [[Portland, Oregon]] in February 2015.{{cite web|url=http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Cabin-Fever-Getting-Remade-Under-Bizarre-Circumstances-67807.html|work=Cinema Blend|title=Cabin Fever Is Getting Remade Under These Bizarre Circumstances|author=Eisenberg, Eric|accessdate=January 30, 2016}} ==Release== In September 2015, [[IFC Midnight]] acquired North American distribution rights to the film,{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2015/film/news/cabin-fever-reboot-2016-release-ifc-midnight-1201588204/|title=‘Cabin Fever’ Reboot Set for 2016 Release From IFC Midnight|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|first=Dave|last=McNary|date=September 8, 2015|accessdate=February 10, 2016}} The film was released in a [[limited release]] and through [[video on demand]] on February 12, 2016.{{cite web|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/news/3375493/cabin-fever-remake-infects-loved-ones-this-february/|title=‘Cabin Fever’ Remake Infects Loved Ones This February?!|publisher=[[Bloody-Disgusting.com]]|first=Brad|last=Miska|date=January 5, 2016|accessdate=February 10, 2016}} ===Critical reception=== ''Cabin Fever'' received a 0% approval rating on [[review aggregator]] website [[Rotten Tomatoes]], based on 28 reviews and has an average rating of 2.5/10.{{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/cabin_fever_2016/|title=Cabin Fever (2016)|publisher=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|accessdate=February 19, 2016}} On [[Metacritic]], the film holds a rating of 14 out of 100, based on 7 critics, indicating "overwhelming dislike".{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/cabin-fever-2016|title=Cabin Fever|publisher=[[Metacritic]]|accessdate=February 14, 2016}} Geoff Berkshire of ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' gave the film a negative review writing: "It’s little surprise that Roth himself is the exec producer of this nearly beat-for-beat redo. Who else would feel as much passion for the middling material? And who better to ensure the copy does nothing to improve on the original? The silver lining of a day-and-date limited theatrical and VOD release is that there’s no chance this repurposed dud duplicates the original’s commercial performance."{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2016/film/reviews/cabin-fever-review-remake-eli-roth-1201704334/|title=Film Review: ‘Cabin Fever’|work=Variety|first=Geoff|last=Berkshire|date=February 12, 2016|accessdate=February 12, 2016}} [[Glenn Kenny]] of ''[[The New York Times]]'' also gave the film a negative review, writing: "Who benefits from the existence of this film? Certainly not the largely bland ensemble of post-adolescent actors cast as the leads, who here can scarcely be called characters." {{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/02/12/movies/review-in-cabin-fever-the-flesh-eating-virus-strikes-again.html?partner=rss&emc=rss|title=Review: In ‘Cabin Fever,’ the Flesh-Eating Virus Strikes Again|publisher=nytimes.com|first=Glenn|last=Kenny|date=February 11, 2016|accessdate=April 26, 2018}} ==Home media== The film was released on [[DVD]] and [[Blu-ray Disc]] on July 5, 2016.

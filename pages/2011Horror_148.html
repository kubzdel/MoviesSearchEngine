The film screened at [[Bucheon International Fantastic Film Festival]] and the [[Foreign Correspondents' Club of Japan]] in 2011 and the Fantasia Film Festival in Montreal in 2012. ==Plot== When all the men in her small town turn into zombies and begin to attack the women, Japanese schoolgirl Sakura ([[Rino Higa|Higarino]]) finds her life turned upside down. The trauma of witnessing the mass murder of her female friends and family members pushes her toward madness. Having few skills and armed only with her English textbook and a ''[[kyūdō]]'' bow, she sets out to survive. Having visions of a red-headed boy named Billy (Max MacKenzie), a character from one of her books, drives her toward insanity and she begins to lose her grip on reality. She wanders about, narrowly escaping her own death, until she finds Aoi (Mai Tsujimoto), a woman who is both equipped and mentally ready to kill. But this woman is dangerous and, rather than support, offers violence and more punishment. The film was developed during the 2010 NAFF IT Project.{{cite web|title=2010 Project Title : School Girl Apocalypse|url=http://naff.bifan.com/eng/02_it/03_view_ifr.asp?pk_seq=36&sc_category=2010&actEvent=detail&page=1&|publisher=Network of Asian Fantastic Films|accessdate=July 7, 2012}} ==Screenings== Its world premiere was July 15, 2011, at the [[Bucheon International Fantastic Film Festival]] (BiFan) in July 2011, and it screened there again in July 2012.{{cite web|title=Schoolgirl Apocolypse|url=http://www.bifan.kr/eng/program/program_view.asp?pk_seq=706&sc_category_seq=207&sc_num=1&actEvent=view|publisher=[[Bucheon International Fantastic Film Festival]]|accessdate=July 7, 2012}} It also screened at the [[Foreign Correspondents' Club of Japan]] in August 2011.{{cite web|title=Sneak Preview Screening: SCHOOLGIRL APOCALYPSE (Seirafuku Mokushiroku)|url=http://www.fccj.or.jp/node/6791|publisher=[[Foreign Correspondents' Club of Japan]]|accessdate=July 7, 2012}} It had its German premiere on May 24, 2012, at the 13th [[List of film festivals in Europe#Germany|Japan Filmfest Hamburg]],{{cite web|language=German|title=Schoolgirl Apocolypse |url=http://www.jffh.de/2012/schoolgirl-apocalypse/ |publisher=Japan Filmfest Hamburg|accessdate=July 6, 2012}} and in its [[North America]]n premiere, the film screened at the 2012 [[Fantasia Film Festival]] in [[Montreal, Canada]].{{cite news|last=Barton|first=Steve|title=Fantasia 2012: Second Wave of Films Announced; Key Selections From This Year's AXIS Lineup|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/57016/fantasia-2012-second-wave-films-announced-key-selections-years-axis-lineup|accessdate=July 6, 2012|newspaper=[[Dread Central]]|date=July 6, 2012}} ==Reception== ''L'Écran Fantastique'' described ''Schoolgirl Apocalypse'' as an "alternative" zombie film. "A skillful and effective road movie at the intersection of ''[[28 Days Later]]'' and ''[[The Road]]''...a great surprise sure to delight alternative zombie movie fans."{{cite news|last=Lehmann |first=Olivier |format=photobucket archive |title=Schoolgirl Apocolypse|url=http://s1157.photobucket.com/albums/p594/MrDOBDOB/ |accessdate=July 6, 2012 |newspaper=L'Écran Fantastique|date=December 2011 |page=111}}{{unreliable source?|date=December 2013}} The film was also described as genre-changing by the [[Germany|German]] online film review magazine ''Manifest''. They stated that it is daring for an American film director to use exaggerated western clichés for a Japanese horror-genre film. They called the film "a liberating 'fuck you', a surprisingly serious and decelerated undead Armageddon." Sound design was favorably compared to ''[[Poltergeist II: The Other Side|Poltergist II]]'', but they faulted the director for his restrained gore. They also stated that while the project was filmed in rural areas near [[Mount Fuji]], the famous landmark did not appear in the film, which was an intentional choice by the director to avoid clichés. They concluded by stating that while the film had too little action and no blood, it was a good movie, and rated it 4 out of 5 stars.{{cite web|last=Karenovics|first=Alexander |language=German|title=SCHOOLGIRL APOCALYPSE (Japan 2011)|url=http://www.dasmanifest.com/04/japanfilmfesthamburg2012.php#5|date=May 28, 2012|work=Manifest - Das Filmmagazin|accessdate=July 6, 2012}} ''News-Gate'' wrote that an important criterion for a successful horror film would include "savage monsters and beautiful heroine," and that the film's plot of heroine and boy versus an epidemic was taken from a comic book by [[Tomohiro Koizumi]].{{cite news|script-title=ja:黒髪＆おでこフェチ必見！ 2012年期待の美少女ヒガリノって？|url=http://www.news-gate.jp/2012/0208/30/|accessdate=July 6, 2012|newspaper=News-Gate|date=February 8, 2012|language=Japanese}} ''Djuna'' noted that this was director John Cairns' first feature film, and developed in 2010 through support of the Network of Asian Fantastic Films (NAFF). They wrote that the film's beginning and title might lead viewers to feel the film was to be simply a "Japanese schoolgirls killing zombies" film, but it took off in an unexpected direction. They also noted that the film was a bit garbled, the political metaphors were too strong, and it did not contain as much action as might have been expected. Overall, the response was positive, and they rated the film 2 stars out of 3.{{cite news|language=Korean|title=세라복 묵시록 Sêrâ-fuku mokushiroku (2011)|url=http://djuna.cine21.com/xe/2585889|accessdate=July 7, 2012|newspaper=Djuna|date=July 21, 2011}} ==Notes== {{Reflist|group=note}} ==References== {{reflist}} ==External links== * {{official website|http://www.schoolgirlapocalypse.com/index.html}} * {{IMDb title|2360114}} * {{rottentomatoes|sera_fuku_mokushiroku}} : {{CC-notice|cc=bysa3|url=http://www.schoolgirlapocalypse.com/}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Schoolgirl Apocalypse}} [[Category:2011 films]] [[Category:2011 horror films]] [[Category:2010s independent films]] [[Category:2010s science fiction horror films]] [[Category:Dystopian films]] [[Category:Films set in the future]] [[Category:Japanese horror films]] [[Category:Japanese films]] [[Category:Japanese independent films]] [[Category:Japanese-language films]] [[Category:Post-apocalyptic films]] [[Category:Zombie films]]

The film was preceded by others based on the same plot, ''Bell Witch Haunting'' (2004), ''[[An American Haunting]]'' (2005) and ''[[Bell Witch: The Movie]]'' (2007).{{cite web | url =http://horrornews.net/77548/film-review-the-bell-witch-haunting-2013/|title=Film Review: The Bell Witch Haunting (2013)|author=Rob Getz|publisher=Horrornews.net| date=25 Sep 2013| accessdate =31 Jan 2016}} ==Plot== In Tennessee, the Robertson County Sheriff’s Department releases a footage found on the cell phones and video cameras of the dead Sawyer family. What was first thought to be a murder-suicide is now believed to be the return of a centuries-old supernatural sinister force. ==Reception== Rob Getz from Horrornews.net noted that "though utilizing the oddly tropical January climate in Tennessee... for a few pivotal witchy woodland shenanigans, much of the film takes place within the walls of the Sawyer home, owing a greater debt to the “[[Paranormal Activity (film series)|Paranormal Activity]]” franchise". Getz added that the film is "slapping elements of "[[The Exorcist (film)|The Exorcist]]", "[[Darkness Falls (2003 film)|Darkness Falls]]" and countless others into the wildly uneven casserole" and that "watching this Amtrak jump the rails is never short of interesting". ==References== {{reflist}} ==External links== *{{Rotten Tomatoes | id=the_bell_witch_haunting}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Bell Witch Haunting}} [[Category:Witchcraft in film]] [[Category:2013 horror films]] [[Category:American supernatural horror films]] {{2010s-horror-film-stub}}

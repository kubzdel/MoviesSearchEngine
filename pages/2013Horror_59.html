{{Infobox film | name = Dark Feed | image = File:DarkFeedfilmposter.jpg | image_size = | border = | alt = | caption = | film name = | director = Michael Rasmussen, Shawn Rasmussen | producer = | writer = Michael Rasmussen, Shawn Rasmussen | screenplay = | story = | based on = | narrator = | starring = Evalena Marie, Jason Beaubien, Michael Reed | music = | cinematography = Monday Sunnlinn | editing = Michael Rasmussen, Reuter (as Michael Reuter) | studio = Rasmussen Brothers Productions | distributor = Lionsgate Home Entertainment | released = {{Film date|2013|3|18}} | runtime = 86 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Dark Feed''''' is a 2013 horror film directed by Shawn and Michael Rasmussen. The movie was first released on March 18, 2013 and centers upon a group of filmmakers that decide to cut costs by filming in an abandoned psychiatric hospital, only to end up slowly turning insane. Filming took place in [[New England]] and utilized some of the buildings seen in ''[[Shutter Island (film)|Shutter Island]]''.{{cite web|title=Trailer, Poster Debut for Dark Feed from the Writers of The Ward|url=http://www.shocktillyoudrop.com/news/167331-trailer-poster-debut-for-dark-feed-from-the-writers-of-the-ward/|publisher=STYD|accessdate=18 December 2013}} The Rasmussens came up with the idea for ''Dark Feed'' while visiting the set of another film, ''Long Distance'', which was filming in an abandoned psychiatric hospital.{{cite web|title=Q&A: "DARK FEED" writer/director Shawn Rasmussen|url=http://www.fangoria.com/new/qa-dark-feed-writerdirector-shawn-rasmussen/|publisher=Fangoria|accessdate=18 December 2013}} ==Synopsis== The movie follows a film crew that decides to film a [[Low-budget film|low budget]] horror film in an abandoned psychiatric hospital. They're already exhausted and overworked, making them eager to finish the project and move on. Unfortunately their presence seems to bring new life to the hospital, and it starts to exert a strange influence over the cast and crew, causing them to act in increasingly bizarre ways.

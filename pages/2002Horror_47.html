West Paterson, New Jersey}} | runtime = 119 minutes | country = United States | budget = | gross = }} '''''I'll Bury You Tomorrow''''' is a 2002 low-budget [[Independent film|independent]] [[horror film]] written and directed by [[Alan Rowe Kelly]] produced by New Millennium Pictures, starring Zoë Daelman Chlanda, Bill Corry, and Katherine O'Sullivan.{{cite book|last=Watt|first=Mike|title=Fervid Filmmaking: 66 Cult Pictures of Vision, Verve and No Self-Restraint|year=2013|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0786493356|pages=37–38|url=https://books.google.com/?id=d60nZ2shxU4C&pg=PA37 }} The film premiered in [[Woodland Park, New Jersey|West Paterson, New Jersey]] on June 17, 2002 and was released in DVD format on January 31, 2006. ''I'll Bury You Tomorrow'' was filmed at Garrett Mountain Reservation in West Paterson, New Jersey. ==Plot== Dolores Finley (Zoë Daelman Chlanda) is a deranged young woman that appears suddenly and asks to work at Beech's Funeral Home, the local mortuary for the dying rural town of Port Oram. The Beechs find her work and Dolores begins her job, but soon shows that she is a [[necrophiliac]].

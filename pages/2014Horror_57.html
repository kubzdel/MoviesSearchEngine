{{Infobox film | name = Debug | image = | alt = | caption = | director = [[David Hewlett]] | producer = [[Steve Hoban]] | writer = David Hewlett | starring = {{plain list| * [[Jeananne Goossen]] * [[Adrian Holmes]] * [[Adam Butcher]] * [[Jason Momoa]] }} | music = Tim Williams | cinematography = Gavin Smith | editing = {{plain list| * Geoff Ashenhurst * Jorge Weisz }} | studio = Copperheart Entertainment | distributor = Signature Entertainment | released = {{Film date|2014|11|03|UK|df=y|ref1=}} | runtime = 82 minutes{{cite web|url=http://www.bbfc.co.uk/releases/debug-video|title=Debug|work=[[BBFC]]|accessdate=2014-09-28}} | country = Canada | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Debug''''' is a 2014 Canadian [[science fiction film|science fiction]] [[horror film]] written and directed by [[David Hewlett]]. It stars [[Jeananne Goossen]], [[Adrian Holmes]], [[Adam Butcher]], Kjartan Hewitt, Sidney Leeder, and [[Jadyn Wong]] as computer programmers who must deal with a hostile [[artificial intelligence]] on an interstellar spaceship. It was released on 3 November 2014 in the UK. == Synopsis == Six young computer programmers on [[work release]] are tasked to debug the artificial intelligence of an interstellar spaceship. Hewlett said that he wanted to make a film from the point of view of [[HAL 9000]], the antagonist of that story.{{cite web|url=http://www.blastr.com/2013-2-20/david-hewlett-my-secret-plans-stargate-atlantis-movie-more|title=David Hewlett: My secret plans for a Stargate Atlantis movie + more|last=Hewlett|first=David|work=[[Blastr]]|date=2013-02-20|accessdate=2014-09-28}} This idea was eventually rejected as too artistic, and the plot was retooled to be more similar to ''[[Final Destination]]''.{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/106025/exclusive-interview-with-david-hewlett-on-debug/|title=Exclusive Interview with David Hewlett on Debug|last=Wilson|first=Staci Layne|work=[[Dread Central]]|date=2015-06-09|accessdate=2015-07-07}} Hewlett said that he did not see HAL 9000 as evil, and he designed Iam to be defending his right to exist.{{cite news|url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/daily/screens/2015-06-09/dvdanger-debug/|title=DVDanger: Debug|last=Whittaker|first=Richard|work=[[The Austin Chronicle]]|date=2015-06-09}} Hewlett intentionally cast Momoa as the ship's AI [[Typecasting (acting)|against type]]. Momoa wanted to do a project different than his traditional roles, and Hewlett was impressed with Momoa's creativity and range; the two men had previously worked together on the sci-fi series ''[[Stargate Atlantis]]''.

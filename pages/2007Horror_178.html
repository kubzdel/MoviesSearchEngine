[[Ben Woolford]] | distributor= [[Yume Pictures]] (2008) (UK) | runtime=120 min. | language=English | movie_series_label= | movie_series= | music= | budget= £7,000,000 (estimated) }} '''''Puffball''''' is a 2007 supernatural [[drama film]] directed by [[Nicolas Roeg]]. The script was adapted from [[Fay Weldon|Fay Weldon's]] 1980 [[Puffball (novel)|novel of the same name]] by her son, Dan Weldon.{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2008/jul/20/drama2 |title= Puffball |last=French |first= Philip |date= 19 July 2008 |website=www.theguardian.com |publisher=The Guardian |access-date= 17 February 2017}} The film was partially funded through the [[UK Film Council]]'s New Cinema Fund. The film had its premiere at the 2007 [[Montreal Film Festival]]. The film was later released in Canada on 28 October 2007, and saw a limited release in the United States on 29 February 2008. ==Plot summary== Liffey ([[Kelly Reilly]]), an ambitious young architect, moves to an isolated and eerie Irish valley to build a modern piece of architecture. The ruined cottage and land that she will use are a gift from her fiancé Richard, and is the former home of Molly, the elderly matriarch of a farming family living on the other side of the woods. Molly’s daughter is Mabs ([[Miranda Richardson]]), herself mother of three girls and wife to farmer Tucker. Now in her forties, Mabs wants another baby, a boy to inherit the farm – or perhaps she just wants to be pregnant again. Mabs and Molly use [[black magic]] to give Mabs a baby boy. But Liffey becomes pregnant after sleeping with both Richard and Tucker, causing everybody to believe that her baby is Tucker's. Mabs seeks Molly's help again to kill Liffey's baby with magic, making it look like a miscarriage. Mabs's elder daughter helps Liffey fight the spell. In the end, Mabs is finally pregnant with her own child, a boy. ==Cast== * [[Kelly Reilly]] – Liffey * [[Miranda Richardson]] – Mabs Tucker * [[Rita Tushingham]] – Molly * [[Oscar Pearce]] – Richard * [[William Houston (actor)|William Houston]] – Tucker * [[Donald Sutherland]] – Lars ==Production== Although the novel upon which it was based was set in rural England, the film was filmed and set in the [[County Monaghan]], [[Republic of Ireland]]. In a 2008 interview, Roeg says of the location, "We shot in Ireland and without wishing to lean towards pretension or cliché there is a mysticism associated with the place... The atmosphere and the locations were very important and yes, as with my other films, it does become something of a character in its own right." ==Reception== ''[[The Guardian]]'' reviewer Philip French wrote of the film, "A curious mixture of [[Cold Comfort Farm]], [[Straw Dogs (1971 film)|Straw Dogs]] and [[Rosemary's Baby (film)|Rosemary's Baby]], Puffball is certainly watchable." [[Blogcritics]]'s Tony Dayoub was somewhat critical, writing "Puffball peters out at the end. There is a lot of build-up, but no payoff to the frightening imagery which the movie employs," but adding "There is enough to recommend a viewing of the film if you are a fan of Roeg's work."{{cite web |url=http://blogcritics.org/movie-review-puffball-the-devils-eyeball/ |title=Movie Review: Puffball – The Devil’s Eyeball |last=Dayoub |first=Tony |date= 5 March 2008 |website=blogcritics.org/ |access-date= 17 February 2017}} [[Not Coming to a Theater Near You]]'s Jason W was more negative, calling it, "a confused film which reminds us that Roeg’s best work will always remain his sexy masterpieces of the 1970s. Hints of Roeg’s brilliance are in short supply..."{{cite web |url=http://notcoming.com/reviews/puffball/ |title=Puffball |last=Woloski |first=Jason |date=1 October 2007 |website= notcoming.com |access-date= 17 February 2017}} The ''[[Toronto Star]]'s'' Geoff Pevere was also highly critical, writing that the film is "Hysterical when it's not merely ridiculous, and shot through with such signature Roegian distractions... Puffball might well be titled for the hypothetical drug one might need to take in order to take it seriously."{{cite web |url=https://www.thestar.com/news/2007/09/28/roegs_former_glory_stuck_in_the_mud.html |title=Roeg's former glory stuck in the mud |last= Pevere |first= Geoff |date=28 September 2007 |website=www.thestar.com |publisher=The Toronto Star |access-date= 17 February 2017}} In a 2008 interview, Roeg claimed that Fay Weldon had seen the film and approved of it, saying "It meant a lot to me that when Fay saw the film she liked it.

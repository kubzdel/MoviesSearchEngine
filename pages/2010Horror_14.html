''[[Box Office Mojo]]''. Retrieved 2012-06-04. }} '''''Bedevilled''''' ({{ko-hhrm|김복남 살인사건의 전말|金福南 殺人事件의 前末|Kim Bok-nam Salinsageonui Jeonmal}}; lit. "The Whole Story of the Kim Bok-nam Murder Case") is a 2010 South Korean [[psychological horror]] [[action film|action]] film starring [[Seo Young-hee]] and [[Ji Sung-won]]. The film premiered as an official selection of [[International Critics' Week]] at the [[2010 Cannes Film Festival]]. It is the feature directorial debut of [[Jang Cheol-soo]], who worked as an [[assistant director]] on the [[Kim Ki-duk]] films ''[[Samaritan Girl]]'' and ''[[Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter... and Spring]]''.{{cite web|last=Huh |first=Nam-woong |title=JANG Cheol-su, the director of Bedevilled |url=http://cinematoday.korea.com/2010/10/jang-cheol-su-the-director-of-bedevilled/ |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130127142606/http://cinematoday.korea.com/2010/10/jang-cheol-su-the-director-of-bedevilled/ |dead-url=yes |archive-date=27 January 2013 |accessdate=2012-11-19 |work=Korean Cinema Today |date=6 October 2010 }} The film was a runaway hit in Korea, with the box-office returns far exceeding its {{SK won|700 million}} ({{USD|636,363}}) budget.{{cite web|last=Ki|first=Sun-min|title=''Bedevilled'' snaps up two festival awards|url=http://koreajoongangdaily.joinsmsn.com/news/article/article.aspx?aid=2926576|archive-url=https://archive.is/20130126222947/http://koreajoongangdaily.joinsmsn.com/news/article/article.aspx?aid=2926576|dead-url=yes|archive-date=26 January 2013|accessdate=2012-11-19|work=[[Korea JoongAng Daily]]|date=1 October 2010}} ==Plot== Hae-won is a middle-rank officer working in a Seoul bank. A stern, tense, single woman, she is brought down by work-related stress and her hypercompetitive environment. Desperate for a change of pace, she takes up an offer from a long-forgotten friend to take a vacation in Mudo, a desolate Southern island where she spent childhood. Arriving at the island, she is warmly welcomed by Bok-nam, with whom she had a close friendship when both were in their teens, but whose constant letters she's since ignored. Life on the undeveloped, socially regressive island is hard, and Bok-nam is treated as little more than a slave by her abusive husband Man-jong, his brother, and the local old women. All of Bok-nam's love is reserved for her young daughter Yeon-hee, with whom she tries to escape from the small, claustrophobic island.

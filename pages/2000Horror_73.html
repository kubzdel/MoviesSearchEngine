Infliction Films (Canada) | released = October 12, 2000 | runtime = 92 min. | country = Canada | language = English | budget = $100,000 CAD }} '''''Subconscious Cruelty''''' is an independent [[horror film]] written and directed by [[Karim Hussain]] and produced by [[Mitch Davis (film producer)|Mitch Davis]]. It was filmed over a long period of time, from February 1994 to December 1999, and debuted at the [[Festival de Cine de Sitges]] in [[Sitges]], [[Spain]], on October 12, 2000. The film went on to screen at several other festivals, including the [[Stockholm International Film Festival]] and [[Amsterdam Fantastic Film Festival]] before being released on DVD on April 18, 2005. == Plot == {{more plot|date=June 2018}} The film is based on several nihilistic and metaphorical stories about life, death and everything between. ==Cast== *Sophie Lauzière – Woman On Slab *Brea Asher – Sister *Ivaylo Founev – Brother *Christopher Piggins – Businessman/Doppelganger *Eric Pettigrew – Martyr/Jesus Christ *Martine Viale – Attacker/Female Demon == History and development == {{rewrite section|date=June 2018}} Hussain began filming ''Subconscious Cruelty'' at the age of 19 after connecting with 22-year-old Davis, a producer whom Hussain knew to believe in the project. Hussain felt that the [[subculture]] of the mid-1990s, a time when [[heroin]] usage was prevalent and [[nihilism]] was gaining acceptance in the art world, would be accepting of his film. Influences for the film include works by [[Alexandro Jodorowsky]], [[Luis Buñuel]], [[Dušan Makavejev]], [[David Lynch]], [[David Cronenberg]] and others.{{cite web|url=http://www.inflictionfilms.com/html/cruelty/dir-com.htm |title=Subconscious Cruelty: Director's Statement |publisher=InflictionFilms.com |date= |accessdate=2010-09-14}} The filmmaking process was beset by various problems, including the disappearance of the film [[Negative (photography)|negative]] in a financial dispute, forcing Hussain to hand-cut the [[Transparency (photography)|positive]] of the film without knowing if he would ever re-gain the negative. Another setback occurred when Hussain was stopped at the [[Canada–United States border]] after a business trip to the [[United States of America|United States]]. Canadian [[customs]] officials inspected the film, and, appalled by its content, confiscated it as illegally [[obscenity|obscene]] material. As a result, the original stock had to be hidden for a long period of time.{{cite web|url=http://www.inflictionfilms.com/html/cruelty/p-histo.htm |title=Subconscious Cruelty: Production History |publisher=InflictionFilms.com |date= |accessdate=2010-09-14}} As the film toured various festivals, it gained a cult following that led to Davis and Hussain seeking out a DVD distributor.{{citation needed|date=June 2018}} The film was first produced on DVD in Japan in 2001, and later in several European countries in 2006.{{citation needed|date=June 2018}} == Critical reception == ''Subconscious Cruelty'' received mixed reviews by mostly non-mainstream critics. Since the film was not released in the US market, traditional critics of English-language films did not see or comment on it. Known establishments such as [[Fangoria]] considered it "a film suffused with images that incite thoughts, many of which would be considered unhealthy in any artistic medium",[http://www.fangoria.com/dvd_review.php?id=4489 ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071217154808/http://www.fangoria.com/dvd_review.php?id=4489 |date=December 17, 2007 }} while some other independent critics considered it "the most amateurish cinematic junk I have ever seen...".{{cite web|url=http://www.dvdbeaver.com/film/DVDReviews12/subconcious-cruelty.htm |title=Karim Hussain's – Subconscious Cruelty – Sazuma – Region 2 – PAL |publisher=Dvdbeaver.com |date=2000-10-12 |accessdate=2010-09-14}} == References == {{reflist}} == External links == *[http://www.inflictionfilms.com/html/cruelty/index.htm Infliction Films page] *[http://www.sazuma.com/showdcd.php3?dcd_id=SP004 Sazuma Trading DVD details] *[https://web.archive.org/web/20071217154808/http://www.fangoria.com/dvd_review.php?id=4489 Fangoria DVD review] *[http://www.dvdmaniacs.net/Reviews/Q-T/subconscious_cruelty.html DVD Maniacs review] *{{IMDb title|166370|Subconscious Cruelty}} *[https://web.archive.org/web/20101109005801/http://www.dvdverdict.com/reviews/subconsciouscruelty.php DVD Verdict Review] *[http://www.horschamp.qc.ca/new_offscreen/subconscious.html Karim Hussain / Mitch Davis Interview at Offscreen.com] [[Category:2000 films]] [[Category:2000 horror films]] [[Category:2000 horror films]] [[Category:Canadian films]] [[Category:Canadian avant-garde and experimental films]] [[Category:Canadian horror films]] [[Category:Independent films]]

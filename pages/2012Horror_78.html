The film stars [[Saheed Balogun]], [[Segun Arinze]], [[Sola Fosudo]] and [[Sola Sobowale]].{{cite web|url=http://www.vanguardngr.com/2012/04/tade-ogidan-plans-to-take-family-on-fire-to-the-people/|title=Tade Ogidan plans to take Family on Fire to the people|work=Vanguard News}} The film was premiered on November 4, 2011 at The Lighthouse Hall, [[Camberwell]] road, [[London]].{{cite web|url=http://www.vanguardngr.com/2011/11/tade-ogidan-sets-family-on-fire/|title=Tade Ogidan sets family on fire|work=Vanguard News}} Prominent actors and actress present includes; [[Kunle Afolayan]], [[Richard Mofe Damijo]], [[Ramsey Nouah]], [[Teju Babyface]], [[Saheed Balogun]], [[Segun Arinze]] and [[Bimbo Akintola]].{{cite web|url=http://www.bellanaija.com/2011/10/19/family-ties-drugs-drama-tade-ogidans-new-blockbuster-family-on-fire-premieres-in-london-this-november/|title=Family Ties, Drugs & Drama! Tade Ogidan’s New Blockbuster “Family on Fire” premieres in London this November|work=BellaNaija}} It was premiered in [[Lagos State]], southwestern Nigeria on April 2012. Notable actors in attendance were [[Femi Adebayo]], [[Desmond Elliot]], [[Yemi Shodimu]] and prominent Nigerians present were [[Molade Okoya-Thomas]], a renowned Nigerian [[Business magnate]], [[Abimbola Fashola]] and [[Oladipo Diya]], a retired Lt. General and former [[Chief of the Defence Staff (Nigeria)|Chief of the Defence Staff]].{{cite web|url=http://www.punchng.com/entertainment/arts-life/nollywood-comes-alive-as-family-on-fire-premieres/ |title=Nollywood comes alive as Family on Fire premieres |work=The Punch - Nigeria's Most Widely Read Newspaper |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120516235710/http://www.punchng.com/entertainment/arts-life/nollywood-comes-alive-as-family-on-fire-premieres/ |archivedate=2012-05-16 |df= }} ==Plot== A young man, Kunle ([[Saheed Balogun]]) commits a grievous act that leaves his family members in anguish. In [[Lagos]], Kunle hides cocaine in his mother's ([[Lanre Hassan]]) baggage before she pays a visit to his siblings, including the eldest brother Femi ([[Sola Fosudo]]) in [[London]]. His mother narrowly escapes from the British immigration officers. A rescheduled flight to the UK foils Kunle’s plans to retrieve the illegal drug. Before his arrival, Femi’s wife ([[Sola Sobowale]]) incidentally sees it while unpacking the food items stuffed along with the drug. A student in London, Moyo who is sponsored by Femi, gets remunerated by the drug deals after stealing it from where it is hidden, but the enraged drug barons, led by the Don ([[Segun Arinze]]) and his gang, unleash terror on Kunle’s family. Suspense fills the air when Femi or nobody makes any attempt to find Moyo at his school since he is assumed to be in danger. Contrary to expectations, he is on a celebration spree in London before his inevitable arrest. ==Accolades== The film received two nominations at the [[8th Africa Movie Academy Awards]] held on April 22, 2012 at the Expo Centre, Eko Hotel & Suites in [[Lagos, Nigeria]].{{cite web|url=http://www.ama-awards.com/awards/2012|title=AMAA Nominees and Winners 2012 - Africa Movie Academy Awards|work=ama-awards.com|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140214081938/http://ama-awards.com/awards/2012|archivedate=2014-02-14|df=}} It was nominated for [[Africa Movie Academy Award for Best Nigerian Film|Best Nigerian Film]] and Best Film in an African Language.

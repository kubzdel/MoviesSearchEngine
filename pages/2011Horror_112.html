Romanian | budget = | gross = }} '''''Morituris''''' is a 2011 [[Italy|Italian]] [[horror film]] directed by Raffaele Picchio. The film had its world premiere on July 30, 2011, at [[Fantasia Festival]].{{cite web|title=Morituris|url=http://fantasiafestival.com/2011/en/films/film_detail.php?id=612|publisher=Fantasia Festival|accessdate=29 December 2013}} The film is inspired by the [[Circeo Massacre]], in which three young men abducted, and then raped and tortured, two young women over a two-day period. As a result of its graphic content, the film has been banned in Italy.{{cite web|title=Mean-Spirited 'Morituris' Gets Banned In Italy|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/news/3203378/mean-spirited-morituris-gets-banned-in-italy/|publisher=Bloody Disgusting|accessdate=29 December 2013}} In May 2013 [[Synapse Films]] announced that they had purchased the rights to ''Mortituris'' with the intent to release it to DVD.{{cite web|title=Italian gorefest "MORITURIS" coming to the U.S.|url=http://www.fangoria.com/new/italian-gorefest-morituris-coming-to-the-u-s/|publisher=Fangoria|accessdate=29 December 2013}} ==Plot== {{expand section|date=April 2015}} While out driving, three young men meet two beautiful women and convince them to go with them to a rave out in a remote location. Once there, the women realize that there is no rave and that the men lied to them in order to beat and rape them. The women try to escape, only to accidentally unleash a pack of zombie gladiators that proceed to attack the group as a whole. ==Cast== *Valentina D'Andrea *Andrea De Bruyn *Désirée Giorgetti *Francesco Malcom *Giuseppe Nitti *Simone Ripanti ==Reception== Critical reception for ''Morituris'' has been mixed, with some decrying what they saw as extreme [[misogyny]] and gratuitous scenes of [[rape]].{{cite web|last=Weinberg|first=Scott|title=Fantasia Fest 2011: 'Morituris' Review|url=http://www.fearnet.com/news/review/fantasia-fest-2011-morituris-review|publisher=Fearnet|accessdate=29 December 2013}} ''[[Twitch Film]]'' gave a mostly positive review, commenting that they found the extended rape scene almost unbearable but that overall that there is "a thin line between misanthropic fun and a hardcore exploitation film.

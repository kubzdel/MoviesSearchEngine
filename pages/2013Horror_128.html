Revolver Entertainment | released = {{Film date|2013|05||Monaco Film Festival|2014|2|14|United States}} | runtime = 94 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Lucky Bastard''''' is a 2013 [[Found footage (pseudo-documentary)|found footage]] [[horror film]] and the [[directorial debut]] of American producer [[Robert Stuart Nathan|Robert Nathan]]. The film had its world premiere in May 2013 at the Monaco Film Festival and was given a limited theatrical release on February 14, 2014 in the [[United States]]. It stars Don McManus as a porn site owner that finds more than he anticipated when he hires a seemingly docile young man ([[Jay Paulson]]) to have sex on camera.{{cite web|last=Zimmerman|first=Samuel|title=EXCL Clip: Mike and Ashley Choose Their “LUCKY BASTARD”|url=http://www.fangoria.com/new/excl-clip-mike-and-ashley-choose-their-lucky-bastard/|publisher=Fangoria|accessdate=30 April 2014}} The film's portrayals of sex and violence resulted in the movie gaining a [[NC-17]] rating. The film staff was given recommendations on how to change this to an [[Motion Picture Association of America film rating system|R rating]], but they declined because "if we recut it there won’t be any movie left". ==Cast== *[[Don McManus]] as Mike *[[Jay Paulson]] as Dave G. *[[Betsy Rue]] as Ashley Saint *[[Chris Wylde]] as Kris *[[Catherine Annette]] as Casey *[[Lee Kholafai]] as Josh *[[Lanny Joon]] as Nico *[[Clint Brink]] as Devin *[[Deborah Zoe]] as Suzanne *[[Angela Shin]] as Maitre D' ==Production== Of the film, Nathan stated that he was inspired to create the movie after his writing and producing partner showed him a porn website that would invite its members to have sex on camera.{{cite web|last=Coleman|first=Jason|title=Interview: 'Lucky Bastard' Helmer Robert Nathan On Bending Genres, Visual Style and Getting An NC-17|url=http://www.starpulse.com/news/Jason_Coleman/2014/03/07/interview_lucky_bastard_helmer_robert_?mobile_pref=FullSite|publisher=Star Pulse|accessdate=30 April 2014}} Nathan found it difficult to believe that the participants, who were portrayed as ordinary, were as normal as they appeared and wondered what it would be like if the "wrong guy" was hired.

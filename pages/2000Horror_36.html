Vía Digital | released = {{film date|2000|10|12|[[Sitges Film Festival]]|ref1={{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2000/film/news/gein-grabs-top-kudos-at-sitges-fest-1117787891/|title=‘Gein’ grabs top kudos at Sitges fest|author=|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=18 October 2000|accessdate=8 March 2014}}|df=y}} | runtime = 101 minutes | country = Spain | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Faust: Love of the Damned''''' is a 2000 English-language Spanish [[superhero film|superhero]] [[horror film|horror]] film, directed by [[Brian Yuzna]]. It is adapted from a screenplay by [[David Quinn (writer)|David Quinn]] and Miguel Tejada-Flores based on the [[Faust (Avatar Press)|comic book of the same name]] by [[Tim Vigil]] and David Quinn. It was produced by Ted Chalmers, Carlos, Julio and Antonio Fernández, Bea Morillas, Miguel Torrente and Brian Yuzna. It premiered at the [[Sitges Film Festival|Sitges International Fantastic Film Festival]] on 12 October 2000. The film, which was the first of nine to be produced by Filmax's ''[[Fantastic Factory]]'' label, won the award for Best Special Effects at the 2000 Catalan International Film Festival in [[Sitges]], Spain.{{cite web |url=http://www.filmaxinternational.com/fichas/343.htm |title=filmax International |work=filmaxinternational.com |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090303214206/http://www.filmaxinternational.com/fichas/343.htm |archivedate=3 March 2009 |accessdate=22 August 2012}} == Plot == {{expand section|date=October 2015}} An artist, John Jaspers (Mark Frost) sells his soul to the mysterious M (as for [[Mephistopheles]]) ([[Andrew Divoff]]) in order to avenge the death of his girlfriend, Blue (Jennifer Rope). However, the deal has an unexpected price, and he is periodically transformed into a horned demon with a passion for killing. He discovers that M plans to release a giant monster called the Homunculus, thereby opening the gates of Hell, and sets out to stop him. == Cast == * Mark Frost as John Jaspers / [[Faust]] * Isabel Brook as Jade de Camp * Jennifer Rope as Blue * [[Jeffrey Combs]] as Lt.

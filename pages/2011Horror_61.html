Breaking Glass Pictures | released = {{Film date|2011|9|24|Buffalo Niagara Film Festival}} | runtime = 100mins | country = United States | language = English | budget = }} '''''The Fields''''' is a 2011 American [[suspense thriller]] directed by Tom Mattera and Dave Mazzoni. The film is touted as being based on true events.[http://addictionary.fearnet.com/b15791_Exclusive__We_Speak_with_the_Filmmakers_Behind_Tara_Reid_s__The_Fields_.html?intcid=search_all_categories:the_fields Exclusive: We Speak with the Filmmakers Behind Tara Reid's 'The Fields'] The film stars [[Cloris Leachman]] and [[Tara Reid]]. Filming wrapped in October 2009, and the film played in festivals in Fall 2011.[https://www.facebook.com/thefieldsmovie The Fields Official Facebook] ==Plot== The film takes place in a small Pennsylvania town in 1973, and tells the story of a young boy (Joshua Ormond) and his family (Tara Reid, Faust Checho, Cloris Leachman, Bev Appleton) as they are terrorized by an unseen presence in the surrounding fields.[http://pretty-scary.net/content/cloris-leachman-and-tara-reid-star-fields Cloris Leachman and [[Tara Reid]] star in 'The Fields'] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20120720031450/http://pretty-scary.net/content/cloris-leachman-and-tara-reid-star-fields |date=2012-07-20 }} As a young boy, Steven finds himself dealing with a very difficult home life, as his parents are constantly at odds. As a result of the deteriorating marriage, Steven spends a lot of time with his grandparents on their farm, where, soon after his arrival, an unseen presence begins terrorizing them while using the massive cornfields surrounding the house to remain hidden. ==Cast== *[[Tara Reid]] as ''Bonnie'' *[[Cloris Leachman]] as ''Gladys'' * Bev Appleton as "Hiney" *Joshua Ormond as ''Steven'' *Faust Checho as ''Barry'' *[[Brian Anthony Wilson]] as ''Charlie'' *Miles Williams as ''Jack'' *Karen Ludwig as ''Tootie'' *Max Antisell as ''Douglas'' *Louis Morabito as "Eugene" *Renee McCartney as ''Sue'' *[[Danielle Treuberg]] as ''Hippie Girl #3'' ==Production== [[File:thefieldsmovie.jpg|right|thumb|The Fields Movie Website.]] The film was produced by Faust Checho with Mr. Big Productions, in association with MazWa Productions.[http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/33053/teaser-poster-the-fields Teaser Poster for The Fields] [[Tommy Lee Wallace]] is attached as an associate producer.[http://www.28dayslateranalysis.com/2009/09/fields-cast-details-and-movie-poster.html The Fields Cast Details and Movie Poster] Production spanned six weeks, throughout September and October 2009, and was shot on location in the Pocono Mountains region in Bartonsville, Pennsylvania and in Kunkletown, Pennsylvania. Some scenes were filmed in one of the oldest amusement parks in America, [[Bushkill Park]], originally built in the 1920s.[http://www.fearnet.com/news/b17221_fields_wraps_let_me_in_begins_production.html 'The Fields' Wraps, 'Let Me In' Begins Production] ==Release== The film was anticipated to be released in late 2011, it was finally released on April 24, 2012 in the USA on DVD and Blu-ray by Breaking Glass Pictures.[http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/53212/fields-finally-gets-release-date Dread Central The Fields release date] ==References== {{Reflist|2}} ==External links== * {{Official website|http://thefieldsmovie.com/}} *[http://breakingglasspictures.com ''Official USA distributor, Breaking Glass Pictures''] * {{IMDb title|1401236|The Fields}} * {{Rotten Tomatoes|the_fields}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Fields, The}} [[Category:2011 films]] [[Category:2011 horror films]] [[Category:2010s independent films]] [[Category:2010s mystery films]] [[Category:2010s psychological thriller films]] [[Category:American independent films]] [[Category:American horror thriller films]] [[Category:English-language films]] [[Category:Films set in Pennsylvania]] [[Category:Films set in 1973]] [[Category:Films shot in Pennsylvania]] [[Category:Films shot in New Orleans]] [[Category:American mystery films]] [[Category:American films]]

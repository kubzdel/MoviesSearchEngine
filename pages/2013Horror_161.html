[[XYZ Films]] | distributor = [[Anchor Bay Entertainment]] | released = {{Film date|2013|01|21|[[Sundance Film Festival]]}} | runtime = 97 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''The Rambler''''' is a 2013 American [[Independent film|independent]] [[horror film]] written and directed by Calvin Reeder. The film's narrative concerns a mysterious loner known only as the Rambler, played by [[Dermot Mulroney]]. Newly released from prison, he embarks on a dark journey across a bleak and strange contemporary landscape, meeting a bizarre cast of characters. ==Plot== {{no plot|date=June 2018}} ==Cast== * [[Dermot Mulroney]] as The Rambler * [[Lindsay Pulsipher]] as The Girl * [[Natasha Lyonne]] as Cheryl * James Cady as The Scientist ==Release== {{expand section|date=June 2018}} ''The Rambler'' was premiered at the [[2013 Sundance Film Festival]], and subsequently screened within such festivals as [[South by Southwest]] and [[Maryland Film Festival]].{{citation needed|date=June 2018}} ===Home media=== The film was released on [[DVD]] and [[Blu-ray]] by [[Lionsgate Home Entertainment|Lionsgate]] and [[Anchor Bay Entertainment|Starz/Anchor Bay]] on June 25, 2013.{{cite web |title=The Rambler (2013) - Calvin Reeder |url=https://www.allmovie.com/movie/the-rambler-v576402/releases |website=Allmovie.com |publisher=AllMovie |accessdate=7 June 2018}} ==Reception== {{expand section|date=June 2018}} On [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the film holds an approval rating of 40% based on {{nowrap|10 reviews}}, with a [[weighted average]] rating of 6.1/10.{{cite web |title=The Rambler (2013) - Rotten Tomatoes |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_rambler_2013/ |website=Rotten Tomatoes.com |publisher=Flixer |accessdate=7 June 2018}} On [[Metacritic]], which assigns a normalized rating to reviews, the film has a weighted average score of 32 out of 100, based on 6 critics, indicating "generally unfavorable reviews".{{cite web |title=The Rambler Reviews - Metacritic |url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/the-rambler |website=Metacritic.com |publisher=MetaCritic |accessdate=7 June 2018}} Amy Nicholson from ''[[LA Times]]'' gave the film a negative review, writing "What all of this means is, frankly, nothing.

It was picked up by [[IFC Films]] shortly after its world premiere at the 2008 [[Tribeca Film Festival]]. {{cite web|url=http://www.rainbow-media.com/release_release_press.jsp?nodeid=5901|title=AMC Networks Inc.|website=Rainbow-media.com|accessdate=11 September 2017}} {{cite web|url=http://www.indiewire.com/ots/2008/03/tribeca_08_trib.html |title=Archived copy |accessdate=March 14, 2008 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080314232152/http://www.indiewire.com/ots/2008/03/tribeca_08_trib.html |archivedate=March 14, 2008 }} Tribeca Unveils Discovery, Midnight Slates for '08 Fest ==Plot== {{expand section|date=September 2014}} This film is allegedly based on the real-life journals of [[Dale S. Rogers]], a man who, in the 1970s, lived along the banks of the [[Navidad River]] in [[Sublime, Texas]] - the same area where [[Wild Man of the Navidad|the original legend of the Wild Man of the Navidad]] surfaced back in the late 1800s.{{cite web|url=http://www.texasescapes.com/MurrayMontgomeryLoneStarDiary/Wild-Woman-of-the-Navidad.htm|title=Wild Woman of the Navidad.|website=Texasescapes.com|accessdate=11 September 2017}}{{cite web|url=http://www.wildmanofthenavidad.com|title=The Wild Man of the Navidad|website=Wildmanofthenavidad.com|accessdate=11 September 2017}} The film follows Dale, his wheelchair-using wife Jean, and her oft-shirtless, lazy-eyed caretaker Mario. Though their ranch sits on vast acres prime for paying hunters, Dale has resisted opening up the land because of the strange, [[Bigfoot]]-like creatures supposedly inhabiting it. But after the prodding of some of the rifle-loving townsfolk and the loss of his welding job, Dale gives in and opens the gate to his compound. Then the hunters become the hunted.[http://www.filmmakermagazine.com/blog/2008/04/tribeca-director-interview-justin-meeks.php] {{dead link|date=September 2017}} ==Cast== * [[Justin Meeks]]* as Dale S.

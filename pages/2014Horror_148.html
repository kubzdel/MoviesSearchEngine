Landon]]. Released on January 3, 2014 in the [[United States]], it is the fifth film of the [[Paranormal Activity (film series)|''Paranormal Activity'' film series]]. It is also Landon's second directorial film, after ''[[Burning Palms (film)|Burning Palms]]''. ==Plot== In June 2012, high school graduate Jesse Arista lives with his father, sister and grandmother in [[Oxnard, California]]. He begins to enjoy the summer with his best friend, Hector. In the apartment below lives a mysterious woman named Ana, who everyone believes to be a [[Brujería|witch]]. When Ana is found murdered, Jesse and Hector spot classmate Oscar running from the scene, suggesting he was responsible. The two investigate the apartment, where they find black magic items, as well as VHS tapes and a journal of spells that can "open doorways to unholy lands." After Jesse, Hector and their friend Marisol try out a ritual, paranormal occurrences gradually begin to take place in Jesse's apartment. One night, the trio begin to communicate with an unknown entity through a game. Jesse finds a mysterious bite mark on his arm and also discovers he has superhuman abilities, which is proven when he knocks out two thugs that assault him. He and Hector at first view his abilities as a "gift". At a party, Jesse takes a girl to Ana's apartment to have sex and encounters Oscar, who has oily black eyes and a similar bite mark on his arm. He tells Jesse that it's only a matter of time before "something inside them" will take over, and if they kill themselves they will not harm those they love. Oscar rushes and disappears; when Jesse and Hector search for him outside, Oscar commits suicide by jumping off a building, landing on a car. The group discover a secret trapdoor in Ana's apartment, where they find a witch altar and photos of Jesse, his pregnant mother, Ana, Oscar, and [[Paranormal Activity 3|Lois]]. At the same time, a strange woman in black enters, but leaves after finding nothing. Jesse is lured to the trapdoor one night after hearing his dog Chavo barking for help, but the door slams shut and Jesse sees the ghostly figures of young Katie and Kristi before being attacked by a roaring demon. Jesse's personality gradually becomes dark, suicidal, and extremely violent. Hector and Marisol are disturbed by Jesse's behavior and meet Oscar's criminal brother Arturo, who tells them that Oscar was in contact with Ali Rey, who had researched demons after [[Paranormal Activity 2|her father and step-mother were killed and half-brother kidnapped by a possessed Katie]]. They meet with Ali, who tells them that Jesse has been "marked" by a worldwide coven of witches called the "Midwives", who have been brainwashing women to give up their firstborn sons to create an army of possessed young men. Ali gives an address to where a final ritual is supposed to take place, and warns that Jesse will no longer exist if the demon manages to fully consume him. Jesse's concerned grandmother Irma visits a [[botánica]] and tries to cleanse Jesse, but he [[Telekinesis|telekinetically]] tears the living room apart. The next morning, an ill Jesse pushes Irma down the stairs before disappearing. While Hector and Marisol drive to the hospital, Jesse ambushes them in the street and attacks Hector, but Marisol knocks him unconscious with a bat. As they are attempting to leave, a van smashes into their car, Irma got killed and Jesse is kidnapped. With help from Arturo and his friend Santo, Hector and Marisol go to the address, which turns out to be [[Paranormal Activity 3|Grandma Lois's house, where Katie and Kristi's mother and her boyfriend were killed]]. When they enter the garden, coven members with knives appear and begin to attack them. Arturo urges Hector and Marisol to flee and they both run to the house, leaving Arturo alone in the garden to defend himself. Santo is found dead by the front door. Marisol disappears, Arturo gets killed off-screen (it is implied that he was overpowered by the coven and then stabbed to death) and then thrown and smashed into a window near to Hector, later, Marisol's corpse is thrown through a glass roof. A possessed Jesse appears and chases Hector upstairs where Hector slams the door shut. Jesse's normal voice asks Hector to open the door but after he doesn't, Jesse breaks down the door causing Hector to open a strange brown door taking him through a portal to the events of [[Paranormal Activity|October 8, 2006, at the household of Katie and Micah]]. Katie goes downstairs to the kitchen and screams for Micah upon seeing Hector. Micah assumes Hector is an intruder and attacks him, but Katie becomes hysterical and stabs him to death with a kitchen knife. Hector runs away, but Jesse, now completely under the demon's influence, appears and attacks him off-screen (presumably killing him) as the camera falls to the ground. Though the film is targeted to the Latino market, most of its dialogue is not in Spanish. [[Christopher B. Landon]], who wrote the screenplay for 2007's ''[[Disturbia (film)|Disturbia]]'', as well as the three ''Paranormal Activity'' sequels, was announced to write and direct the project, which has been described as a "cousin" or "ese" to the series as opposed to a direct sequel, prequel, or reboot. The film maintains the look of found footage, a style used throughout the ''[[Paranormal Activity (film series)|Paranormal Activity]]'' series.{{cite web|last=Taylor|first=Drew|title=The First Trailer for Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones Will Freak You Out|url=http://news.moviefone.com/2013/10/17/paranormal-activity-the-marked-ones-trailer/|work=Movie Fone|accessdate=October 21, 2013}} Filming finished in late July, after producer [[Jason Blum]] confirmed that the film was almost finished shooting. This resulted in the film getting its release date pushed from October 25, 2013, to January 3, 2014. ==Release== ''Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones'' was released on January 3, 2014 in U.S., Mexican and Canadian theaters.{{cite web|title=Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones Global Sites and Release Dates|url=http://apps.paramountpictures.com/movie/themarkedones/releasedates/release-dates.html/|publisher=Paramount Pictures|accessdate=October 21, 2013}} The release date was pushed to January due to its longer and larger production and shooting than the previous films. ===Box office=== Despite predictions suggesting that ''The Marked Ones'' would open at #1 in its debut weekend,{{cite web|last=Subers|first=Ray|title=Forecast: 'Paranormal' Spin-Off to Haunt First Weekend of 2014|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/news/?id=3768&p=.htm|website=[[Box Office Mojo]]|accessdate=January 2, 2014|date=January 2, 2013}} the film took a close second to ''[[Frozen (2013 film)|Frozen]]'' (in its seventh week) with $18,343,611 at the North American box office.{{cite web|last=Subers|first=Ray|title=Weekend Report: 'Frozen' Chills 'Paranormal' Spin-Off|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/news/?id=3769&p=.htm|website=[[Box Office Mojo]]|accessdate=January 6, 2014|date=January 6, 2013}} By the end of its theatrical run, ''The Marked Ones'' grossed $32.5 million in North America and $58.4 million in other territories for a worldwide total of $90.9 million.{{cite web|title=Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=paranormalactivity5.htm|website=[[Box Office Mojo]]|accessdate=June 14, 2014|date=January 6, 2013}} ===Critical reception=== On [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the film received a score of 39%, with an average score of 4.6/10, based on 80 reviews. The site's consensus states: "A change of setting breathes some new life into the franchise, but ''Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones'' fails to provide enough consistent thrills to justify a [[Paranormal Activity: The Ghost Dimension|fifth film]] in the series".{{cite web|title=Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/paranormal_activity_the_marked_ones/|work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|publisher=[[Flixster]]|accessdate=January 5, 2014}} On [[Metacritic]], the film has a score of 42 out of 100, based on 19 reviews, considered to be "mixed or average reviews".{{cite web|title=Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones Reviews|url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/paranormal-activity-the-marked-ones|work=[[Metacritic]]|publisher=[[CBS Interactive]]|accessdate=January 5, 2014}} The change of setting and tone was primarily praised by critics. Mark Olsen of the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' praised the film, saying that it "feels like a fresh start".{{cite web|title=Review: 'Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones' has fresh frights|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/movies/moviesnow/la-et-mn-paranormal-activity-20140103,0,1833700.story#axzz2pI7CSqLN|work=Mark Olsen|publisher=[[Los Angeles Times]]|accessdate=January 5, 2014}} ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' critic Andrew Barker celebrated the "welcome diversity and humor" of the film as did [[Richard Corliss]] of ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine who summarised that the film "provided the familiar cheap thrills but with a salsa tang." {{cite web |title= Film Review: Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones |url= https://variety.com/2014/film/reviews/film-review-paranormal-activity-the-marked-ones-1201024158/ |author= Andrew Barker |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |accessdate= January 5, 2014}}{{cite web |title= Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones: Familiar Scares With a Salsa Tang |url= http://entertainment.time.com/2014/01/03/paranormal-activity-the-marked-ones-familiar-scares-with-a-salsa-tang/ |work= [[Richard Corliss]] |publisher= [[Time Magazine]] |accessdate= January 5, 2014}} ''[[Filmink|Filmink Magazine]]'' critic Eden Caceda applauded the humor and action, but claimed that the spin off "lacks the memorable scares required for this to rate any higher than an above average horror film."{{cite web |title= Film Review: Paranormal Activity: The Marked Ones |url= http://www.filmink.com.au/reviews/paranormal-activity-the-marked-ones-film/ |work= Eden Caceda |publisher= [[Filmink]] |accessdate= January 5, 2014}} Evan Dickson of [[Bloody Disgusting]] was the first to give his impressions on the film, giving it a positive review of 4/5. Stating, "Fun, scary and remarkably cinematic within the found footage conceit, ''The Marked Ones'' might be the first ''Paranormal Activity'' movie that feels like an event film while you’re watching it".

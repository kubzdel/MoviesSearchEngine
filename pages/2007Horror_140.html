The film deviates from the usual stories about spirits showing an ending where the good guy do not always win. This film was Williams' debut.{{cite journal|url=http://www.esmas.com/espectaculos/cine/607741.html|author= Mayra Adauto Gómez |title=Presentan J-ok'el|journal=Esmas.com|date=Feb 27, 2007|accessdate=Sep 13, 2011}} == Plot == An American man travels to a small town in [[Chiapas]], Mexico called [[San Cristobal de las Casas]], to help his mother when he knows that his stepsister has been abducted. Everything indicates that it is a wave of kidnappings attributed to the legendary J-ok'el ([[Weeping Woman]]). This woman had drowned her children a long time ago and her spirit has returned to take other children and thus forget her own suffering.{{cite journal|url=http://anodis.com/nota/8753.asp|author=|title=J-ok'el: la llorona se aparece en Chiapas|journal=Anodis.com|date=Mar 5, 2007|accessdate=Sep 13, 2011}} == Awards == The film won gold medal for best music in the Park City Film Festival in Park City, Utah.{{cite journal|url=http://www.rodrigodelgado.com/2009/04/j-okel-la-llorona|author= Rodrigo Delgado |title=J-ok’el, la Llorona|journal=RodrigoDelgado.com|date=Apr 5, 2009|accessdate=Sep 13, 2011}} == Trivia == J-ok'el means "weeping woman" in [[Tzotzil language|Tzotzil]] language.

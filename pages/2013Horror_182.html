It stars [[Dallas Roberts]] and [[Alison Eastwood]]. == Plot == The film begins with a series of videos posted to [[YouTube]], with users of the website relating their experiences with unexplained sightings. Later, radio talk show host Charlie Crowe (Dallas Roberts) becomes convinced that mysterious nocturnal entities known as [[Shadow person|shadow people]] are responsible for a spate of deaths in his hometown. He is joined by [[Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|CDC]] investigator Sophie Lacombe (Alison Eastwood), who seeks a more rational explanation. == Cast == * [[Dallas Roberts]] as Charlie Crowe * [[Alison Eastwood]] as Sophie Lacombe * [[Anne Dudek]] as Ellen * Mariah Bonner as Maggie * [[Mattie Liptak]] as Preston * Christopher Berry as Tom * Jonathan Baron as Jeff Pyatt * Tony Schiena as Robert * Richie Montgomery as Sparky * [[Marco St. John]] as Professor Fisher * Raeden Greer as Britney * [[Billy Slaughter]] as TV Anchor * Earl Maddox as store clerk == Production == ''Shadow People'' was filmed in [[Baton Rouge]], [[Louisiana]] and in [[Phnom Penh]], [[Cambodia]].{{citation needed|date=December 2013}} Events depicted in the film are set in Cambodia in 1979 and in Eastern [[Kentucky]] in 1971 and 2008. The film mixes dramatisations with real and [[Mockumentary|faux]] archival footage. The faux archival footage includes interviews with people who are depicted in the dramatisations. The real archival footage includes segments from the 2008 web documentary ''Your Worst Nightmare''.{{cite web |url= http://culturecrypt.com/movie-reviews/shadow-people-2012|title= Shadow People (2012)|work=Culture Crypt |date=March 13, 2013 |first=Ian|last=Sedensky|accessdate=November 30, 2013}} The film is dedicated to victims of [[Sudden unexpected death syndrome|Sudden Unexpected Nocturnal Death Syndrome]] (SUNDS). == Release == The film premiered at the [[Marché du Film]] in [[Cannes]] in 2012.{{cite web |url= http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/cannes-shadow-people-dallas-roberts-alison-eastwood-329644/|title= Cannes 2012: 'Shadow People' Picked Up by Anchor Bay for North America, the U.K, New Zealand|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |date=May 25, 2012 |first=Stuart|last=Kemp |accessdate=November 30, 2013}} It was released [[direct-to-video]] on March 19, 2013. == Reception == Matt Molgaard of [[Best Horror Movies]] wrote, "This is a stirring flick, and in truth, I’d be holding back if I called Shadow People anything other than an outstanding success that far exceeded expectations...Shadow People has already established itself as one of 2013’s most inspired efforts."{{cite web |url= http://www.best-horror-movies.com/review?name=shadow-people-2013-review|title= 'Shadow People 2013 Review' |publisher=[[Best Horror Movies]] |date=March 27, 2013 |first=Matt|last=Molgaard|accessdate=November 30, 2013}} David Maine of [[Pop Matters]] wrote, "Shadow People is a spooky little movie that could have been much spookier, but still makes good use of its limited means to convey an unsettling story."{{cite web |url= http://www.popmatters.com/review/169467-shadow-people/|title= 'Shadow People', a Low Budget Creeper, Is Low on Effects, High on Atmospherics|work=[[PopMatters]] |date=March 19, 2013 |first=David|last=Maine |accessdate=November 30, 2013}} [[Ain't It Cool News]] declared "it made me think twice about turning on the light as I went to bed.

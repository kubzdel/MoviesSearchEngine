{{Infobox film | name = Spring Break Zombie Massacre | image = | alt = | caption = | director = Bobby Carnevale | producer = Jesse Suchmann | writer = {{plainlist| * Sam Suchmann * Mattie Zufelt }} | starring = {{plainlist| * Sam Suchmann * Mattie Zufelt }} | music = | cinematography = | editing = Tim Forster | studio = | distributor = | released = {{film date|2016|07|07}} | runtime = 45 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Spring Break Zombie Massacre''''' is a 2016 American horror short film directed by Bobby Carnevale. Sam Suchmann and Mattie Zufelt star as half-brothers who must stop Satan from ruining their [[spring break]]. == Premise == After failing to steal their magic powers, Satan kills the mothers of two half-brothers shortly after they are born. Twenty years later, the brothers must use their powers to stop Satan and his zombie minions from ruining their spring break. == Cast == * Sam Suchmann as Sam * Mattie Zufelt as Mattie * Madeline Brumby as Nikki * Justine S Harrison as Elizabeth/Mrs. Fox * Allison Maier as A. J. * Joseph Turner as Frakenzombie *Johnny Collins as the Devil [[Pauly D]] appears in a cameo near the end. == Production == Sam Suchmann and Mattie Zufelt, both of whom have [[Down syndrome]], met at the [[Special Olympics]]. Sam's older brother, Jesse, said their families initially viewed the friends' enthusiasm for a film as a phase the two would eventually outgrow. When they did not, Jesse investigated further, finding that they had written a notebook full of scenes, including storyboards.{{cite news|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/best-friends-zombie-movie-gives-audiences-something-to-smile-about/?google_editors_picks=true|title=Best friends' zombie movie gives audiences something to smile about|last=Hartman|first=Steve|work=[[CBS News]]|date=2016-10-28|accessdate=2016-10-28}} Jesse initially considered shooting the film do-it-yourself style but was impressed enough with their work that he took the film to [[Kickstarter]], where it received $68,000 in funding.{{cite news|url=https://www.pbs.org/newshour/art/turn-reporter-zombie/|title=How this NewsHour reporter became a zombie|last=Melia|first=Mike|work=[[PBS NewsHour]]|date=2015-08-27|accessdate=2016-10-28}} Taking the position of producer, Jesse brought in director Bobby Carnevale, whom he had been friends with in high school.{{cite news|url=http://www.providencejournal.com/news/20160713/ri-friends-with-down-syndrome-premiere-their-epic-zombie-movie|title=RI friends with Down syndrome premiere their epic zombie movie|last=McDermott|first=Jennifer|work=[[The Providence Journal]]|agency=[[Associated Press]]|date=2016-07-13|accessdate=2016-10-28}} Filming took place over 16 days in Providence, Rhode Island. The Kickstarter campaign and resulting publicity resulted in increased interest in the film, and industry veterans like [[Farrelly brothers]] visited the set. Casting involved diverse groups of people. Some of the extras are friends on the autism spectrum or with Downs, and some auditioned after discovering the project. Other talent came from Atlanta, where director Carnevale had contacts.{{cite news|url=http://www.ajc.com/entertainment/movies/inspired-atlantans-play-big-part-zombie-massacre/kfoRQGUqWpQ0RcXNkl1JnO/|title=Inspired Atlantans play a big part in ‘Zombie Massacre’|last=Waterhouse|first=Jon|work=[[Atlanta Journal-Constitution]]|date=2016-10-18|accessdate=2016-10-28}} [[Pauly D]], a hero to Mattie, accepted a cameo role after the friends insisted that he be approached. Jesse said the film was Sam and Mattie's project, and they were not simply helped by a professional film crew.{{cite news|url=http://www.boston.com/culture/entertainment/2016/07/09/sam-and-mattie|title=How two best friends with Down syndrome from Rhode Island made the kickass action movie of their dreams|last=Slane|first=Kevin|work=[[Boston.com]]|date=2016-07-09|accessdate=2016-10-28}} A sequel is planned. == Release == ''Spring Break Zombie Massacre'' premiered on July 7, 2016, in Providence, Rhode Island.{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/174551/teenage-friends-unleash-spring-break-zombie-massacre-watch-trailer/|title=Teenage Friends Unleash a Spring Break Zombie Massacre; Watch the Trailer!|last=Squires|first=John|work=[[Dread Central]]|date=2016-07-01|accessdate=2016-10-28}} It later played at the [[Telluride Film Festival]] in October 2016.{{cite web|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3411115/telluride-horror-show-16-recap-festival/|title=[Telluride Horror Show ’16] A Recap of the Festival|last=Barkan|first=Jonathan|work=[[Bloody Disgusting]]|date=2016-10-21|accessdate=2016-10-29}} == Reception == [[Steve Hartman]] of [[CBS News]] called it "really gross in parts, terribly offensive in others and completely ludicrous throughout", concluding that it is a future [[cult film]]. == References == {{reflist}} == External links == * {{IMDb title|5831924}} [[Category:2016 films]] [[Category:2016 horror films]] [[Category:American films]] [[Category:American short films]] [[Category:American horror films]] [[Category:English-language films]] [[Category:Supernatural horror films]] [[Category:Demons in film]] [[Category:Zombie films]]

{{Infobox film | name = Eyes in the Dark | image = Eyes-in-the-Dark-Poster-Final.jpg | caption = Promotional poster | director = [[Bjorn Anderson (director)|Bjorn Anderson]] | producer = Bjorn Anderson | writer = Bjorn Anderson | starring = {{ubl|[[Wayne Bastrup]]|[[Melissa Goad]]|[[Maureen Francisco]]|[[Paul Eenhoorn]]}} | music = S.Y.F.T. | cinematography = [[Joseph Cole (cinematographer)|Joseph Cole]] | editing = Robyn Scaringi | studio = Emerald City Pictures | distributor = | released = {{Film date|2010|4|25|Alabama Film Festival}} | runtime = 78 minutes | country = United States | language = English }} '''''Eyes in the Dark''''' is a 2010 American horror film written and directed by [[Bjorn Anderson (director)|Bjorn Anderson]]. It is filmed in the "[[Found footage (pseudo-documentary)|found footage]]" style.{{cite web|url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/seattlefilmbuff/archives/184282.asp|title=Local Filmmakers Debut Their Horror Flick|date=2009-11-05|publisher=Seattle Post Intelligencer}} ==Plot== {{more plot|date=June 2018}} The movie follows seven college students as they take a weekend trip to a lodge in the [[Cascade Mountains]]. As they film themselves for their [[video blog]], they record some strange occurrences. The students and the lodge's caretaker come across a mysterious cave with unusual markings. They soon become hunted by an unknown creature with glowing red eyes. ==Cast== * [[Wayne Bastrup]] as Josh Adams * [[Melissa Goad]] as Lacy Meyers * John Symonds as Dean McClaren * [[Maureen Francisco]] as Heather Conroy * Telisa Steen as Stephanie Miller * Melinda Ausserer as Allison Stevens * Jason Robison as John Smith * [[Paul Eenhoorn]] as Anders * Ernie Joseph as Dr. Steven Monteray * Charlie Sims as Dr. Wesley Mason ==Production== The idea for the film came to director [[Bjorn Anderson (director)|Bjorn Anderson]] in a nightmare. He was influenced by ''[[Cloverfield]]'' and ''[[The Blair Witch Project]]'' to shoot the movie in a similar first-person point-of-view style.{{cite web|url= http://roberthood.net/blog/index.php/2010/02/23/dreams-from-the-dark/|title=Dreams From the Dark|date=February 23, 2010|publisher=Undead Backbrain}} Anderson first pitched the idea of the ''Eyes in the Dark'' to producers Joseph Cole and Mike Ash in 2007 while finishing their first film, ''[[Warrior's End]]''. [[Principal photography]] was done during the summer of 2008, but additional scenes were filmed later.{{cite web|url=http://www.issaquahpress.com/2010/02/02/an-eye-for-directing/|title=An Eye for Directing|date=February 2, 2010|publisher=The Issaquah Press|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20121208142343/http://www.issaquahpress.com/2010/02/02/an-eye-for-directing/|archivedate=December 8, 2012|df=}} ==Release== The film had its world premiere on April 25, 2010 at the Alabama International Film Festival.{{cite web|url= http://www.alafilm.org/films.html|title=Festival Films|date=April 2010|publisher=Alabama International Film Festival}}{{cite web|url= http://www.firstfright.com/horrorpress/northwest-filmmaker-has-world-premiere-in-the-southeast/|title=Northwest Filmmaker Has World Premiere In The Southeast|date=2010-04-25|publisher=FirstFright.com}} In 2010, it was also an official selection for [[Seattle's True Independent Film Festival (STIFF)|Seattle's True Independent Film Festival]], Fright Night Film Fest, Local Sightings, and Killer Film Fest. The movie was released on DVD through [[Amazon.com]] on March 21, 2011.{{cite web | url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B004T4N3L6 | title=Eyes in the Dark: Movies & TV | publisher=Amazon.com | date=2011-03-21 }} ==Reception== {{expand section|date=October 2014}} Viewings of the trailer for ''Eyes in the Dark'' created curiosity for what the red eyes are.{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/36278/what-are-eyes-dark-staring-back-you|title=What Are the Eyes in the Dark Staring Back at You?|date=2010-03-06|publisher=Dread Central}} After attending an early, private screening, movie reviewer Jeff Walls said that he “enjoyed the film” and it “was edited together really well.” Just before the DVD release, Reviewer Hal C. F. Astell describes the movie as "astounding" in comparison to big budget movies like ''[[Avatar (2009 film)|Avatar]]''.

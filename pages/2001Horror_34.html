{{Infobox film | name = Elvira's Haunted Hills | image = Elvira's-Haunted-Hills.jpg | alt = | caption = Theatrical release poster | director = [[Sam Irvin]] | producer = Mark Pierson | writer = {{Plainlist| * [[Cassandra Peterson]] * [[John Paragon]] }} | starring = {{Plainlist| * [[Cassandra Peterson|Elvira]] * [[Richard O'Brien]] * [[Mary Scheer]] * Scott Atkinson * Mary Jo Smith * Heather Hopper }} | music = Eric Allaman | cinematography = Viorel Sergovici | editing = Stephen Myers | studio = {{Plainlist| * [[MediaPro Studios]] * Queen B Productions * The Elvira Movie Company }} | distributor = The Elvira Movie Company | released = {{Film date|2001|6|23|International Rocky Horror Fan Convention|2002|10|31|United States}} | runtime = 90 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = $1.5 million }} '''''Elvira's Haunted Hills''''' is a 2001 American [[comedy horror]] film directed by [[Sam Irvin]]. The film [[Film premiere|premiered]] at the International [[The Rocky Horror Picture Show|Rocky Horror]] Fan Convention on June 23, 2001. It was the second film starring [[Cassandra Peterson]] in the title role, after ''[[Elvira, Mistress of the Dark]]'' (1988). ==Plot== In 1851 in the [[Carpathian Mountains]] of Romania, Elvira and her maidservant Zou Zou (Mary Jo Smith), on their way to a [[can-can]] revue in [[Paris]], get kicked out of an inn for a slight monetary discrepancy. After making their way out of the village, they are rescued by Dr. Bradley Bradley (Scott Atkinson), who takes them to stay at Castle Hellsubus, in the hills high above the village. While there, Elvira meets the residents—and discovers that she happens to resemble the deceased former wife of his Lordship the Count Vladimere Hellsubus. ==Cast== * [[Cassandra Peterson]] as Elvira / Lady Elura Hellsubus * [[Richard O'Brien]] as Lord Vladimere Hellsubus * [[Mary Scheer]] as Lady Ema Hellsubus * Scott Atkinson as Dr. Bradley Bradley * Gabriel Andronache as Adrian * Mary Jo Smith as Zou Zou * Heather Hopper as Lady Roxanna Hellsubus * Remus Cernat as Nicholai Hellsubus * Lucia Maier as the maid * Jerry Jackson as the English gentleman * [[Theodor Danetti]] as the innkeeper ==Production== The film fondly parodies most of the [[Roger Corman]]-directed [[Edgar Allan Poe]] films and the British horror films from [[Hammer Studios]].

''Harbinger'' was among films screened at 22nd [[San Antonio Film Festival]], {{cite web |url=https://safilm.com/event/harbinger/ |title=Harbinger |publisher=[[San Antonio Film Festival]] |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170809085604/https://safilm.com/event/harbinger/ |archivedate = 2017-08-09 |accessdate=2017-08-09}} 19th [[Cine Las Americas International Film Festival]] and 49th WorldFest-Houston festival, where it won "Silver Remi Award". {{cite web |url=https://worldfest.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/WFH49FinalRemiAwards.pdf |title=All 2016 Remi Winners |publisher= WorldFest-Houston festival |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6saJS3KW3 |archivedate = 2017-08-09 |accessdate=2017-08-09}} == Plot == The [[Fantasy|fantasy]] film follows twelve-year-old girl Mira Gonzaga who has been having terrible dreams after a [[traumatic brain injury]] of an undisclosed origin. Taking care of Mira and his pregnant wife, Jesús Gonzaga moves the family away from the city to a secluded [[ranch]] in the [[Texas]] hill-country. Mira soon discovers that the ranch is just as far from safety. She has vivid night dreams, sees her deceased grandmother and a monster who claims to be her soon-to-be-born brother. There are several hints in the movie that these strange occurrences are taking place as a result of poisoned water from nearby [[Hydraulic fracturing in the United States|fracking operations]]. After Mira's mother dies and Jesús tries to feed Mira poisonous wild boar meat, their neighbor Dimitri comes and saves her, chasing Jesús until giant [[drilling rig]]s explode. == Cast == * Dimitrius Pulido as Jesús Gonzaga * Tina Rodriguez as Gabriella Gonzaga * Paeka Campos as Mira Gonzaga * Joseph T. Campos as Dimitri * Anne Frances as Abuela * Gerard Flores as Kitty (monster) * Roland Uribe as Doctor == Production == Harbinger was shot in [[Texas]] during summer 2014 between [[Fischer, Texas |Fischer]] and [[Austin, Texas| Austin]]. The original script was written as a horror film while the director was still in college.

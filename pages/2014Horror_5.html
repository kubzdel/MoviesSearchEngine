It contains 26 different shorts, each by different directors spanning various countries. It is a sequel to the 2012 film ''[[The ABCs of Death]]''.{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2013/film/news/abcs-of-death-gets-sequel-1200481272/|title=Cannes: ‘ABCs of Death’ Gets Sequel|work=Variety|date=May 5, 2013|author=Rachel Abrams|accessdate=October 5, 2014}} Directors featured include [[Jim Hosking]], [[Lancelot Oduwa Imasuen]], the [[Soska sisters]], [[Julian Barrett]], [[Rodney Ascher]], [[Kristina Buožytė]], [[Larry Fessenden]], [[Aharon Keshales]], [[Bill Plympton]], and [[Vincenzo Natali]]. The film received a much more positive response than its predecessor. A second sequel, ''[[ABCs of Death 2.5]]'', was released in late 2016. ==Plot== Exactly like the first film, the sequel is divided into twenty-six individual chapters, each helmed by a different director assigned a letter of the alphabet. The directors were then given free rein in choosing a word to create a story involving death. The varieties of death range from accidents to murders.{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/pop-culture/2014/10/abcs-of-death-2-interviews/|title=Permanent Midnight: What’s Crazier Than "ABCs of Death 2"?

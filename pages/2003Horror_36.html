Península Films | distributor = [[Lions Gate Entertainment|Lionsgate]] | released = {{Film date|2003|5|13}} | runtime = 88 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = $250,000 }} '''''Gacy''''' is a 2003 [[direct-to-video]] [[Biography film|biographical]]-[[drama film]] directed by Clive Saunders and written by Saunders and David Birke. The story revolves around the life of [[serial killer]] [[John Wayne Gacy]]. ==Plot== {{expand section|date=May 2015}} [[John Wayne Gacy]] ([[Mark Holton]]) seemed to be a model citizen. He even volunteered as a clown for the children at the local hospital, but he kept a gruesome secret. A trail of missing young men led to Gacy's suburban home. The nation watched in horror as, one by one, the details of over 30 murders came to light and most of the victims, entombed in the crawl space underneath his house, were unearthed. John Wayne Gacy had been one of the most infamous killers in the whole country. ==Cast== * [[Mark Holton]] (Scott Alan Henry, young) as [[John Wayne Gacy|John Wayne Gacy, Jr.]] * [[Adam Baldwin]] as John Gacy, Sr. * [[Charlie Weber (actor)|Charlie Weber]] as Tom Kovacs * Allison Lange as Gretchen * Edith Jefferson as Mother Gacy * Joleen Lutz as Karen Gacy * Kenneth Swartz as Dave * Matt Farnsworth as Stu * [[Joseph Sikora]] as Roger * Jeremy Lelliot as Little Stevie * [[Oren Skoog]] as Jimmy * Joe Roncetti as Peter * Eddie Adams as Duane * Doran Ray as Tony * [[Larry Hankin]] as Eddie Bloom * [[Glenn Morshower]] and Jessica Schatz as Ted and Julie Boyle * Jessica and Grace Hanamoto as the daughters * Wyatt Denny as Steve ==Production notes== * The character of Tom Kovacs is a composite of two young men who had lived with Gacy in reality. ==Reception== {{expand section|date=May 2015}} On the film review aggregate website [[Rotten Tomatoes]], ''Gacy'' received a 17% approval rating, based on six reviews, with an average rating of 3.8/10.{{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/gacy/|title=Gacy (film)|publisher=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|accessdate=2011-06-28}} Chuck Wilson of ''[[LA Weekly]]'' found ''Gacy'' to be an "unenlightening" and "unconvincing" film that suffered from "dime-store psychology" and a failure to "convey the subtleties of Gacy’s scheming intellect." {{cite web|url = http://www.laweekly.com/film/film_results.php?showid=2329|title = ''Gacy''|last = Wilson|first = Chuck|publisher = [[Internet Archive]]|website = laweekly.com|accessdate = 19 June 2018|deadurl = yes|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20030708224114/http://www.laweekly.com/film/film_results.php?showid=2329|archivedate = 8 July 2003}} Mike Long of [[DVD Talk]] gave the film a half star out of a possible five, opined that it "offers no suspense, no gore, and no thrills" and concluded, "If nothing else, a film about a serial-killer should offer a glimpse inside the criminal mind.

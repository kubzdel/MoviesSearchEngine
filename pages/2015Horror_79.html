{{Infobox film | name = The Final Project | image = | alt = | caption = Theatrical release poster | director = [[Taylor Ri'chard|Taylor Ri’chard]] | producer = {{plainlist| * [[Taylor Ri'chard]] * Zach Davis }} | writer = {{plainlist| * [[Taylor Ri'chard]] * Zach Davis }} | starring = {{plainlist| * Teal Haddock * Arin Jones * Leonardo Santaiti * Evan McLean * Sergio Suave * Amber Erwin }} | music = {{plainlist| * Antoine Wilson * Paula Taylor }} | cinematography = Leonardo Santaiti | editing = Richard Robinson | studio = 3rd Fathom Films | distributor = Cavu Pictures | released = {{film date|2015|04|16|Louisiana|2016|02|12|Limited release|2016|03|04|Wide}} | runtime = 80 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''The Final Project''''' is a 2015 American [[Found footage (pseudo-documentary)|found footage]] [[horror film]] directed by [[Taylor Ri'chard]]. It stars Teal Haddock, Arin Jones, Leonardo Santaiti, Evan McLean, Sergio Suave, and Amber Erwin as student ghosthunters who fall prey to a vengeful spirit at a haunted Louisiana plantation. It premiered in April 2015 and received a theatrical release in February 2016. == Plot == After being told that they require extra credit to pass their film class, six college students investigate an abandoned plantation in rural Louisiana despite warnings from the locals that it is haunted. While making a documentary, they find the rumors of a hostile spirit are true. == Cast == * Teal Haddock as Anna Davenport * Arin Jones as Genevieve Richard * Leonardo Santaiti as Jonah Girard * Evan McLean as Ky Brooks * Sergio Suave as Gavin Charles * Amber Erwin as Misty Gilroy * Robert McCarley as Professor McCarley * Tiffany Ford as store clerk * Benjie Anderson as caretaker * Charles Orr as Charles == Production == After becoming dissatisfied with his corporate job, Ri'chard left it to pursue filmmaking.{{cite web|url=http://www.artsatl.com/2016/02/preview-taylor-richard-the-final-project/|title=Preview: Taylor Ri'chard leaves the corporate world to chase his dream of "The Final Project"|last=Farmer|first=Jim|work=Arts Atlanta|date=2016-02-12|accessdate=2016-02-20}} The story, co-written by Richard, was based on local stories about [[Chretien Point Plantation]] in [[Sunset, Louisiana]].{{cite web|url=http://www.shockya.com/news/2016/02/09/interview-taylor-richard-talks-the-final-project-exclusive/|title=Interview: Taylor Ri’chard Talks The Final Project (Exclusive)|last=Benardello|first=Karen|work=Shockya.com|date=2016-02-09|accessdate=2016-02-09}} When casting the film, Ri'chard looked for inexperienced actors, as he wanted the film to seem realistic and true to the "found footage" genre.{{cite web|url=http://www.backstage.com/interview/1-hands-approach-casting-horror-film/|title=1 Hands-On Approach to Casting a Horror Film|last=Williams|first=Jonathan|work=[[Backstage (magazine)|Backstage]]|date=2016-02-19|accessdate=2016-02-20}} Shooting began in 2012, but not all of the sets were available until 2014. Principle photography lasted six weeks and took place in [[Atlanta, Georgia]].{{cite web|url=http://rollingout.com/2016/02/12/taylor-richard-discusses-his-new-horror-film-the-final-project/|title=Taylor Ri’chard discusses his new horror film, 'The Final Project'|author=|work=RollingOut.com|date=2016-02-12|accessdate=2016-02-20}} Ri'chard said he wanted to build relationships in his new home, Atlanta, rather than shoot on location. == Release == The film screened in a one-night engagement in Louisiana, Texas and Georgia on April 16, 2015.{{cite news|url=http://www.theadvertiser.com/story/entertainment/2015/04/14/church-point-native-directs-horror-film-local-setting/25791873/|title=Church Point native directs horror film in local setting|last=Fuelier|first=Herman|work=[[The Daily Advertiser (Lafayette)|The Daily Advertiser]]|date=2015-04-15|accessdate=2016-02-20}} CAVU Pictures released it theatrically on February 12, 2016, in Atlanta and Houston.

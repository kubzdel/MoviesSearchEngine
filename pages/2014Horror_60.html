{{Infobox film | name = The Device | image = | alt = | caption = | director = Jeremy Berg | producer = {{plainlist| * Matt Medisch * John Portanova * Jeremy Berg }} | writer = {{plainlist| * Jeremy Berg * John Portanova }} | starring = {{plainlist| * Angela DiMarco * David S. Hogan * Kate Alden }} | music = Joseph Molner | cinematography = Jeremy Berg | editing = Autumn Lisa Mason | studio = {{plainlist| * October People * Ruthless Pictures }} | distributor = [[Image Entertainment]] | released = {{film date|2014|09|27}} | runtime = 87 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''The Device''''' is a 2014 American science fiction horror film directed by Jeremy Berg. It stars Angela DiMarco and Kate Alden as sisters who must deal with an [[alien abduction]] after one's husband, played by David S. Hogan, becomes obsessed with a strange device. == Plot == Still recovering from a family tragedy in the past, two sisters return home to settle their dead mother's affairs. In a nearby forest, they come upon a strange device. The husband of one of the women becomes obsessed with it despite their demands that he get rid of it. After they experience nightmares and other phenomena, they come to believe it may be the result of the device. It is eventually revealed to be of alien origin, and the sisters are abducted. == Cast == * Angela DiMarco as Abby * David S. Hogan as Calvin * Kate Alden as Rebecca == Production == Writer-director Berg pitched the concept of the film – a couple who find an object of possibly alien origin – to Ruthless Pictures after they distributed his previous film, ''[[The Invoking]]''.{{cite web|url=http://iconsoffright.com/2014/12/16/icons-of-fright-interview-with-the-device-director-jeremy-berg/|title=Icons of Fright interview With THE DEVICE Director Jeremy Berg!!|last=Smith|first=Jerry|work=Icons of Fright|date=2014-12-16|accessdate=2016-02-21}} Co-writer and producer John Portanova had wanted to write an alien abduction screenplay for a long time, but he experienced writer's block on the second act. Portanova used Berg's ideas to fill in the missing parts. Portanova's own film, ''[[Valley of the Sasquatch]]'', was due to start pre-production soon afterward, so they worked to complete production on ''The Device'' early. Berg and Portanova credited their already existing interest in alien abduction stories as making it possible to complete film's pre-production so quickly. Shooting took place in Seattle over 12 days.{{cite web|url=http://www.searchmytrash.com/cgi-bin/articlecreditsb.pl?jeremybergjohnportanova%289-14%29|title=An Interview with Jeremy Berg, Writer/Director, and John Portanova, Writer/Producer of The Device|last=Haberfelner|first=John|work=Search My Trash|date=2014-09-14|accessdate=2016-02-21}} The film's low budget necessitated a limited number of locations. They discussed using a single location but rejected that idea as too limiting.{{cite web|url=http://www.fangoria.com/new/qa-director-jeremy-berg-on-the-device/|title=Q&A: Director Jeremy Berg on "THE DEVICE"|last=Hanley|first=Ken W.|work=[[Fangoria]]|date=2014-12-17|accessdate=2016-02-21}} The alien special effects were practical. Berg said he kept the alien in the shadows both for practical reasons and to heighten tension.{{cite web|url=http://www.starburstmagazine.com/features/interviews/11363-jeremy-berg-interview|title=Jeremy Berg {{!}} THE DEVICE|last=Pollard|first=Andrew|work=[[Starburst (magazine)|Starburst]]|accessdate=2016-02-21}} == Release == ''The Device'' premiered on September 27, 2014, at the Local Sightings Film Festival. [[Image Entertainment]] released it on DVD in the US on December 16, 2014,{{cite web|url=http://twitchfilm.com/2014/11/the-device-first-trailer-for-sci-fi-thriller.html|title=THE DEVICE: First Trailer For Sci-Fi Thriller|last=Mack|first=Andrew|work=[[Twitch Film]]|date=2014-11-03|accessdate=2016-02-21}} and in the UK on March 23, 2015.{{cite web|url=http://www.brutalashell.com/2015/02/trailer-for-indie-alien-abduction-horror-the-device/|title=Trailer for indie alien abduction horror The Device|last=Bussey|first=Ben|work=Brutal as Hell|date=2015-02-03|accessdate=2016-02-21}} The DVD features commentary from [[Tracy Tormé]], who wrote ''[[Fire in the Sky]]'' and ''[[Intruders (miniseries)|Intruders]]''. The filmmakers asked him if he was interested in performing a commentary for their film, as he had never been able to do one for his. == Reception == Ryan Pollard of ''[[Starburst (magazine)|Starburst]]'' rated it 4/10 stars and, while praising the focus on characters instead of aliens, said the film is slow, cliched, and has a poor payoff.{{cite web|url=http://www.starburstmagazine.com/reviews/dvd-and-blu-ray-home-entertainment-reviews/11005-the-device|title=The Device|last=Pollard|first=Ryan|work=[[Starburst (magazine)|Starburst]]|accessdate=2016-02-21}} Howard Gorman of ''Scream'' magazine rated it 3.5/5 stars and wrote, "If a calculatingly creepy and consuming horror film is what you are in need of then I highly recommend ''The Device''".{{cite web|url=http://www.screamhorrormag.com/device-film-review/|title=THE DEVICE: Film Review|last=Gorman|first=Howard|work=Scream|date=2015-02-11|accessdate=2016-02-21}} Jess Hicks of [[Bloody Disgusting]] rated it 1.5/5 stars and wrote that the film should have been a short, as too much of the film was taken up with family bickering.{{cite web|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/3326412/review-device-another-cookie-cutter-alien-movie/|title=[Review] ‘The Device’ Is Another Cookie Cutter Alien Movie|last=Hicks|first=Jess|work=[[Bloody Disgusting]]|date=2015-01-02|accessdate=2016-02-21}} Ari Drew of [[Dread Central]] rated it 3/5 stars and wrote, "''The Device'' remains a passable, if not sufficiently entertaining film for sci-fi/horror fans who can appreciate a subdued approach to the abduction tale."{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/reviews/144881/device-the-dvd/|title=Device, The (DVD)|last=Drew|first=Ari|work=[[Dread Central]]|date=2016-02-16|accessdate=2016-02-21}} Mark L.

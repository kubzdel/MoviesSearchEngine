The movie had its world premiere on July 5, 2013 at the [[Neuchâtel International Fantastic Film Festival]], where it won a special mention for best feature film.{{cite web|title=Le film suisse "Chimères" primé au festival portugais Fantasporto|url=http://www.rts.ch/info/culture/5676788-le-film-suisse-chimeres-prime-au-festival-portugais-fantasporto.html|publisher=RTS|accessdate=31 May 2014}} It stars Yannick Rosset as a young man that ends up contracting [[vampirism]] after receiving a blood transfusion. ==Synopsis== Alex (Yannick Rosset) and Livia (Jasna Kohoutova) are a young couple on vacation in [[Romania]]. Their trip is uneventful until Alex severely injures himself while drunk and requires a trip to the hospital. His injuries necessitates a blood transfusion and Alex initially seems like he will be fine. However, when he and Livia return home, Alex begins to develop strange new symptoms. He has an all new aversion to light, he is unable to eat anything, and develops insomnia. Alex and Livia initially believe this to be brain damage, but they soon discover the reality of the situation: that Alex is turning into a vampire. ==Cast== *[[Jasna Kohoutova]] as Livia *[[Yannick Rosset]] as Alex *[[Catriona MacColl]] as Michelle *[[Ruggero Deodato]] as Butcher *[[Paulo Dos Santos (actor)|Paulo Dos Santos]] as Fred *[[Derek Robin]] as Doctor ==Production== Beguin had initially planned for his first feature film to be a "mix of thriller and road-movie" but found that the concept would be more expensive than he'd expected.{{cite web|last=Decker|first=Sean|title=Screamfest LA 2013 Exclusive: Olivier Beguin Talks US Premiere of Chimères; See the Trailer|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/71094/screamfest-la-2013-exclusive-olivier-beguin-talks-us-premiere-chim%C3%A8res-see-trailer#axzz33GR5kgEK|publisher=Dread Central|accessdate=31 May 2014}} He chose to instead direct a monster movie that centered around vampirism, as it would fit both his budget and the type of story he wanted to tell. The movie's plot was originally plotted out as a horror comedy, but Beguin soon found that it worked well as a love story. Rosset was intrigued by how his character was written, as Alex sees himself as violent and beastly when he looks in the mirror "but in reality he’s just sick, he’s simply tired, injured". ==Reception== ''[[Fangoria]]'' and ''[[Twitch Film]]'' both gave ''Chimères'' positive reviews,{{cite web|last=Gomez Jr.|first=Norberto|title="CHIMÈRES" (Movie Review)|url=http://www.fangoria.com/new/chimeres-movie-review/|publisher=Fangoria|accessdate=31 May 2014}} and ''Twitch Film'' praised it for "really [knowing] how to play to its audience. Especially if said audience is seeing it at the late show, beer in hand and thirsty for blood."{{cite web|last=Croll|first=Ben|title=Neuchatel 2013 Review: CHIMÈRES Heralds a New Talent in Swiss Horror|url=http://twitchfilm.com/2013/07/nifff-2013-review-chimeres-heralds-a-new-talent-in-swiss-horror.html|publisher=Twitch Film|accessdate=31 May 2014}} In contrast, ''[[Dread Central]]'' rated the film at two and a half blades, stating that it was a "respectable effort at doing something different with the genre, but those looking for a much more well-rounded and confident approach would be better served checking out Scott Leberecht's excellent ''[[Midnight Son]]'' instead."{{cite web|last=Jones|first=Gareth|title=Chimeres (review)|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/reviews/chimeres-2013#axzz33GR5kgEK|publisher=Dread Central|accessdate=31 May 2014}} ===Awards=== *''Special Mention for Best Feature Film'' at the [[Neuchâtel International Fantastic Film Festival]] (2013, won){{cite web|last=Rosser|first=Michael|title=Dark Touch wins at Neuchâtel|url=http://www.screendaily.com/news/dark-touch-wins-at-neuchtel/5058302.article|publisher=Screen Daily|accessdate=31 May 2014}} *''Narcisse Award for Best Feature Film'' at the Neuchâtel International Fantastic Film Festival (2013, nominated) *''Grand Prize of European Fantasy Film in Silver'' at the [[Sitges -

Waller * [[Elijah Wood]] }} | writer = {{plainlist| * Craig Macneill * Clay McLeod Chapman }} | starring = {{plainlist| * [[Jared Breeze]] * [[Rainn Wilson]] * [[David Morse (actor)|David Morse]] }} | music = [[Hauschka]] | cinematography = Noah Greenberg | editing = Craig Macneill | studio = {{plainlist| * [[SpectreVision]] * [[Chiller Films]] }} | distributor = Chiller Films | released = {{Film date|2015|03|14|[[SXSW]]|2015|08|14|US}} | runtime = 105 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''The Boy''''' is a 2015 American [[Horror (genre)|horror]] film directed by [[Craig Macneill]], written by Macneill and Clay McLeod Chapman, and starring [[Jared Breeze]], [[David Morse (actor)|David Morse]], and [[Rainn Wilson]]. It was based on a short film by Macneill and Chapman, ''Henley'', which was in turn loosely inspired by a novel written by Chapman, ''Miss Corpus''. Breeze plays the titular boy, a budding [[serial killer]]. == Synopsis == {{More plot|date=January 2016}} Ted Henley ([[Jared Breeze]]) and his father ([[David Morse (actor)|David Morse]]) operate a floundering family business—The Mt. Vista Motel. It’s just the two of them since Ted’s mother ran off with a guest years earlier, stealing the motel’s soul and one of its last regular patrons. Since then, Ted’s father has drifted into despondency—becoming a living ghost, haunting his own motel and leaving Ted to fend for himself. In his isolation, unchecked by the bounds of parenting, friendship, or affection, Ted's fascination with death awakens with destructive consequences.http://schedule.sxsw.com/2015/events/event_FS17770 == Cast == * [[Jared Breeze]] as Ted Henley * [[David Morse (actor)|David Morse]] as John Henley * [[Rainn Wilson]] as William Colby * [[Bill Sage]] as the Sheriff * [[Mike Vogel]] * [[Zuleikha Robinson]] * Aiden Lovekamp as Ben * David Valencia as Marcus == Production == The story has its genesis in a book written by Clay McLeod Chapman, ''Miss Corpus''.{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/51868/the-boy-doles-out-more-casting-news/|title=The Boy Doles Out More Casting News|last=Barton|first=Steve|work=[[Dread Central]]|date=2014-02-26|accessdate=2015-04-12}} Chapman said that few people read it except for Craig William Macneill, the film's co-writer and director. Fond of one chapter in particular, Macneill suggested they expand it to a short film, which eventually screened at the [[Sundance Film Festival]]. [[SpectreVision]] took an interest in the short, and it was expanded to feature length. A full trilogy is planned in which the lead character's progress will be examined at the ages of 9, 13, and 18. The trilogy is not based on the book and is original material.{{cite web|url=http://www.aintitcool.com/node/70811|title=SXSW '15: Vinyard questions director Craig William Macneill and writer Clay McLeod Chapman about THE BOY!|work=[[Ain't It Cool News]]|date=2015-03-22|accessdate=2015-04-12}} Macneill expects the films to be released in quick succession.{{cite web|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2015/03/03/sxsw-director-craig-william-macneill-talks-about-making-maniac-horror-thriller-0|title=SXSW: Director Craig William Macneill talks about making a maniac in horror-thriller The Boy|last=Collis|first=Clark|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=2015-03-03|accessdate=2015-04-12}} Filming began on February 17, 2014, in [[Medellin, Colombia]].{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/rainn-wilson-mike-vogel-joining-683854|title=Rainn Wilson, Mike Vogel Joining Horror Pic 'The Boy'|last=Kit|first=Borys|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|date=2014-02-26|accessdate=2015-04-12}} Colombia was chosen due to its tax incentives and the fact that they could afford to build their own set – something not possible in the United States for their budget. It took six weeks to construct. Composer [[Hauschka]] was announced to have joined in April 2014.{{cite web|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/videos/3290760/serial-killer-trilogy-boy-lands-composer-hauschka/|title=Serial Killer Trilogy 'The Boy' Lands Composer Hauschka|last=Barkan|first=Jonathan|work=[[Bloody Disgusting]]|date=2014-04-25|accessdate=2015-04-12}} Macneill said that the themes of the film include "the dangerous blend of isolation, neglect and youth".

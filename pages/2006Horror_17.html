''Automaton Transfusion'' was shot on location in Orlando, [[Florida]] on an estimated budget of $30,000.{{cite web|url=http://abcnews.go.com/WNT/story?id=2611799&page=1 |title=Evolution of Scary Movies |last=Rooney |first=Brian |work=[[ABC News]] |date=2006-10-27 |accessdate=2011-08-22 |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/617rX6x73?url=http://abcnews.go.com/WNT/story?id%3D2611799%26page%3D1 |archivedate=2011-08-22 |deadurl=yes }} It is intended to be the first of a trilogy of horror films and emulates classic examples of the genre. The film was purchased and released by [[Dimension Extreme]], the straight-to-DVD genre arm of The Weinstein Company. ==Plot== In the early 1970s, while the majority of Americans were focused on [[Vietnam War|events in Vietnam]], the United States Army was secretly developing a way to resurrect and control dead bodies. Their intention was to have the dead fight instead of the living, but the experiments were shut down when the [[Zombie|reanimated corpses]] were unable to control their hunger for human flesh. Thirty years later, the army decides to reopen the project. [[Grover City]], because of its remote location, would be the home of their main testing facilities. Without warning, the Grover City experiments go horribly wrong and the reanimated corpses go on a rampage, eating everyone in sight. With the town overtaken by zombies, a group of High School seniors take it upon themselves to fight back and find a cure for the disease. ==Reception== [[Rotten Tomatoes]], a [[review aggregator]], reports that 50% of six surveyed critics gave the film a positive review; the average rating was 5.3/10.{{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/automaton_transfusion/|title=Automaton Transfusion (2006)|work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|accessdate=2015-02-26}} Brad Miska of [[Bloody Disgusting]] praised the film, giving it a 3½ out of 5, and closing his review with, "Even with its low budget flaws, ''Automaton Transfusion'' is the [[Holy Grail]] of true [[Independent film|independent]] horror films. After sifting through buckets and buckets of pure sh-t, it's such a relief to finally get that slice of pie I've been longing for. [[Steven C. Miller]] will be a household name by the end of 2007, you just watch, ''Automaton Transfusion'' is only the beginning". {{cite web|url = http://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/110257/automaton-transfusion-v-2/|title = ''Automaton Transfusion'' (V)|last = Miska|first = Brad|work = [[Bloody Disgusting]]|date = 11 September 2006|accessdate = 25 December 2014}} In a review for [[DVD Verdict]], Gordon Sullivan commended the gore and the ingenuity of the filmmakers considering their lack of money and time, but also criticized many aspects (such as the pacing, picture quality, and plot) of the film, and stated, "It feels like a video game: get the chainsaw power-up, go to the school gym, kill the zombies, get the serum. In a video game, this might be fun, but these characters aren't particularly well-drawn, so following them around a poorly-established town isn't very enjoyable". {{cite web|url = http://www.dvdverdict.com/reviews/automatontransfusion.php|title = ''Automaton Transfusion''|last = Sullivan|first = Gordon|work = [[DVD Verdict]]|date = 4 March 2008|accessdate = 25 December 2014}} [[Dread Central]]'s Steve Barton awarded a 2½ out of 5, and said, "''Automaton Transfusion'' does a few things right, but sadly it gets most things wrong" and "I can't help but feel a bit queasy as the scent of missed opportunity permeates the air. I wanted to love this movie. I wanted this to be the next big thing.

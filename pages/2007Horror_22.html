It was scheduled for [[direct-to-video]] release on September 1, 2007. ==Plot== The film retells the haunting legend about the [[Bell Witch]] of [[Adams, Tennessee]],{{cite journal |work=The Journal of American Folklore |title=The Bell Witch of Tennessee and Mississippi: A Folk Legend |author=McCarter, Pete Kyle |volume=47 |issue=183 |year=1934 |pages=45-63 |publisher=American Folklore Society |location=Bloomington, IN}}{{cite web |author=University of Tennessee, Institute of Agriculture |title=The Bell Witch |publisher=University of Tennessee, Institute of Agriculture |date=1 October 2012 |location=Knoxville, TN |url=https://ag.tennessee.edu/news/VideoReleases/Pages/1210_bellwitch.aspx |accessdate=5 August 2016}}{{cite book |last=Kazek |first=Kelly |title=Forgotten Tales of Tennessee |pages=139-141 |publisher=History Press |location=Charleston, SC |year=2011 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=SNOzoFJNwX4C&pg=PA139&lpg=PA139&dq=%22townsend%22+%22+tennessee%22+%22bell+witch%22+movie&source=bl&ots=LaNnZgfHvC&sig=LCTL24eCGm3hEbFm_FFwq_Fi4P8&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwj3lpnxtarOAhXFTSYKHX8dAhkQ6AEISjAI#v=onepage&q=%22bell%20witch%22&f=false |accessdate=5 August 2016}}{{cite web |publisher=Fitzhugh |last=Fitzhugh |first=Pat |title=The Bell Witch Haunting |work=The Bell Witch Web Site |year=2013 |url=http://www.bellwitch.org/story.htm |accessdate=5 August 2016}} a historically documented [[haunting]] that took place in the early 19th century. The film is described as a love story turned [[tragedy|tragic]] when entangled with the legendary haunting of the Bell Witch. After stumbling across an ancient burial (in what is now known as the Bell Witch cave), brothers John Jr. and Williams Bell bring a strange curse home to their family causing their father, John, and sister, Betsy, to experience phantom attacks in the night and strange visions during the day. ==Filming details== ''Bell Witch: The Movie'' was shot in [[Townsend, Tennessee]] in September, 2002.

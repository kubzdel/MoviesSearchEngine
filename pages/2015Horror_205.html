It was released on video by [[Anchor Bay Entertainment]] on February 3, 2015.{{cite web |url=https://www.socialpaintball.com/2015/01/19/paintball-movie-zombie-killers-elephants-graveyard-2015-exclusive-trailer/ |title=Paintball Movie “Zombie Killers: Elephant’s Graveyard” (2015) Exclusive Trailer |website=Social Paintball |access-date=April 14, 2017}} ==Plot summary== There are only a few of the living left in the zombie infested planet. In the town of Elwood, young people are trained by an ex-soldier to fight zombies with [[paintball]]s. When Elwood is running out of provisions, they must battle through the zombies to get more food and paintballs. ==Production== The film went into post-production in 2013. Its special effects were created by Toby Sells, who has also done work for the television series ''[[The Walking Dead (TV series)|The Walking Dead]]''. The writer and director B. Harrison Smith said of the production, "The goal was to integrate the paintball aspect organically, so that it made sense".{{cite web |url=http://www.fangoria.com/new/paintball-and-ghouls-combine-in-zombie-killers-elephants-graveyard/ |title=Paintball and Ghouls Combine in "Zombie Killers: Elephant's Graveyard" |last=Alexander |first=Chris |date=October 15, 2013 |website=Fangoria |access-date=April 14, 2017}} ==Release== It was released unrated and direct to video on Blu-ray and DVD. The video release includes three special features—a video from the [[YouTube]] channel Bloodbath & Beyond with the users on the site of the film, a look at the film's productions, and a behind the scenes look. None of the special features are in [[High-definition video|high definition]]. It has a 1080p HD transfer that has a 2.35:1 aspect ratio and a Dolby TrueHD 5.1 audio mix. The subtitles are in English and Spanish.{{cite web |url=http://bluray.highdefdigest.com/16449/zombiekillers.html |title=Zombie Killers: Elephant's Graveyard |last=Kluger |first=Bryan |date=May 5, 2015 |website=High-Def Digest |access-date=April 14, 2017}} The film had a showing at the [[USC School of Cinematic Arts]].{{cite web |url=http://cinema.usc.edu/events/event.cfm?id=14749 |title=Zombie Killers: Elephant's Graveyard |website=USC School of Cinematic Arts |access-date=April 14, 2017}} ==Reception== Staci Layne Wilson, writing for [[Dread Central]], praised the acting and the effects, but said that "the lackluster plot and shaky-cam cinematography are bound to have more viewers diving for the fast-forward button early on, skipping to the end and not missing a thing in-between".{{cite web |url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/reviews/87602/zombie-killers-elephants-graveyard-blu-ray-dvd/ |title=Zombie Killers: Elephant’s Graveyard (Blu-ray / DVD) |last=Layne Wilson |first=Stacy |date=February 2, 2015 |website=Dread Central |access-date=April 14, 2017}} Martin Liebman, of Blu-ray.com, wrote, " Zombie Killers: Elephant's Graveyard is a film that tries too hard and ends up lacking the tighter cohesion necessary to tell a better, richer story".{{cite web |url=http://www.blu-ray.com/movies/Zombie-Killers-Elephants-Graveyard-Blu-ray/121349/#Review |title= Zombie Killers: Elephant's Graveyard Blu-ray |last=Liebman |first=Martin |date=January 31, 2015 |website=Blu-ray.com |access-date=April 14, 2017}} High-Def Digest's Bryan Kluger wrote, "'Zombie Killers: Elephant's Graveyard' lacks any real sense of direction, horror, and gore.

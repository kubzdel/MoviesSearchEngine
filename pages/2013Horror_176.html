Fullerton | editing = Scott Lewis | studio = ProtoMedia | distributor = WildEye Releasing | released = {{Film date|2013|1|6}} | runtime = 85 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Scream Park''''' is a 2013 horror film by American director Cary Hill and his [[directorial debut]].{{cite web|last=Zimmerman|first=Samuel|title=DVD, VODetails: Doug Bradley Slasher, “SCREAM PARK”|url=http://www.fangoria.com/new/dvd-vodetails-doug-bradley-slasher-scream-park/|publisher=Fangoria|accessdate=27 April 2014}} The film had its world release on January 6, 2013 in [[Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania]] and was partially funded through a successful ''[[Kickstarter]]'' campaign.{{cite web|title=Doug Bradley and Nivek Ogre Featured in Kickstarter Success Story Scream Park|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/56507/doug-bradley-and-nivek-ogre-featured-kickstarter-success-story-scream-park#axzz305Le2q9j|publisher=Dread Central|accessdate=27 April 2014}} Of the film, Hill stated that he was inspired to create ''Scream Park'' after visiting a theme park and thinking that it would be a good location for an 80's themed horror film. Filming took place at [[Conneaut Lake Park]] in [[Conneaut Lake, Pennsylvania]] and at the [[University of Pittsburgh]].{{cite web|last=Richards|first=Dave|title=Horror film shot at Conneaut Lake Park screens Friday|url=http://www.goerie.com/horror-film-shot-at-conneaut-lake-park-screens-friday|publisher=Go Erie.com|accessdate=27 April 2014}} ==Synopsis== Jennifer (Wendy Wygant) works at Fright Land, a theme park that's closing after years of declining sales and lack of interest from the general public. While initially reluctant, Jennifer agrees to attend a party held by the other park employees to memorialize the park's closing. She can't help but feel that something feels off about the night's events and her suspicions are soon proven to be correct, as park owner Mr. Hyde ([[Doug Bradley]]) has decided that the only way to save the park is to hire two men to murder all of the park's employees. He theorizes that after the killers complete their task, morbid murder fans will pay large amounts of money to visit an amusement park themed around the murders. ==Cast== *[[Wendy Wygant]] as Jennifer *[[Steve Rudzinski]] as Marty *[[Nivek Ogre]] as Iggy *[[Alicia Marie Marcucci]] as Allison *[[Nicole Beattie]] as Missi *[[Doug Bradley]] as Mr. Hyde *[[Kailey Marie Harris]] as Carlee *[[Dean Jacobs]] as Tony *[[Tyler Kale]] as Rhodie *[[Ian Lemmon]] as Ogre *[[Carrie Lee Martz]] as Attendant *[[Kyle Riordan]] as Roy ==Reception== Critical reception for ''Scream Park'' has been positive, and ''[[Bloody Disgusting]]'' commented that the movie had "a lot to enjoy".{{cite web|last=Cooper|first=Patrick|title=Amusement Park Thriller ‘Scream Park’ Is Worth the Price of Admission!|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/3286132/bd-review-amusement-park-thriller-scream-park-is-worth-the-price-of-admission/|publisher=Bloody Disgusting|accessdate=27 April 2014}} ''[[DVD Talk]]'' noted that the movie was low budget and had some shortcomings, but that the acting was good and that fans of similarly low budget films would greatly enjoy the movie.{{cite web|last=Biltz|first=Jeremy|title=Review: Scream Park|url=http://www.dvdtalk.com/reviews/64396/scream-park/|publisher=DVD Talk|accessdate=27 April 2014}} ''[[Ain't It Cool News]]'' stated that ''Scream Park'' felt familiar to similar films in the genre, but that it "does the familiar really, really well."{{cite web|title=AICN HORROR looks at BIG BAD WOLVES!

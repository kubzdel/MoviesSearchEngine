It stars [[Kim Kkot-bi]]. It is the debut film by Kayoko Asakura and had its premiere at the [[Yubari Film Festival]] in 2013.{{cite web|url=http://www.filmbiz.asia/reviews/its-a-beautiful-day|work=[[Film Business Asia]]|title=It's a Beautiful Day|accessdate=June 25, 2013|date=June 15, 2013|author=Elley, Derek}} The film is set in [[California]], where A-jung (Kim Kkot-bi) is picked up by her Japanese friend Takako ([[Amagi Chika]]). They visit Takako's friends who treat A-jung poorly. That night around a campfire, mysterious supernatural events begin to happen involving Takako's friends. ==Release== ''It's a Beautiful Day'' premiered at the Yubari Film Festival on February 22, 2013. It received theatrical release in Japan on June 8, 2013. ==Reception== Derek Elley of [[Film Business Asia]] gave the film a seven out of ten rating, noting that the film is "restrained, with no fountains of blood and few flying bodyparts" and that "the film deliriously pushes the borders of the genre" ==Notes== {{Reflist}} ==External links== * {{IMDb title|2739358|It's a Beautiful Day}} [[Category:2013 horror films]] [[Category:Japanese horror films]] [[Category:Japanese films]] [[Category:American horror films]] [[Category:American films]] [[Category:2013 films]] [[Category:Blood in fiction]] {{2010s-horror-film-stub}}

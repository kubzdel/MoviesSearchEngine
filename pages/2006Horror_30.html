Despite featuring [[Bram Stoker]]'s name in the title, the film is not directly based on any of his writings or a [[mockbuster]] to the [[1992 in film|1992]] film ''[[Bram Stoker's Dracula]]'', but shares similarities to films such as ''[[Blade: Trinity]]'', ''[[Dracula 2000]]'', ''[[Underworld: Evolution]]'' and ''[[Van Helsing (film)|Van Helsing]]''. The film also shares some similarities with the 1971 [[Hammer Film Productions|Hammer horror]] film ''[[Countess Dracula]]'', which also features a Dracula-esque [[femme fatale]] in the lead role. == Plot == The film takes place in an unidentified city (presumably [[New York City]]) in the modern day, and follows the characters of Rufus King ([[Thomas Downey (actor)|Thomas Downey]]) and [[Abraham Van Helsing|Jacob Van Helsing]] ([[Rhett Giles]]), both of whom have been observing recent attacks made upon young teenagers in the city at night. Van Helsing correctly identifies that the attacks are being made by a group of vampires residing in the city. The vampires are led by a foreign seductress named [[Countess Elizabeth Báthory|Countess Bathorly]] ([[Christina Rosenberg]]), who hopes to use the humans to feed her growing vampire clan and to eventually seize control of the city, while at the same time using her growing power to gain the powers of [[Count Dracula|"the Master"]]. Discovering Bathorly's plan, Van Helsing and King begin to hunt down and destroy the vampires one by one, until they finally face the Countess herself and try to kill her once and for all, before her evil consumes the city and allows Dracula's curse to consume the human race. ==Reception== Critical reaction to ''Dracula's Curse'' has been mixed to positive.

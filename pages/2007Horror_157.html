It has the distinction of being the first [[lucha film]] starring any of the "Big 3" ([[El Santo|Santo]], [[Blue Demon]], Mil Máscaras) to be produced in English.{{cite web|url=http://www.mmvsam.com/about.shtml|title=AboutFilm}} It was a tribute to the 1960s Mexican horror and lucha libre films and was the first of a trilogy of Mil Mascaras films that includes [[Academy of Doom]] (2008) and [[Aztec Revenge]] (2015). ==Plot== An [[Aztec mummy]] is resurrected in a ceremony in which the blood of a human sacrifice is dripped onto the mummified remains. The mummy possesses a scepter with a jewel that can be used to control people’s minds for purposes of world conquest. Mil Máscaras learns of the mummy’s plans and is determined to thwart him. When the scepter fails to do so, the mummy attempts to control Mil's mind by exploiting the hallucinogenic effects of Aztec magic mushrooms and the allure of identical twin seductresses. Mil tracks the mummy to an abandoned monastery but is captured. After escaping with the help of the Professor, Mil is joined by a team of luchadores in a battle against the mummy's warriors. Mil eventually defeats the mummy and entombs him forever. ==Cameos== The film has appearances by some of the most famous wrestlers/luchadores in the world, including [[El Hijo del Santo]] (the son of [[El Santo|Santo]], both in the ring and in real life), [[Blue Demon, Jr.]], [[Harley Race]], [[Dos Caras]] (Mil Máscaras' real-life brother), [[Huracán Ramírez|Huracán Ramírez, Jr.]], Neutron, and others.{{cite journal|title=Screem Magazine|pages=6–9|issue=23|year=2012}} Veteran Hollywood actress [[P. J. Soles]] also makes a brief appearance, and [[Richard Lynch (actor)|Richard Lynch]] plays the President of the United States.{{cite journal|title=Filmfax|pages=48–52|issue=1|year=2012}} ==Distribution== In 2009 the film was released theatrically in the US by Monogram Releasing[https://web.archive.org/web/20170104181616/http://www.bmovienation.com/?p=700] and often screened with [[Academy of Doom]] and the short film [[Susan's Big Day]][https://web.archive.org/web/20160703141731/http://www.mmvsam.com/thrillville.htm]. It was released on DVD in 2012[https://web.archive.org/web/20160216092833/http://www.amazon.com/Mil-Mascaras-vs-Aztec-Mummy/product-reviews/B003L3OQ4O]. ==Critical response== The film screened at festivals around the world garnering awards and award nominations along with very positive critical reviews.{{cite web|url=http://www.mmvsam.com/press.shtml|title=Reviews|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080724022611/http://mmvsam.com/press.shtml|archivedate=2008-07-24|df=}} In particular, a review from the Cine Fantastico festival in [[Estepona, Spain]], suggested that the film may be the best ever made in the traditionally Mexican lucha genre despite being produced in the United States.{{cite web|url=http://www.viruete.com/2008/09/festival_de_cine_fantastico_de.html|title=Cine Fantastico de Estepona|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090106151144/http://www.viruete.com/2008/09/festival_de_cine_fantastico_de.html|archivedate=2009-01-06|df=}} Reviews from major film critics were similarly positive with [[PopMatters]]' Bill Gibron calling it "amazing movie entertainment... rivals any super hero film made in the mainstream"{{cite web|url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/post/mil-mascaras-resurrection|title=PopMatters}} and [[M. J.

Mageswaran | cinematography = Veeramani | studio = White Screen Entertainment | distributor = | released = 27 May 2016 | runtime = 110 minutes | country = [[India]] | language = [[Tamil language|Tamil]] | editing = Shyjith Kumaran }} '''''Meera Jaakirathai''''' ({{lang-en|Beware of Meera}}) is a 2016 [[Tamil language|Tamil-language]] horror film, directed by R. G. Kesvaan. The film stars an ensemble cast featuring Surendar, [[Monica (actress)|Monica]] and amongst others. Music for the film was composed by the Raj and the film opened to mixed reviews in May 2016, having been in production for six years. ==Cast== *Surendar * [[Monica (actress)|Monica]] * [[Bobby Simha]] * [[Swaminathan]] * Sri Priya * Santhosh * Joy * Sanjay * Ramalingam ==Production== In 2010, producer Kesavan decided to finance a film directed by Sathish, a contestant from the reality show Naalaiya Iyakkunar. Sathish subsequently selected his friend, [[Bobby Simha]], then a small-time actor, to portray a negative role in the film. The shoot for the film was primarily completed in a week-long schedule at Tiruchengode. During the making of the film, Sathish and Bobby often disagreed with Kesavan and after an alcohol-fuelled incident, Kesavan reported Sathish to the police, following which the director left the project.{{cite web|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/tamil/movies/news/Bobby-Simhaa-denies-involvement-in-upcoming-film-director-says-otherwise/articleshow/52290608.cms|title=Bobby Simhaa denies involvement in upcoming film; director says otherwise - Times of India|publisher=}} In May 2016, the production team suddenly started promoting the film through posters in newspapers after having completed the film with extra scenes involving actress [[Monica (actress)|Monica]]. After finding the promotional material, Bobby Simha complained to the [[Nadigar Sangam]] claiming that he did not recall starring in such a film and that the producers had used pictures from his previous film, ''[[Urumeen]]'' (2015). He had previously made similar claims against the makers of another film, ''[[Chennai Ungalai Anbudan Varaverkirathu]]'' (2015).{{cite web|url=http://www.behindwoods.com/tamil-movies-cinema-news-16/bobby-simha-complains-to-nadigar-sangam-regarding-meera-jaakirathai-issue.html|title=Bobby Simha complains to Nadigar Sangam regarding Meera Jaakirathai issue|date=15 May 2016|publisher=}} ==Release== The film opened in May 2016 to negative reviews from critics and had a low profile release across Tamil Nadu.{{cite web|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/tamil/movie-reviews/Meera-Jakirathai/movie-review/52498868.cms|title=Meera Jakirathai Movie Review, Trailer, & Show timings at Times of India|website=The Times of India}} A critic from the New Indian Express stated what it is "appreciable is the effort the first-time director has put in", labelling it was a "horror-thriller crafted in the conventional way".{{cite web|url=http://www.newindianexpress.com/entertainment/tamil/Story-of-a-woman-wronged-and-vendetta/2016/05/29/article3455947.ece#ifrndnloc|title=Story of a woman wronged and vendetta|publisher=}} ==References== {{reflist}} [[Category:2016 films]] [[Category:Indian films]] [[Category:Tamil-language films]] [[Category:2010s Tamil-language films]] [[Category:2016 horror films]] [[Category:Indian horror films]] [[Category:Directorial debut films]] {{2010s-Tamil-film-stub}}

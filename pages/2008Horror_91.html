[[Anchor Bay Entertainment]] ([[Canada]]) | released = July 10, 2008 | runtime = 76 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = | preceded_by = }} '''''Home Movie''''' is a 2008 [[horror film]] and is the [[film director|directorial]] debut of [[film actor|actor]] [[Christopher Denham]].{{cite web|url=http://www.horror-asylum.com/news/article.asp?item=6731|title=IFC Entertainment Acquire 'Home Movie', Horror Movie Entertainment News and Reviews|first=Steven|last=Davies|website=Horror-asylum.com|accessdate=17 September 2017}}{{cite web |url=http://torontoafterdark.com/2008/program/feature-films/home-movie/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2008-12-31 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081121115821/http://torontoafterdark.com/2008/program/feature-films/home-movie/ |archivedate=2008-11-21 |df= }} The film received favorable reviews at [[Montreal, Canada|Montreal’s]] 2008 [[Fantasia Festival|Fantasia Film Festival]]. Following the final screening, bids were made on the film and [[IFC Films|IFC Entertainment]] acquired the [[United States|U.S.]] rights for [[IFC Films|IFC's]] Festival Direct [[Video On Demand]] and [[DVD]] rights Nationwide. The film stars [[Adrian Pasdar]], [[Cady McClain]], Amber Joy Williams, and [[Austin Williams]].{{cite web |url=http://www.bloody-disgusting.com/film/1685 |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2008-12-31 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081120073842/http://www.bloody-disgusting.com/film/1685 |archivedate=2008-11-20 |df= }} ==Plot== ''Home Movie'' documents one family's descent into darkness through compilation of the Poe family's home-made video footage. In the remote woods of upstate [[New York (state)|New York]], David ([[Adrian Pasdar]]) and Clare ([[Cady McClain]]) Poe are attempting to live an idyllic life. However, the Poe children are hiding a dark secret and something is wrong with ten-year-old twins, Jack ([[Austin Williams]]) and Emily (Amber Joy Williams) Poe. To stop them, their parents must enter the nightmare of their children’s minds. As they try to regain control of the children’s lives, the pressing question becomes who will ultimately survive the battle.{{cite web|url=http://www.fangoria.com/reviews/2-film/4298-home-movie-and-the-butcher-film-reviews.html|title=HOME MOVIE and THE BUTCHER (Film Reviews)|website=Fangoria.com|accessdate=17 September 2017}} ==Soundtrack== The music was composed by [[Ryan Shore]]. ==Reception== Dr. Nathan of ''Quiet Earth'' said, "This truly disturbing movie, written and directed by Christopher Denham, is thus far the only TAD premiere Dr Nathan has experienced which actually, really gave me that deep-down visceral sense of dread and foreboding."[[Quiet Earth]], by Dr. Nathan (October 24, 2008), [http://www.quietearth.us/articles/2008/10/24/TAD-08-Review-of-Christopher-Denhams-HOME-MOVIE "TAD 08: Review of Christopher Denham's HOME MOVIE"], Quietearth.us, accessed 12-30-2008 From Dread Central: "There’s something about home videos that brings out the worst in people.

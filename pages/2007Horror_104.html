Scott Smith]] | distributor = | released = {{Film date|2007|12|11}} | runtime = 90 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Furnace''''' is a 2007 [[horror film]] written and directed by [[William Butler (actor)|William Butler]]. It stars [[Danny Trejo]], [[Michael Paré]], [[Tom Sizemore]], [[Ja Rule]], and [[Paul Wall]]. ==Plot== {{expand section|date=August 2014}} A group of prisoners are assigned to assist in the re-opening of an old prison's closed wing. They find that the furnace room in the wing is the location of a murder, and the victim's spirit seeks death of those who enter the room. ==Cast== * [[Michael Paré]] as Detective Michael Turner * [[Ja Rule]] as Terrence Dufresne * [[Jenny McShane]] as Dr. Ashley Carter * [[Danny Trejo]] as Fury * [[Tom Sizemore]] as Frank Miller * [[Kelly Stables]] as Karen Bolding * [[Paul Wall]] as Joey Robbins ==Production== The original script for ''Furnace'' was written by [[Anghus Houvouras]], and the project was brought up to director [[William Butler (actor)|William Butler]], who thought that the story was reminiscent of the 1988 film ''[[Prison (1988 film)|Prison]]''. Butler signed to direct, and he and his writing partner [[Aaron Strongoni]] collaborated to polish the script to meet Melee Entertainment's budgetary concerns. The rewriting lasted six months and changed the story substantially. Butler said of the change, "This of course was no reflection on Anghus, but instead the team of people at Melee that sort of took the film in another direction."{{cite news | author=Phil Davies Brown | url=http://www.horror-asylum.com/interview/williambutler/interview.asp | title=William Butler | publisher=Horror Asylum | date=2007-08-09 | accessdate=2007-12-16 }} ==Release== {{expand section|date=November 2014}} The film was released on DVD by Melee Studio on February 12, 2008.{{cite web|title=Furnace (2006) - Releases - AllMovie|url=http://www.allmovie.com/movie/furnace-v426193/releases|website=Allmovie.com|publisher=Allmovie|accessdate=24 November 2014}} ==Reception== {{expand section|date=November 2014}} Critical reception for the film has been mostly negative. [[Dread Central]] awarded the film a score of 2.5 / 5 stating "All in all, Furnace is just another blah horror entry to clutter the direct-to-DVD market. As if the floating head cover weren't enough on its own to dissuade you from picking it up believe me, this furnace is just blowing smoke".{{cite web|last1=Butane|first1=Johnny|title=Furnace (DVD) - Dread Central|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/reviews/6189/furnace-dvd/|website=Dread Central.com|publisher=Johnny Butane|accessdate=24 November 2014}} David Johnson from [[DVD Verdict]] gave the film a negative review criticizing the film's first half as being too slow and stated that the film would likely disappoint horror fans.{{cite web|last1=Johnson|first1=David|title=DVD Verdict Review - Furnace|url=http://www.dvdverdict.com/reviews/furnace.php|website=DVD Verdict.com|publisher=David Johnson|accessdate=24 November 2014}} ==References== {{Reflist}} ==External links== * {{Official website|http://www.furnacethemovie.com/}} * {{IMDb title|0497373|Furnace}} * {{Rotten Tomatoes|furnace|Furnace}} * {{tcmb title|647144|The Furnace}} [[Category:2007 direct-to-video films]] [[Category:2007 horror films]] [[Category:American films]] [[Category:English-language films]] [[Category:Supernatural horror films]] [[Category:Ghost films]] [[Category:Direct-to-video horror films]] [[Category:2007 films]]

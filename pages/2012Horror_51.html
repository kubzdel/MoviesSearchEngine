{{Infobox film | name = Children of Sorrow | image = File:Children of Sorrow Movie Poster.jpg | image_size = | border = | alt = | caption = | film name = | director = Jourdan McClure | producer = Doug Archibald | writer = Ryan Finnerty | screenplay = | story = | based on = | narrator = | starring = [[Bill Oberst Jr.]], Hannah Levien, Whitney Nielsen | music = | cinematography = Dmitry Koshutin | editing = Josh Earl | studio = [[After Dark Films]] | distributor = [[Lionsgate Home Entertainment]] | released = {{Film date|2012|10|6|Sacramento Horror Film Festival}} | runtime = 88 minutes | country = United States | language = Espanish | budget = | gross = }} '''''Children of Sorrow''''' is a 2012 [[horror film]] directed by Jourdan McClure and part of the [[After Dark Originals]] series.{{cite web|last=Miller|first=Ryan|title=Children of Sorrow added to After Dark Originals|url=http://www.joblo.com/horror-movies/news/children-of-sorrow-added-to-after-dark-originals|publisher=JoBlo|accessdate=30 January 2014}} The movie had its world release on October 6, 2012 at the Sacramento Horror Film Festival and was released to DVD on March 4, 2014 through [[Lionsgate Home Entertainment]]. The film stars [[Bill Oberst Jr.]] as a twisted cult leader.{{cite web|last=Turek|first=Ryan|title=Children of Sorrow Trailer Makes Its Debut|url=http://www.shocktillyoudrop.com/news/345713-children-of-sorrow-trailer-makes-its-debut/|publisher=STYD|accessdate=30 January 2014}} ==Synopsis== Desperate to discover what has become of her sister, Ellen (Hannah Levien) infiltrates Simon Leach's ([[Bill Oberst Jr.]]) cult. All she knows is that her sister was last seen joining his group in order to find herself, only to go missing after joining. Ellen is welcomed with open arms and although her initial intent is to search for her sister, she finds herself drawn to Simon's magnetic personality and message of love and acceptance. However what she soon discovers is that she, along with all of the cult's companionship-hungry members, are being manipulated to a more sinister and dark end than she could have predicted.{{cite web|last=Woods|first=Kevin|title=Lionsgate releases new trailer, release date for Children of Sorrow|url=http://www.joblo.com/horror-movies/news/lionsgate-releases-new-trailer-release-date-for-children-of-sorrow|publisher=JoBlo|accessdate=30 January 2014}} ==Cast== *[[Bill Oberst Jr.]] as Simon Leach *[[Hannah Levien]] as Ellen *[[Whitney Nielsen]] as Mary *[[Galen Howard]] as Alan *[[Liesel Hanson]] as Robin *[[Peabo Powell]] as Frank *[[Daniel Mentz]] as Evan *[[Jaquelyn Johnson]] as Tracey *[[Rachel Orlikoff]] as Grace *[[Martha Brigham]] as Veronica *[[Nich Kauffman]] as Ben *[[Jefferson Rogers]] as Brian ==Reception== ''[[Fearnet]]'' gave ''Children of Sorrow'' a mixed review, praising Oberst as one of the film's highlights while commenting that the film did "sag in the middle".{{cite web|last=Weinberg|first=Scott|title=FEARNET Movie Review: 'Children of Sorrow'|url=http://www.fearnet.com/news/review/fearnet-movie-review-children-sorrow|publisher=Fearnet|accessdate=5 March 2014}} ''[[Film School Rejects]]'' panned the film overall, remarking that it "reveals the ending in the first few minutes, has a nonsensical screenplay, and is edited like a narrative film thus defeating the purpose of the found footage format".{{cite web|last=Hunter|first=Rob|title=The Best Blu-ray/DVDs of the Week: ‘The Visitor,’ ’12 Years a Slave,’ and ‘The Grandmaster’|url=http://filmschoolrejects.com/features/new-bluray-dvd-releases-march-4-2014.php|publisher=Film School Rejects|accessdate=5 March 2014}} The reviewer also noted that "For what it’s worth, the director of the film claims it isn’t found-footage, so there’s that." ===Awards=== *''Best Actor Award'' at the Shockfest Film Festival (2012, won -

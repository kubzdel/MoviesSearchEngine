The film contains 26 different [[Short film|short]]s, each by different directors spanning fifteen countries, including [[Nacho Vigalondo]], [[Kaare Andrews]], [[Adam Wingard]], [[Simon Barrett (filmmaker)|Simon Barrett]], [[Banjong Pisanthanakun]], [[Ben Wheatley]], [[Lee Hardcastle]], [[Noboru Iguchi]], [[Ti West]], and [[Angela Bettis]]. It premiered at the [[Toronto International Film Festival]] in 2012. In 2013, it was released on [[Video on demand|VOD]] January 31 and in theaters March 8.{{cite web|url=http://www.shocktillyoudrop.com/news/170233-get-a-sneak-peek-of-abcs-of-death-before-vhs-this-week|title=Get a Sneak Peek of ABCs of Death Before V/H/S This Week|publisher=Shock Till You Drop|date=October 3, 2012|author=Ryan Turek|accessdate=November 9, 2012}} The end credits of the film feature Australian band [[Skyhooks (band)|Skyhooks]]' 1974 song "[[Horror Movie (song)|Horror Movie]]". A sequel, ''[[ABCs of Death 2]]'', was released in late 2014 and a second sequel, ''[[ABCs of Death 2.5]]'' in 2016.{{cite web|first=Perri |last=Nemiroff |title=‘ABCs of Death 2’ Directors List: ‘Splice’s’ Vincenzo Natali, Bill Plympton & More|year=2013|url=http://www.upcominghorrormovies.com/movie/abcs-death-2|publisher=upcominghorrormovies.com|accessdate=24 May 2014}} ==Plot== The film is divided into twenty-six individual chapters, each helmed by a different director assigned a letter of the alphabet. The directors were then given free rein in choosing a word to create a story involving death. The varieties of death range from accidents to murders.{{cite web|title=Exclusive Interview: Angela Bettis on The ABCs of Death|url=http://www.craveonline.com/film/interviews/197089-exclusive-interview-angela-bettis-on-the-abcs-of-death|publisher=Crave Online|accessdate=21 October 2012}} A contest was held for the role of the twenty-sixth director. The winner was UK-based director [[Lee Hardcastle]], who submitted the [[claymation]] short for T.{{cite web|title=Watch the Winning ‘ABCs of Death’ Short Film ‘T is for Toilet’|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/news/27239/|publisher=Bloody Disgusting|accessdate=21 October 2012}} ==Content== The movie begins showing a close up of a funnel with blood pouring ominously from it. The blood proceeds to fill up various rooms inside of a house before filling up the interior of a small room containing [[Toy block|letter blocks]]. The room floods as the blocks float to the top spelling out the title.

It stars [[Jesseca Liu]] as a grieving mother who seeks to return her dead child to life through supernatural means. It was released theatrically in Singapore on January 8, 2015, and grossed US$284,876. == Plot == Overcome with grief after the death of her son, Jia En turns to a medium, Madam Seetoh, who promises she can help return the boy's soul. == Cast == * [[Jesseca Liu]] as Jia En * Jacko Chiang * Liu Lingling as Madam Seetoh * Shawn Tan as XiaoLe * [[Timothy Law]] as Tam == Production == Writer-director Lee was attracted to the original short story, which he adapted, because of its emotional depth. Lee said casting for the film was difficult, as he had trouble finding an actress in her 30s who was both comfortable with horror and playing a mother.{{cite news|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/entertainment/bringing-back-a-dead-son|title=Bringing back a dead son|last=Chan|first=Boon|work=[[The Straits Times]]|date=2015-01-07|accessdate=2016-04-09}} Liu, who was cast as the mother, said the role was her "most challenging to date", as she had to play a range of emotions without having been a mother.{{cite news|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/entertainment/jesseca-liu-is-not-just-a-bikini-babe|title=Jesseca Liu is not just a bikini babe|last=2015-09-21|accessdate=2016-04-09}} To prepare for the emotional scenes, she thought about the loss of her cats.{{cite news|url=http://www.todayonline.com/entertainment/movies/jesseca-liu-cries-lot-alone|title=Jesseca Liu cries a lot alone|last=Yi|first=Hon Jing|work=[[Today Online]]|date=2014-08-19|accessdate=2016-04-09}} Liu cited a scene in which a disembodied hand fondles her as the most difficult in the film.

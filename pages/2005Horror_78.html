[[Ryan Godard]] | editing = [[Laurent Gorse]] | distributor = [[Box Office Entertainment]] | released = September 15, 2005 | runtime = | country = Thailand | language = Thai | budget = | preceded_by = | followed_by = }} '''''Ghost of Mae Nak''''' ({{lang-th|นาค รักแท้/วิญญาณ/ความตาย}}) is a [[2005 in film|2005]] [[Cinema of Thailand|Thai]] [[horror film]] thriller about a protecting ghost directed and written by British director [[Mark Duffield]]. The film stars Pataratida Pacharawirapong, [[Siwat Chotchaicharin]] and [[Porntip Papanai]] as the ghost. ==Synopsis== Set in modern [[Bangkok]], the life of groom Mak is disturbed, by successive nightmares with a ghost woman, [[Mae Naak|Mae Nak]], an ancient Thai legend. He meets his beloved fiancée Nak to acquire an [[antique]] [[brooch]] and an old abandoned house in [[Phra Khanong]] through an unscrupulous [[real estate]] agent Angel and they decide to buy the property. [[Image:Ghostofmae2.jpg|left|thumb|Siwat Chotchaicharin as Mak.]] After their wedding, two small-time thieves break into the house and steal their gifts and other objects. Mak happens to see the criminals on the streets of [[Bangkok]] selling his goods. He chases the burglars and they run their van over Mak, who falls into a deep [[coma]]. The ghost Mae Nak protects the young couple against Angel and the burglars, but in return she holds the soul of Mak. Nak finds the remains of Mae Nak in an ancient cemetery, and with some monks, they [[exorcism|exorcise]] Mae Nak from Mak using the ancient brooch and release her spirit. ==Cast== *[[Pataratida Patcharawirapong]] as Nak *[[Siwat Chotchaicharin]] as Mak *[[Porntip Papanai]] as [[Mae Naak|Mae Nak]] *[[Jaran Ngamdee]] as Por Mak *[[Meesak Nakarat]] as Angel ==Background== [[Image:Shrinetomaenak.jpg|thumb|left|Shrine to Mae Nak at Wat Mahabut on Sukhumvit Soi 77 in Suan Luang district, Bangkok.]] The story of [[Mae Naak|Mae Nak Phra Khanong]] is famous and a favorite among Thai people. There is a shrine dedicated to her at Wat Mahabut on [[Sukhumvit Road|Sukhumvit]] [[Soi]] 77 (On Nut) in [[Bangkok]]'s [[Suan Luang]] (formerly [[Phra Khanong]]) district. The tale has been depicted on film numerous times since the silent era, with one of the most famous being ''Mae Nak Pra Kanong'' in 1958. A 1999 version, ''[[Nang Nak]]'', by [[Nonzee Nimibutr]], gained worldwide acclaim as part of the "Thai New Wave" cinema movement.

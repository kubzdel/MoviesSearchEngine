Russian | budget = $65–80 million | gross = $40.2 million{{cite web |url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=invasion.htm |title=The Invasion (2007) |publisher=''[[Box Office Mojo]]'' |accessdate=September 21, 2007}} | image_size = 215px }} '''''The Invasion''''' is a 2007 American [[Science fiction film|science fiction]] [[horror film]] directed by [[Oliver Hirschbiegel]], with additional scenes written by [[The Wachowskis]] and directed by [[James McTeigue]], and starring [[Nicole Kidman]] and [[Daniel Craig]]. Its plot follows a [[Washington, D.C.]] psychiatrist (Kidman) who finds those around her turning into emotionless beings shortly after a major space shuttle crash. ''The Invasion'' is the fourth [[film adaptation]] of the 1955 novel ''[[The Body Snatchers]]'' by [[Jack Finney]], following [[Don Siegel]]'s 1956 film ''[[Invasion of the Body Snatchers]]'', [[Philip Kaufman]]'s 1978 remake ''[[Invasion of the Body Snatchers (1978 film)|Invasion of the Body Snatchers]]'', and [[Abel Ferrara]]'s 1993 ''[[Body Snatchers (1993 film)|Body Snatchers]].'' ==Plot== After the [[space shuttle]] ''Patriot'' crashes on Earth, a fungus-like alien lifeform is discovered on the remaining parts scattered over U.S. territory. Once people come into contact with the organism, they are controlled by it when they enter [[Rapid eye movement sleep|REM sleep]]. One of the first people infected is Tucker Kaufman, a [[Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|CDC]] director investigating the crash. Tucker's ex-wife, psychiatrist Carol Bennell, begins to feel something is amiss when people seem to have "changed". Her patient Wendy Lenk describes how her husband "is not her husband", and one of her son's friends acts detached and emotionless. At a neighborhood [[Halloween]] party, Carol's son Oliver finds a "skin" on a partier which is initially believed to be costume makeup. Carol speculates that the skin may be an organism connected to reports of a fast-spreading flu. Carol takes the organism to her doctor friend Ben Driscoll to have it checked. Carol attends a friendly dinner meeting between Russian diplomat Yorish and Czechoslovakian diplomat Belicec (along with female companion Luddie) and discusses her [[postmodern feminism]] views over caviar and champagne. Meanwhile, Tucker uses the CDC to spread the disease further, disguising the spores as flu vaccine. Ben and Dr. Stephen Galeano, a biologist, discover how the spore takes over the brain during REM sleep. During this investigation, Luddie calls Ben, worried about Yorish's behavior. Driscoll and Galeano also find that people who had brain-affecting illnesses, such as [[encephalitis]] or [[Acute disseminated encephalomyelitis|ADEM]], are immune to the spore because their previous illnesses prevents the spore from "latching on" to the brain matter. Carol's son, Oliver, is immune to the spore because of the ADEM he had as a young child. Carol decides to get her son, who might show a way to a cure, back from Tucker. Before she drives to Tucker's house, she joins Ben's team who is called to the house of the Belicecs, the Czech ambassador and his wife, in a case of emergency. There they witness the transformation of Yorish, the Russian ambassador and the Belicecs' friend. When Carol arrives at Tucker's house, he and several colleagues close in on her. He explains that the changed humans, devoid of irrational emotions, are offering a better world, and asks her to join them. When Carol resists, he holds her to the ground and infects her by spurting his saliva on her. She escapes and returns to Ben at the Belicecs' house. They flee when Belicec returns with more transformed people intent on infecting anyone in the house. Galaneo and one of his assistants head to a base outside Baltimore where they and other scientists attempt to find a cure for the alien virus. Carol and Ben separate to find Oliver, who texts his location, the apartment of Tucker's mother, to Carol. Finally, Ben arrives, but Carol realizes that he too has become one of the infected. He successfully seduces her to give in to the new society, but also frankly states that there is no room for people like Oliver who are immune. Carol shoots Ben in the leg and flees with Oliver. She is picked up by helicopter and flies to the center preparing the inoculations. In time, the alien virus is reversed and society reverts to its violent ways. ==Cast== * [[Nicole Kidman]] as Dr. Carol Bennell * [[Daniel Craig]] as Dr. Ben Driscoll * [[Jeremy Northam]] as Tucker Kaufman * Jackson Bond as Oliver * [[Jeffrey Wright]] as Dr. Stephen Galeano * [[Veronica Cartwright]] as Wendy Lenk * [[Josef Sommer]] as Dr. Henryk Belicec * [[Celia Weston]] as Ludmilla Belicec * [[Roger Rees]] as Yorish Kagonavich * Eric Benjamin as Gene * [[Susan Floyd]] as Pam * Stephanie Berry as Carly * Alexis Raben as Jill * [[Adam LeFevre]] as Richard Lenk * [[Joanna Merlin]] as Joan Kaufman * [[Malin Åkerman]] as Autumn (uncredited) * [[Jeff Wincott]] as Transit Cop ==Production== ===Conception=== In March 2004, [[Warner Bros.]] hired screenwriter David Kajganich to write a script that would serve as a remake of the 1956 science fiction film ''[[Invasion of the Body Snatchers (1956 film)|Invasion of the Body Snatchers]]''.{{cite news |author=Cathy Dunkley |url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117902307.html?categoryid=13&cs=1 |title=Scribe warms to WB's 'Body' |work=Variety |date=March 25, 2004 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} In July 2005, director [[Oliver Hirschbiegel]] was attached to helm the project, with production to begin in [[Edgemere, Maryland]].{{cite news |url=http://www.comingsoon.net/news/topnews.php?id=10400 |title=''Body Snatchers'' Get a Director |publisher=''ComingSoon.net'' |date=July 15, 2005 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} The following August, Nicole Kidman was cast to star in the film then titled ''Invasion'', receiving a salary of close to $17 million. ''Invasion'' was based on the script by Kajganich, originally intended as a remake of ''Invasion of the Body Snatchers'', but Kajganich crafted a different enough story for the studio to see the project as an original conception.{{cite news |author= Fleming, Michael |author2= Brodesser, Claude |url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117926842.html?categoryid=1236&cs=1 |title=WB unearths 'Invasion' |work=Variety |date=August 1, 2005 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} Kajganich described the story to reflect contemporary times, saying, "You just have to look around our world today to see that power inspires nothing more than the desire to retain it and to eliminate anything that threatens it." The screenwriter said that the story was set in Washington, D.C. to reflect the theme.{{cite news |author=Cheong, Felix |url=http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/entertainment/view/299761/1/.html |title=Remaking the Remake |work=''[[Today (Singapore newspaper)|Today]]'' |date=September 14, 2007 |accessdate=October 5, 2007}} In August, Daniel Craig was cast opposite Kidman in the lead.{{cite news |author= McClintock, Pamela |url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117927747.html?categoryid=13&cs=1 |title=Craig plans for 'Invasion' |work=Variety |date=August 18, 2005 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} The film, whose original title ''Invasion of the Body Snatchers'' was shortened to ''Invasion'' due to Kajganich's different concept, was changed once more to ''The Visiting'' so it would not be confused with [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]'s TV series ''[[Invasion (U.S. TV series)|Invasion]]''.{{cite news |author= McClintock, Pamela |url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117930411.html?categoryid=1019&cs=1 |title='Invasion' title snatched |work=Variety |date=October 9, 2005 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} ===Filming=== Filming began on September 26, 2005 in Baltimore and lasted 45 days.{{cite news |author= Kaltenbech, Chris|url=http://www.baltimoresun.com/entertainment/movies/bal-to.kidman24sep24,0,5340977.story |title='Invasion,' downgraded to a 'Visiting,' will hit city |work=[[The Baltimore Sun]] |date=September 24, 2005 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} The film had minimal visual effects, with no need for [[greenscreen]] work. Instead, the director shot from odd camera angles and claustrophobic spaces to increase tension in the film.{{cite news |author=Wloszczyna, Susan|url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/movies/news/2006-01-11-visiting-main_x.htm |title=Paranoia gets revisited in 'The Visiting' |work=[[USA Today]] |date=January 11, 2006 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} In October 2006, ''The Visiting'' changed to the title of ''The Invasion'', due to the cancellation of the ABC TV series.{{cite news |author= McClintock, Pamela|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117951861.html?categoryid=1019&cs=1 |title=The 'Invasion' is back on again |work=Variety |date=October 15, 2006 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} The studio, however, was unhappy with Hirschbiegel's results and hired [[The Wachowskis]] to rewrite the film and assist with additional shooting. The studio later hired director [[James McTeigue]] to perform re-shoots that would cost $10 million,{{cite news |author=Goldstein, Patrick |title=Success and Failure Can Cross Hollywood Border |work=Los Angeles Times |date=March 7, 2007 |accessdate=August 7, 2007}} an uncredited duty by McTeigue.{{cite news |author=Fleming, Michael|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117967691.html?categoryId=2431&cs=1 |title=McTeigue to get Thai'd up in 'Bangkok' |work=Variety |date=June 26, 2007 |accessdate=July 7, 2007}} After 13 months of inactivity, re-shoots took place in January 2007 to increase action scenes and add a [[twist ending]].{{cite news |title=August 17 – The Invasion |work=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |date=May 4, 2007 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} The re-shoot lasted for 17 days in Los Angeles. During the re-shooting, Kidman was involved in an accident, while in a [[Jaguar (car)|Jaguar]] that was being towed by a stunt driver and was taken to a hospital briefly.{{cite news |author=Associated Press |url=http://www.comingsoon.net/news/movienews.php?id=18572 |title=Kidman in Crash on ''The Invasion'' Set |publisher=''ComingSoon.net'' |date=January 25, 2007 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} Kidman broke several ribs, but she was able to get back to work soon after being hospitalized.{{cite news |author=Lee, Patrick|url=http://www.scifi.com/scifiwire/index.php?id=43214 |title=Kidman Talks ''Invasion'' Injuries |publisher=[[Sci Fi Wire]] |date=August 14, 2007 |accessdate=August 14, 2007 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071011231444/http://scifi.com/scifiwire/index.php?id=43214 |archivedate=October 11, 2007}} ===Musical score=== In May 2007, composer [[John Ottman]] recorded the musical score for ''The Invasion'', using heavy synthesizers combined with a 77-piece orchestra intended to create "otherworldly foreboding and tension". The music was also designed to have an [[avant-garde]] [[postmodern music|postmodern]] style, with atmospheric and thrilling action elements.{{cite news |author=Dan Goldwasser |url=http://www.soundtrack.net/news/article/?id=992 |title=John Ottman scores ''The Invasion'' |publisher=SoundtrackNet |date=May 25, 2007 |accessdate=May 9, 2007}} ==Release== ''The Invasion'' was originally intended to be released in June 2006,{{cite news |author=Wloszczyna, Susan |url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/people/2005-11-17-kidman_x.htm |title=Kidman happily visits while filming 'Visiting' |work=[[USA Today]] |date=November 17, 2005 |accessdate=April 28, 2007}} but it was postponed to 2007. The film was released on August 17, 2007 in the United States and Canada in 2,776 theaters. The film grossed $5,951,409 over the opening weekend. ''The Invasion'' has grossed $15,074,191 in the United States and Canada and $24,727,542 in other territories for a worldwide gross of $40,170,558 {{as of|2008|3|9|lc=on}}. The music in the trailer is called "[[Untitled 8 (a.k.a. "Popplagið")]]" by [[Sigur Rós]]. ===Critical response=== The film received generally negative reviews. On review aggregator [[Metacritic]], ''The Invasion'' received an average score of 45 out of 100.{{cite web |url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/the-invasion |title=Invasion, The (2007): Reviews |publisher=''[[Metacritic]]'' |accessdate=October 27, 2011}} [[Roger Ebert]] of the ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]'' called it "the fourth, and the least, of the movies made from Jack Finney's classic science fiction novel."{{cite web|url=http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20070816/REVIEWS/708160304 |title=The Invasion|work=Chicago Sun-Times|date=August 17, 2007|author=[[Roger Ebert|Ebert, Roger]]|accessdate=May 3, 2018}} [[Owen Gleiberman]] of [[Entertainment Weekly]] wrote that it was "a soulless rehash...The movie isn't terrible;

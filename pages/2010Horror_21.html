{{Infobox film | name = Blood Junkie | image = Blood Junkie.jpg | alt = | caption = | director = [[Drew Rosas]] | producer = Drew Rosas | writer = Drew Rosas | starring = Nick Sommer, Mike Johnson, Sarah Luther, Emily Treolo, [[Andrew Swant]] | music = Lodewijk Broekhuizen, Evan Murphy, Drew Rosas, Michael Stasny, Ryan Olson | cinematography = Michael Kubaszak, Drew Rosas | editing = Drew Rosas | studio = Surgery Room | distributor = [[Troma Entertainment]] | released = {{Film date|2010|5|20}} | runtime = 72 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = $7000{{cite web|title=Blood Junkie (review)|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/158590/blood-junkie-v-2/|publisher=Bloody Disgusting|accessdate=24 December 2013}} | gross = }} '''''Blood Junkie''''' (originally filmed under the working title ''Rocky Trails'') is a 2010 American independent comedy horror movie that was written and directed by [[Drew Rosas]]. The film was released on May 20, 2010 in [[Milwaukee, Wisconsin]] and was later released on DVD through [[Troma Entertainment]] in 2011. The film stars Nick Sommer, Mike Johnson, Emily Treolo, and Sarah Luther, and follows a group of teenagers that go out into the woods to get high but end up getting killed one by one. ==Synopsis== Several teenagers go out into the woods in order to have fun, drink alcohol, and smoke [[marijuana|pot]]. While out there, Teddy (Mike Johnson) begins to tell the others stories about the old chemical plant. Years ago an explosion caused the closure of the plant and the body of the plant's night operator was never found. Teddy claims that he read in his grandfather's journal that there is a manbeast in the woods that would mutilate livestock. The group decides to travel to the old chemical plant to check it out, only to discover that there is something dangerous living there. ==Cast== *Nick Sommer as Craig Wilson *Mike Johnson as Teddy Bender *Sarah Luther as Laura *Emily Treolo as Rachel *[[Andrew Swant]] as The Creature *Ross Bachhuber as Jef Bolstead *Chris Chuzles as Hitchhiker *[[Bobby Ciraldo]] as Billy the Butt *Brady Cohen as Andy *Kelly Cunningham as Rachel's Boobs *Eric Gerber as Radio Reporter (voice) *Kelly Hendzel as Speed Walker *Carrie Hitchcock as Mrs. Bender *Dave Kiehl as Hank Sanders *Anne Killelea as Jenifer Gantly ==Production== Rosas has stated that he began working on ''Blood Junkie'' partially as "a retaliation against uber-stylized horror filmmaking and the unadulterated rebooting and mutilation of the classic 80s franchises" but also because it was "a stylistic choice based on the materials and film assets I had at my disposal".{{cite web|title=Drew Rosas: Financing a Dream|url=http://www.dvdverdict.com/interview_rosas|publisher=DVD Verdict|accessdate=24 December 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131224105519/http://www.dvdverdict.com/interview_rosas|archivedate=24 December 2013|df=}} He created half of the music in the film using a Korg polysynth keyboard and Rosas had several of his friends create the other portions of the movie's soundtrack. He had originally planned to make ''Blood Junkie'' as a short 30-40 minute film, but later decided to stretch the film into a feature-length film, adding more material to the film and allowing the actors to improvise on the set. ==Reception== Critical reception for ''Blood Junkie'' was generally positive. Common elements of praise centered around Rosas's attempt to make the film feel as if it was made in the 1980s, and ''HorrorNews.net'' commented that they "would not have known the difference" if they had not known the film's creation date beforehand.{{cite web|title=Film Review: Blood Junkie (2010)|url=http://horrornews.net/33895/film-review-blood-junkie-2010/|publisher=HorrorNews.net|accessdate=24 December 2013}}{{cite web|title=Review: Blood Junkie|url=http://www.dvdverdict.com/reviews/bloodjunkie.php|publisher=DVD Verdict|accessdate=24 December 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131224121742/http://www.dvdverdict.com/reviews/bloodjunkie.php|archivedate=24 December 2013|df=}} The reviewer for ''[[Ain't It Cool News]]'' marked the film as one of their favorite horror films released so far that year, saying that at first they "wondered if this was untalented folks making a bad film or if it was talented folks going out of their way to make a good film that looks like a bad film.

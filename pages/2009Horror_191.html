{{Infobox film | name = The Sky Has Fallen | image = | alt = | caption = | director = Doug Roos | producer = Doug Roos | writer = Doug Roos | starring = {{plainlist| * Carey MacLaren * Laurel Kemper * Cory Knisely * Nathan Shelton }} | music = James Sizemore | cinematography = Doug Roos | editing = Doug Roos | studio = Lost Forever Productions | distributor = | released = {{Film date|2009|5|7|ref1={{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_sky_has_fallen/|title=The Sky Has Fallen - Rotten Tomatoes|last=Buchanan|first=Jason|work=Rotten Tomatoes|date=2009-05-07|accessdate=2015-04-23}}}} | runtime = | country = United States | language = English | budget = $20,000 | gross = }} '''''The Sky Has Fallen''''' is a 2009 American horror film written and directed by Doug Roos. It stars Carey MacLaren and Laurel Kemper as two survivors of a gruesome apocalypse. == Plot == An [[Ebola virus disease|Ebola]]-like plague decimates the world's population. Attempting to escape infection, people flee to rural locations but strange demonic figures appear, transforming the dead into their army. The two main characters, Lance and Rachel, try to hunt down the leader of these creatures before humanity gets wiped out.{{cite web|url=http://fangorianews.blogspot.com/2010/02/sky-has-fallen-onto-dvd.html|title=THE SKY HAS FALLEN onto DVD|last=Gingold|first=Michael|work=FANGORIA Magazine Newsblog|date=2010-02-16|accessdate=2015-04-23}} == Cast == * Carey MacLaren as Lance * Laurel Kemper as Rachel * Cory Knisely as The Leader * Nathan Shelton as Chilton == Production == The film was shot in Missouri with a wide range of influences including [[Versus (2000 film)|''Versus'']] and [[The Exorcist (film)|''The Exorcist'']].{{cite web|url=http://www.quietearth.us/articles/2008/04/03/Trailer-and-images-for-Doug-Roos-post-apocalyptic-love-story-The-Sky-has-Fallen|title=Trailer and images for Doug Roos "post apocalyptic love story" The Sky Has Fallen|last=Webster|first=Christopher|work=Quiet Earth|date=2008-04-03|accessdate=2015-04-23}} The opening shots of a large city were actually done in Tokyo, Japan. The film is known for containing all practical effects without any CGI. == Release == The original cut of ''The Sky Has Fallen'' premiered at the British Film Festival Los Angeles.{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1316078/releaseinfo|title=The Sky Has Fallen 2009 - Release Info - IMDb|last=Editors|first=Data|work=IMDb|date=2009-05-07|accessdate=2015-04-23}} It was independently distributed on DVD on February 16, 2010. New footage was shot in 2011 and again in 2014, resulting in three very different versions of the film with the latest being an 80-minute Ultimate Edition that came out in June 2015. == Reception == Matt Boiselle of ''Dread Central'' rated it a 4/5, calling the film "a very large win for independent cinema in my opinion and isn’t a film that should be passed by."{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/reviews/107674/sky-has-fallen-the-2015/|title=Sky Has Fallen (2015) - Dread Central|last=Boiselle|first=Matt|work=Dread Central|date=2015-06-19|accessdate=2015-08-23}} ''HorrorNews.net'' gave it "7 zombies" out of 10, writing there is "some really creative stuff going on in this film."{{cite web|url=http://www.horrornews.net/100995/film-review-the-sky-has-fallen-2009/|title=Film Review: The Sky Has Fallen (2009)|last=Stormbringer|first=Rio|work=HorrorNews.net|date=2015-08-19|accessdate=2015-08-23}} Bella Blitz of ''The Horror Honeys'' was "pleasantly surprised" with a score of 3 out of 5, adding, "Seriously though, so much blood. It was awesome."

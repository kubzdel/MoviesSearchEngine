Daniel Newman | cinematography = Jayson Crothers | editing = Greg Robbins | distributor = Anchor Bay | released = {{Film date|2008|09|23}} | runtime = 92 minutes | country = United States | language = English | budget = $25,000 | gross = }} '''''Bryan Loves You''''' is a 2008 horror film directed by [[Seth Landau]]. The film relates the supposed true story of the Cult of the Bryans who took over a small Arizona town in 1993. It was shot in 17 days with a budget of $25,000.{{cite web|url=http://temple-news.com/arts/2006/10/31/beware-of-this-cult-classic/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2014-01-08 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140108192219/http://temple-news.com/arts/2006/10/31/beware-of-this-cult-classic/ |archivedate=2014-01-08 |df= }} ==Plot== The plot revolves around a 32-year-old psychotherapist as he begins to suspect that the local religious cult has been murdering the town's citizens. He soon becomes sucked into a web of mysterious happenings, with the cult of the 'Bryans' being the focal point. The film uses the 'found footage' format, and claims that the film is a compilation of security footage. ==Reception== Critical reception for the film has been mixed to negative,[http://emol.org/emclub/?q=bryanlovesyoudvd Bryan Loves You] Emol.org[http://www.dvdverdict.com/reviews/bryanlovesyou.php Bryan Loves You] DVD Verdict with [[Dread Central]] criticizing the film's lack of realism, saying "Watching “found footage” shouldn’t have the viewer asking questions like, “Why is everyone acting so overly evil?” "[http://www.dreadcentral.com/reviews/bryan-loves-you-dvd Bryan Loves You (DVD)] Dread Central About.com's Mark Harris negatively reviewed the film, stating that "Bryan is mostly made up of drawn-out moments of inconsequential activity loosely tied together by a scenario that never delivers."[http://horror.about.com/od/dvdhorrorreviews/gr/bryanlovesrev.htm Bryan Loves You DVD] About.com JoBlo.com reviewed the film positively, giving it three-and-half "Arrows in the Head", and writing that despite the film's resemblance to previous horror works such as Clockwork Orange, the movie "still feels fresh and inventive".[http://www.joblo.com/horror-movies/dvd-reviews/bryan-loves-you BRYAN LOVES YOU] JoBlo.com BlogCritics.org also reviewed the film, stating "spotty acting" and "a clumsy action sequence" as two of the film's weak spots but praising the Landau's versatility.[http://blogcritics.org/video/article/movie-review-bryan-loves-you/page-2/ Movie Review: Bryan Loves You ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110319081024/http://blogcritics.org/video/article/movie-review-bryan-loves-you/page-2/ |date=March 19, 2011 }} BlogCritics.org MovieWeb called the film "a masterfully creepy ride through religious fundamentalism gone horribly awry".[http://www.movieweb.com/news/reel-advice-bryan-loves-you REEL ADVICE: Bryan Loves You] MovieWeb The film has gained a sizable cult following since its release.

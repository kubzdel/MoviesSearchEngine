Joe Speirs | studio = [[Mandragora Productions]] | distributor = | released = {{Film date|df=yes|2009}} | runtime = 76 minutes | country = United Kingdom | language = English | budget = | gross = }} '''''Dark Nature''''' is a 2009 [[United Kingdom|British]] [[thriller film]] written by Eddie Harrison, directed by Marc de Launay, and starring Niall Greig Fulton, Vanya Eadie and [[Imogen Toner]]. Shot on a small budget, it was filmed in the Scottish Highlands from September to October 2008 before receiving a limited cinematic showing in 2009. The film went on to be released on [[DVD]] and [[Blu-ray Disc|Blu-ray]] in the [[United Kingdom]] and the [[United States]] in May 2010. == Plot == Jane and her family travel to Scotland to spend time with Jane's mother. Unbeknownst to Jane and her family, their relatives have been murdered by a sinister force, and that dark and unnatural something remains, waiting for them. Despite her best efforts, the already established tensions with her daughter Chloe only manage to escalate, while her son and her lover have troubles of their own. == Cast == * Vanya Eadie as Jane * [[Imogen Toner]] as Chloe * Niall Greig Fulton as McKenzie * James Bryce as Jonathan * Len McCaffer as Alex * Joanna Miller as Emily * Jane Stabler as Magda * Tom Carter as Hayward * Callum Warren-Brooker as Sean * Dorren McGillivray as Mrs Petrie == Reception == ''Dark Nature'' received negative reviews from critics. Joseph Howell, reviewing the film for the Scottish newspaper [[Daily Record (Scotland)|''Daily Record'']] said the cast "look as though they've come straight from the Milngavie amateur dramatics society," but that the film was so bad it had its own charm. He commented "The movie's writer is the film critic Eddie Harrison and I could be cruel and say stick to the day job. But I have complete admiration for him and director Marc de Launay for getting it off the ground and creating a film that has 'cult following' written all over it."{{cite web|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/life/reviews/film-reviews/2009/10/30/dark-nature-15-86908-21784437/|title=''Dark Nature *** 15''|work=[[Daily Record (Scotland)|The Daily Record]]|accessdate=4 January 2011}} Film website Talk of Horrors said "it's never that scary or intriguing", and that "all of the characters are extremely annoying," giving it a 2.5 out of 10 score.{{cite web|url=http://talkofhorrors.blogspot.com/2010/07/dark-nature-2009.html|title=''Dark Nature (2009)''|work=Talk of Horrors|accessdate=4 January 2011}} Website Mad Mad Movies said the film has "pretty atrocious CG gore."{{cite web|url=http://mmmmmovies.blogspot.com/2010/05/dvd-reviews-dark-nature-2009-and-pep.html|title=''DVD Reviews: DARK NATURE (2009) and PEP SQUAD (1998)''|work=Mad Mad Mad Mad Movies|accessdate=4 January 2011}} Contrastingly, Detroit Burns of film review website Sound on Sight applauded the film, stating "It's strong point is patience and a commitment to build up. The use of the rich Scottish landscape and maniacal score create a stark and tense atmosphere, an atmosphere that the film creates very well." He did, however, criticize the film's pace and general stillness, stating that ''Dark Nature''{{'}}s "weakness is in the film's hesitance to go one-step further" and that "it's death scenes are rather anti-climatic and serve no purpose other than the elimination of a character from the rest of the script".{{cite web|url=http://www.soundonsight.org/troma-presents-dark-nature/|title=''Troma Presents: Dark Nature''|work=Sound on Sight|date= 7 August 2010|accessdate=4 January 2011}} Light entertainment blog ''[[Pop Matters]]'' called the film "pristine" and "practically popping off the screen in its shot of digital dynamic", but attacked director Marc de Launay for "trying to redefine the category, taking much of the mystery out of your standard slice and dice".{{cite web|url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/post/126557-a-bunch-of-blu-2/|title=''Dark Nature''|work=[[PopMatters]]|accessdate=4 January 2011}} Horror fansite Horrortalk applauded the film as a "quiet and deliberately paced look at the balance between man and nature" and considered the film to echo "the more existential cinema of the 1970s" and that it focuses on "internal struggle while adding horror elements". Horrortalk did, however, admit that the results of such elements were mixed.{{cite web|url=http://www.horrortalk.com/reviews/864-dark-nature.html|title=''Dark Nature''|work=Horror Talk|accessdate=4 January 2011}} Gordon Sullivan of ''[[DVD Verdict]]'' moderately approved the film. Further to applauding its cinematography, Sullivan congratulated Marc de Launay as building a "dread-soaked atmosphere". He stated "The family relationship is the best part of the film by far", claiming "they display some very good chemistry". While considering the film to be itself confused as to "whether it wants to be a slice-and-dice thriller or an eco-horror film", Sullivan nonetheless considered that de Launay "shows himself to be a skilled young filmmaker with a good sense of humor and a knack for atmosphere". He went on to say "There aren't a lot of jokes in the film, but I caught myself laughing at a few of the darker situations. The director balances the humor nicely with the dread, which comes through strongly in the foggy shores of the Scottish landscape".{{cite web|url=http://www.dvdverdict.com/reviews/darknature.php|title=''Dark Nature''|work=[[DVD Verdict]] Review|accessdate=4 January 2011}} == References == {{reflist}} == External links == * {{Official website|http://www.darknature.net/}} * {{IMDb title|1285216|Dark Nature}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Dark Nature}} [[Category:2009 films]] [[Category:British films]] [[Category:English-language films]] [[Category:British horror films]] [[Category:British slasher films]] [[Category:Films shot in Highland (council area)]] [[Category:2009 horror films]] [[Category:British independent films]] [[Category:2000s thriller films]] [[Category:Films shot in Dumfries and Galloway]]

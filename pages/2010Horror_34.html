''Cold Fish'' premiered at the [[67th Venice International Film Festival]] on September 7, 2010, and received the best screenplay award in the Fantastic Features section at [[Fantastic Fest]] 2010. The film was released as part of the [[Bloody Disgusting|Bloody Disgusting Selects]] line. ==Premise== The film concerns quiet and unambitious owner of a tropical fish shop whose life and family are taken over by a fellow fish entrepreneur who happens to be a serial killer. The film is loosely based on the exploits of two Tokyo serial killers, [[Sekine Gen and Hiroko Kazama]], a husband and wife duo who owned a pet shop and murdered at least four people.{{cite web|url=http://twitchfilm.com/interviews/2011/01/screenwriter-yoshiki-takahashi-talks-cold-fish.php|work=Twitch|title= Screenwriter Yoshiki Takahashi Talks COLD FISH|author=Todd Brown|date=January 20, 2011|accessdate=August 25, 2012}} ==Cast== * [[Mitsuru Fukikoshi]] - Nobuyuki Syamoto * [[Denden]] - Yukio Murata * [[Asuka Kurosawa]] - Aiko Murata * [[Megumi Kagurazaka]] - Taeko Syamoto * Hikari Kajiwara - Mitsuko Syamoto * [[Tetsu Watanabe]] - Takayasu TsuTsui ==Production== Following ''[[Alien vs Ninja]]'' and ''[[Mutant Girls Squad]]'', ''Cold Fish'' is the third film to be released by [[Nikkatsu]]'s [[Sushi Typhoon]], their gore-themed series. Director and writer [[Sion Sono]] was influenced by Japanese crime cases while developing ''Cold Fish'', specifically about an actual killing spree committed by a dog kennel owner in the 1980s (the story of the film involves a family of three that becomes entangled in a string of ongoing murders perpetrated by a tropical fish salesman in [[Shizuoka Prefecture]]). Sono also wanted to "depict a sense of total hopelessness" which he felt is "lacking in Japanese films."{{cite web|url=http://www.tokyoreporter.com/2010/11/28/sion-sonos-cold-fish-nets-japan-premiere-at-tokyo-filmex/|work=The Tokyo Reporter|author=CJ|title=Sion Sono’s ‘Cold Fish’ nets Japan premiere at Tokyo Filmex|date=November 28, 2010|accessdate=July 11, 2011}} ==Release== ''Cold Fish'' premiered at the [[67th Venice International Film Festival]] on September 7, 2010.{{cite web|url=http://www.filmbiz.asia/reviews/cold-fish|work=Film Business Asia|title=Cold Fish (冷たい熱帯魚)|accessdate=July 11, 2011|date=September 10, 2010|last=Elley|first=Derek}} It was also shown at film festivals in [[Pusan]] and at the [[Toronto International Film Festival]] where it received its North American premiere.{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/notorious-japanese-director-sion-sono-42963|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|title=Notorious Japanese Director Sion Sono Unveils Next Project|accessdate=July 11, 2011|date=November 8, 2010|last=Landreth|first=Jonathan}} {{subscription required}}{{cite web|url=http://www.allrovi.com/movies/movie/cold-fish-v525140|work=[[Allrovi]]|title=Cold Fish|last=Deming|first=Mark|accessdate=July 11, 2011|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111129145039/http://www.allrovi.com/movies/movie/cold-fish-v525140|archivedate=November 29, 2011|df=}} ''Cold Fish'' won the best screenplay award in the Fantastic Features section at [[Fantastic Fest]] 2010. It was released in Japan on January 29, 2011. ==Reception== Film Business Asia gave ''Cold Fish'' an 8 out of 10 rating praising the actor Denden who without his "tour-de-force performance...''Cold Fish'' may never have worked." The review went on to state that "Though there's considerable gore on display, it's largely cartoonish. ''Cold Fish'' is not so much a blood-and-guts horror movie, more a danse macabre about social breakdown." In the United Kingdom, ''[[Total Film]]'' gave the film a three out of five rating, suggesting that plot twists and black comedy offered welcome reprieve from the "largely hysterical acting and rivers of viscera."{{cite web |url= http://www.totalfilm.com/reviews/cinema/cold-fish?ns_campaign=reviews&ns_mchannel=rss&ns_source=totalfilm&ns_linkname=0&ns_fee=0 |work=[[Total Film]] |accessdate= July 11, 2011 |date=April 13, 2011 |title= Cold Fish |last= Harley |first=Kevin}} ''[[The Guardian]]'' found the film to be "fairly ordinary" in comparison to Sono's other works and felt that the film was too long.{{cite web |url= https://www.theguardian.com/film/2011/apr/10/cold-fish-review-shion-sono?INTCMP=SRCH |work= [[The Guardian]] |title= Cold Fish – review |last= French |first= Philip |accessdate= July 11, 2011 |date= April 10, 2011}} ''[[Radio Times]]'' gave the film three out of five stars, praising the acting from Denden, Fukikoshi and Kurosawa and Shinya Kimura's photography and Takashi Matsuzuka's production design which made up for "some overindulgent directorial moments".{{cite web |url= http://www.radiotimes.com/servlet_film/com.icl.beeb.rtfilms.client.simpleSearchServlet?searchTypeSelect=5&frn=50703 |work= [[Radio Times]] |title= Cold Fish |accessdate= July 11, 2011 |author= Parkinson, David}} ==Notes== {{reflist}} ==External links== * {{Official website |http://www.coldfish.jp/index.html}} {{ja icon}} * {{IMDb title|1632547|Cold Fish}} * {{rotten-tomatoes|cold_fish_2011|Cold Fish}} {{Sion Sono}} {{Blue Ribbon Award for Best Film}} [[Category:2010 films]] [[Category:2010 horror films]] [[Category:Japanese films]] [[Category:Films based on actual events]] [[Category:Japanese horror films]] [[Category:Japanese thriller films]] [[Category:2010s thriller films]] [[Category:Nikkatsu films]] [[Category:Serial killer films]] [[Category:Films directed by Sion Sono]]

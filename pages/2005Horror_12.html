English | budget = }} '''''Beneath Still Waters''''' is a 2005 [[horror film]] directed by [[Brian Yuzna]]. It stars [[Michael McKell]], Raquel Meroño and [[Charlotte Salt]]. It was based on a novel by [[Matthew Costello]]. ==Plot== Studying under a disciple of [[Aleister Crowley]], the leader of an upper class group invokes a supernatural force that slowly devours the village of Marienbad and its inhabitants, threatening to spread beyond its geographical limits. The mayor from the town nearby commissions the building of a dam which would flood the valley and therefore submerge the village forever sealing the evil force under water after leader and his followers were incapacitated to be kept from escaping. However, fate ensured the leader's freedom as he remained in the depths when the waters covered Marienbad. Now 40 years later an array of disappearances and deaths in mysterious circumstances are threatening the town next to the reservoir that now covers Marienbad. ==Cast== * [[Michael McKell]] as Dan Quarry * Raquel Meroño as Teresa Borgia * [[Charlotte Salt]] as Clara Borgia * Patrick Gordon as Mordecai Salas * [[Axelle Carolyn]] as Partygoer ==Production== It was produced in [[Catalonia]]. The film is the ninth and last film to be produced by Filmax's ''[[Fantastic Factory]]'' label.[http://www.labutaca.net/films/31/beneathstillwaters.htm LA BUTACA - Bajo aguas tranquilas (Beneath still waters)] It is based on the novel ''Beneath Still Waters'' by [[Matthew J. Costello]].[http://www.popcornpictures.co.uk/bfilms/stillwaters.shtml Popcorn Pictures Review] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080819225504/http://www.popcornpictures.co.uk/bfilms/stillwaters.shtml |date=19 August 2008 }} ==Release== The film premiered on 24 October 2005 as part of the San Sebastián Horror and Fantasy Film Festival,[http://www.lahiguera.net/cinemania/pelicula/1745/ Bajo aguas tranquilas (Beneath Still Waters)] followed with Spain theatrical release on 26 May 2006.{{fact|date=February 2015}} It was released on DVD in the US on 9 April 2007.{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/1733/dvd-release-list-fear-the-ball/|title=DVD Release List: Fear the Ball|last=Condit|first=Jon|work=[[Dread Central]]|date=9 April 2007|accessdate=26 February 2015}} ==Reception== Steve Barton of [[Dread Central]] rated it 1/5 stars and called it a "poorly assembled, laughably bad, absolute waste of time".{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/reviews/4685/beneath-still-waters-dvd/|title=Beneath Still Waters (DVD)|last=Barton|first=Steve|work=[[Dread Central]]|date=6 April 2007|accessdate=26 February 2015}} Annie Riordan of Brutal as Hell rated it 1/5 stars and wrote that the film does not live up to the potentially interesting premise.{{cite web|url=http://www.brutalashell.com/2009/04/dvd-review-beneath-still-waters/|title=Beneath Still Waters (2005)|last=Riordan|first=Annie|work=Brutal as Hell|date=6 April 2009|accessdate=26 February 2015}} David Johnson of [[DVD Verdict]] wrote that it has good scenes but criticized the overreliance on poor CGI and tedious character development on unlikeable characters.{{cite web|url=http://www.dvdverdict.com/reviews/beneathstillwaters.php|title=Beneath Still Waters|last=Johnson|first=David|work=[[DVD Verdict]]|date=20 April 2007|accessdate=26 February 2007}} At [[DVD Talk]], Scott Weinberg rated it 2/5 stars, and Paul Mavis rated it 1.5/5 stars;
